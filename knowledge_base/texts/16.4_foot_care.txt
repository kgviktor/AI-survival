CHAPTER 16. PERSONAL HYGIENE IN THE FIELD
16.4 Foot care on long hikes

WHY FEET COME FIRST

In survival, **feet are transport.** Hot spots become blisters; blisters become infected wounds; infected feet **stop walking.** Old rule: **protect feet like a weapon** — a soldier who cannot march is combat-ineffective.

**Main threats:**
1. Friction / blisters  
2. Fungal infection (moisture + warmth)  
3. Cold injury (damp + frost)  


BLISTER PREVENTION

**Socks:**
— **Two layers** often help: thin **synthetic** liner (wicking) + **wool** outer (cushion).  
— **No heel/toe seams** against skin — or seam **outside.**  
— **No wrinkles** — smooth before lacing.  
— **Change** daily or every **~8–12 h** on hard marches.

**Boots:**
— **Broken in** before hard use — new boots → predictable blisters.  
— Snug heel, **toe wiggle room** — toes should not jam downhill.  
— **Lace:** firm at ankle, slightly looser over forefoot.  
— **Wet boots** — dry at first chance, **inside** too.

**Lubricant:** petroleum jelly, animal fat, or oil on **heel, little toe, big toe, between toes** before long walks.


EARLY WARNING

**Burning or “hot spot”** in one spot — **stop now** — blister forms fast (**~30 min**).

**Action:**
— Boot/sock off; inspect.  
— If red: **tape / moleskin** several layers.  
— No tape: cloth pad, bandage, **moss** pad secured with wrap.  
— Fix cause: smooth sock, relace.


BLISTERS

**Intact blister** — often **do not** puncture — roof protects.  
**Painful / must walk:** sterile needle at **edge**, drain, **leave roof**, cover sterile + tape.

**Ruptured:** clean with antiseptic; sterile dressing; tape; watch for **redness, heat, pus** = infection.


END-OF-DAY ROUTINE

1. Boots and socks **off** at rest.  
2. Inspect: hot spots, cracks, color.  
3. Wash or wipe feet — **especially between toes.**  
4. **Dry thoroughly** — including web spaces.  
5. **Air feet ~20–30 min** if conditions allow.  
6. **Clean dry socks** if available.

**Foot/calf massage ~5–10 min** — circulation, cramp prevention.


FUNGUS (“ATHLETE’S FOOT”)

**Signs:** itch/burn between toes, scaling, white film, odor.

**Care:** antifungal cream if you have it (e.g. clotrimazole, terbinafine). **Without meds:** **keep feet dry** — open air, sock changes, dry boots. Dilute **vinegar 1:1** on lesions — acidic. **Strong salt soak** — some relief. **Wood ash** dust in socks/shoes — drying, alkaline.

**Prevention:** dry feet; rotate socks; dry shoes; **talc/starch/ash** to absorb moisture.

**Nails:** trim short; **thick yellow crumbling nail** — fungal — slow to fix — hygiene + meds when possible.


COLD AND WET

**Numb toes while moving:** wiggle toes **~every 20–30 min**; stop, warm/rub if numbness persists — precursor to frostbite.

**Soaked feet in freezing weather:** **critical** — stop when safe; remove wet socks; dry feet; **warm socks on body** (e.g. inside jacket); wrap in dry cloth while socks dry.

**Spare socks:** one **dry pair** in a **waterproof bag** — **emergency only** — do not “borrow” from that pair casually.


CALLOUSES AND CRACKS

**Thick painless callus** on experienced hikers — usually **leave it** — natural protection.  
**White soggy skin** (maceration) — too wet — **dry out**, protect.

**Heel fissures:** grease (vaseline, fat, oil); antiseptic if deep — infection portal.


CRAMPS

Night calf cramps — often **dehydration** / electrolyte imbalance. **Hydrate**; stretch calves before sleep. During cramp: **pull toes toward shin.**
