CHAPTER 16. PERSONAL HYGIENE IN THE FIELD
16.6 Lice, fleas, scabies

PARASITES ARE A REAL THREAT

Crowding, little hot water, rare clothing change → **lice**, **fleas**, **scabies mite** spread fast. Historically, lice were tied to **epidemic typhus**; fleas to **plague** (with rodent reservoirs). Today they still mean **misery**, **infection** from scratching, and **stealth loss** (noise, smell, movement).


LICE

**Three human types:**
— **Head** (*Pediculus humanus capitis*) — scalp hair  
— **Body/clothing** (*Pediculus humanus corporis*) — seams of clothing; feeds on skin  
— **Pubic** (*Phthirus pubis*) — coarse body hair  

**Signs:** intense itch in typical areas; scratch marks; **~2–3 mm** insects; **nits** (eggs) glued to hair shafts.

**Head lice:** spread by **direct head contact** or shared combs/hats — **not** flying/jumping.

**Body lice:** live in clothing; linked to **epidemic (louse-borne) typhus** — serious where it still occurs. **Boiling** clothes is key.

**Typhus-type illness (historical / outbreak context):** high fever, severe headache, rash sparing face/palms **~days after** — **needs medical care when available.**


FIGHTING LICE

**Nit comb:** very fine teeth; damp hair; comb **root to tip** daily **~2 weeks** (life cycle). Removes adults; not all nits.

**Boil / hot wash:** **~100 °C** **~30 min** on washable items kills lice and eggs — **most reliable** non-chemical for fabric.

**Heat without water:** hot **iron** on seams; or hold clothing **near** (not in) fire **~10–15 min** per side — **fire risk.**

**Freeze:** about **−20 °C** **~24 h** can kill on fabrics — winter option.

**Oil / petroleum jelly:** heavy coating on hair **~8–12 h** under cap — smothers many adults; **nits** harder — comb after.

**Kerosene (folk method):** effective but **highly flammable**, irritates skin, toxic fumes — **keep from fire**; **short** contact; thorough wash; **avoid children** and broken skin.

**OTC / pharmacy:** **permethrin** products; **malathion** lotions where sold; **dimethicone** (physical smother) — **follow label.**

**Nits:** vinegar **~9%** may loosen glue — apply, wrap **~30–60 min**, then comb.

**Reinfestation:** treat **hats, bedding, combs** — or lice return in days.


FLEAS

Live in **floor cracks, bedding**; **jump** on host to feed; linked to **plague** and other diseases via **rodents**.

**Signs:** bites **ankles/legs**; intense itch; tiny fast insects seen.

**Control:** boil bedding; **freeze** mattresses if possible; **salt** in cracks dehydrates some stages; **clean clutter**; **insecticide spray** if available and safe to use. **Repellent plants** (wormwood, etc.) — weak; not a cure.

**Pets:** major carriers — treat animals if you have them and supplies.


SCABIES

**Cause:** mite *Sarcoptes scabiei* — prolonged **skin-to-skin** contact.

**Signs:** **worse itch at night**; **burrows** (fine lines **~5–15 mm**); web spaces of hands, wrists, waist, groin, axillae; rash from scratching.

**Treatment (when available):** **33% sulfur ointment** nightly **~5–7 days** (messy, smells); **benzyl benzoate** preparations per local pharmacy directions; **permethrin cream** where prescribed.

**Without prescription meds:** hot soak + thorough soap scrub removes **some** surface mites — **does not** clear burrows — still treat contacts and **boil/freeze** bedding/clothes.

**Itch** can last **weeks** after mites are gone — antihistamines if available; soothing lotion.

**Simultaneously** treat **clothing/bedding** — or reinfestation in days.


TICKS (IXODID)

**Not** lice — but common field hazard. Can carry **Lyme disease**, **TBE**, others — region-dependent.

**After woods:** full **skin check** — hairline, ears, armpits, groin, backs of knees — **~2–3 h** after exposure ticks may still be crawling.

**Removal:** fine tweezers or thread loop — grasp **close to skin**, pull **straight out** steady — **no** twisting, crushing, hot match, or oil folk tricks (increases pathogen injection risk).

**After:** antiseptic on bite; **watch site ~30 days** — **expanding “bull’s-eye” rash** or flu-like illness after tick in Lyme areas → **medical care / antibiotics when available.**

**Prevention:** tuck pants; light clothing to see ticks; **repellents**; **permethrin-treated** clothing where used safely per label.
