CHAPTER 8. ENERGY AND ELECTRONICS
8.9 Basic electronics: multimeter, simple repair

MULTIMETER

Measures voltage, current, resistance — core diagnostic tool.

Digital (DMM): common, easy.
Analog: sometimes passive on AC ranges.

DCV: red +, black −; start high range; battery and 12 V bus checks.

ACV: mains/generator — **lethal voltages** — probes and training matter.

DCA: **series** in circuit; use 10 A jack if needed; wrong range blows internal fuse often.

Ohms: power off in circuit; continuity beeps low Ω.

Diode mode: ~0.4–0.7 V forward on silicon.


COMMON FAULTS

Won’t charge battery:
— Battery V <10 V may be dead; charger open-circuit V should exceed battery; check fuses; continuity harness.

Load dead:
— V at load under load? If good → load fault. If zero → fuse/wire.

Hot wire:
— Measure I — overload or partial short.

Alternator not charging (vehicle):
— System V under load vs battery alone.


SOLDERING

25–40 W fine work; 40–60 W terminals.
Tin iron; flux; shiny fillet joint; shrink tube or tape after.

Cold joint: dull, grainy — reheat with flux.

Emergency: tin strands over candle + rosin; or crimp lugs.


BASIC PARTS

Resistor: limits current — LED example R ≈ (Vsupply − Vf)/If → e.g. (12−3.3)/0.02 ≈ 435 Ω → use 470 Ω standard.

Capacitor: bulk DC filter — respect polarity on electrolytics.

Diode: 1N4007 ~1 A; 1N5408 ~3 A; blocking + rectifier.

LED: always current limited.

MOSFET: switch heavy DC loads (e.g. IRFZ44N class).

Relay: small signal switches big load.

DC-DC modules: buck 12→5 V USB common.


COMMON FIXES

Blown fuse: replace same type; if instant pop → find short.

SMPS charger: swollen primary cap — replace match V rating + higher temp grade; inspect charred resistors.

USB cable: red +5 V, black GND — data pair optional for charge-only repair.

Corroded battery terminal: sand clean; tighten; protect with grease.


MINIMUM TOOL KIT

DMM; iron + solder + flux; tweezers; cutters; stripper; tape + heat-shrink; screwdrivers; small files.
