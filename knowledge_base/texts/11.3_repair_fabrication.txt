CHAPTER 11. TOOLS AND REPAIR
11.3 Repairing and making household items

REPAIR MINDSET

Normal life: trash it. Crisis: fix, repurpose, harvest parts.

Three moves:
1. Restore original function.
2. Adapt to a new job.
3. Strip for components.


POTS AND CONTAINERS

Pinhole in metal pot:
— **Food-safe:** tin solder with iron + flux (lead-free solder for food contact).
— Large hole: same-metal patch, solder or folded seam + peen.
— No solder: bread dough + fat plug — **one cook only** emergency.

Plastic crack:
— Heat-weld edges with careful hot knife/nail.
— Staples across crack (stress relief holes at ends).
— 2-part epoxy — clean, dry surfaces essential.

Leaky bucket:
— Rubber washer + bolt sandwich through wall.
— Bitumen / pine pitch on outside.

Broken jar neck:
— Sand sharp lip with stone/file.
— Use open-top or cloth + rubber band lid.


FURNITURE AND STRUCTURE

Broken chair/table leg:
— Screws + corner brackets; wooden dowel + PVA; rope wrap while glue cures.

Wobbly chair:
— Disassemble, scrape old glue, fresh PVA, clamp/rope ~8–12 h.

Door binds (twist):
— Chalk rub on strike edge — close — marks high spots; plane or sand.
— Rehang hinges if frame moved.

Defeated lock:
— Bolt bar + hasp; internal diagonal brace (see shelter hardening chapters).

Roof leak:
— Bitumen felt / plastic sheet overlap, batten down.
— Bitumen mastic in cracks.
— Foam gap-fill temporary only.
— Liquid rubber / elastomeric paint brush coat.

Broken window:
— Temp: poly sheet + duct tape + battens.
— Replace: score glass cutter, tap underside, break clean; glaze with putty or narrow strips + pins.


MAKING ESSENTIALS

Shelf:
— Salvage boards; metal brackets or folded strap; anchors for wall type; check level.

Bed / bunk:
— Two crates or stumps + boards; or four posts + rails + deck; or hammock fabric between anchors.

Table:
— Door slab on sawhorses; or frame + plywood top.

Stool:
— Four legs + seat + diagonal braces; or smoothed stump.

Water container:
— Heavy poly liner in wooden box.
— Nested buckets with gap sealed.
— Tarp-lined pit — temporary pool.

Brick rocket / stove sketch:
— Dry-stack U or L; grate on top; can-stove flue possible — detail in fire/cook chapters (`4.4` if present).

Wash station:
— Tank + threaded plastic tap; or hanging can with plug cord.

Water filter (rough):
— Gravel → coarse sand → fine sand → charcoal — bottle neck down.
— **Still boil or disinfect** after — see water treatment chapter (`2.3`).


METAL WITHOUT A WELDER

Rivet / peen:
— Drill both layers; nail, bolt, or rivet; peen tail on anvil or heavy steel.

Bend sheet:
— Clamp edge; bend over wood block with hammer to avoid scarring.

Cut:
— Hacksaw; cold chisel for thick stock; angle grinder if powered.

Wire wraps:
— Double twist with pliers for tight mechanical joints.


ADHESIVES IN PRACTICE

2-part epoxy:
— Metal, wood, glass, many plastics; mix 1:1 typical; full cure ~24–48 h.

Contact cement:
— Rubber, leather, fabric — coat both sides, open time ~10–15 min, marry.

Cyanoacrylate:
— Fast small rigid bonds; weak on flex; not structural.

PVA:
— Wood, paper, cardboard; clamp while drying.

Hot-melt glue:
— Quick tack; heat- and moisture-weak — temporary/light duty.

Silicone sealant:
— Flexible waterproof joints; full cure ~24–48 h before stress.
