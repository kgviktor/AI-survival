CHAPTER 16. PERSONAL HYGIENE IN THE FIELD
16.1 Washing the body with little water: conservation techniques

WHY HYGIENE STILL MATTERS IN A CRISIS

Dirty skin breeds infection. Highest risk: **groin**, **armpits**, **between toes** — warm, damp, bacterial. Fungal and bacterial skin problems without medical care can become serious. **One thorough wash per week** often beats daily half-measures.


HOW MUCH WATER

**Full shower:** ~**30–60 L** (typical home use).
**Effective “bird bath”:** ~**0.5–2 L**.
**Minimal wipe-down:** ~**0.2–0.5 L**.

When water is scarce: **prioritize body zones**, not volume.


THE “BIRD BATH” METHOD

Most efficient minimal-water wash — used by militaries, climbers, travelers.

You need:
— **0.5–1 L** warm water (if you can heat it)
— Small bowl or pot lid
— Cloth or sponge (or hands only)
— Soap or **wood ash paste**

**Order (important):**
1. Face and neck  
2. Hands to elbows  
3. Armpits  
4. Groin and genital area  
5. Legs and feet  

**Separate cloth for groin and face** — strict rule — or **rinse cloth** between zones.

**Save water:** wet cloth → wipe zone → **squeeze rinse water into bowl** — do not pour water over the body. Work folds and creases. Final rinse uses what is left in the bowl.

**Temperature:** **~35–38 °C** removes grease better than cold; more comfortable; less chill risk when washing outdoors. Heating water is worth the time.


WASHING WITHOUT MUCH WATER

**Wet wipes:** whole-body wipe; **alcohol wipes** add antisepsis (avoid sensitive mucosa).

**Ash:** wood ash contains alkali (potash). Mix with a little water → **alkaline paste** — cleans like crude soap. Rub; rinse.

**Sand:** clean river sand as abrasive — removes grease and dead skin; follow with cloth wipe.

**Snow:** rub briefly — refreshes and scours. **Avoid** if hypothermia risk, exhaustion, or frost injury. Work fast: melt → dry → dress.


FREQUENCY BY ZONE

**Daily (~0.1–0.2 L total):**
— Hands (often)  
— Face  
— Groin  

**Every other day (~0.3–0.5 L):**
— Armpits  
— Feet and legs  

**Every 3–5 days (~0.5–1 L):**
— Full bird bath  

**Every 1–2 weeks (2–5 L if available):**
— More complete wash  
— Hair wash (**16.5**)


WHERE TO WASH

**Shelter:** dedicated spot; plastic/tarp underfoot; catch greywater — do not flood living space.

**Outdoors:** **≥30 m** from streams/lakes you use for **drinking** — soap contaminates water. Screen from wind in cold. **Fast:** wet → soap → rinse → dress.


PSYCHOLOGY

Washing is not only hygiene — it is a **morale ritual.** Feeling clean helps mental state. In a long crisis, keep some washing routine; balance water savings with **human needs.**
