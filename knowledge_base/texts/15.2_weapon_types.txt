CHAPTER 15. FIREARMS: HANDLING BASICS
15.2 Weapon types: pistols, shotguns, carbines, rifles

HOW GUNS DIFFER

By:
— Barrel length (pistol vs carbine vs precision rifle).
— Ammunition (pistol cartridge vs rifle vs shotshell).
— Cycling (manual, semi-automatic, selective fire where legally present).
— Operating principle (pump, gas, recoil, bolt, etc.).

Knowing types helps you **identify** a found gun, **handle** it correctly, and judge **capability and limits.**


PISTOLS

Traits:
— Short barrel (~**7–15 cm** typical).
— Pistol cartridges: **9 mm**, **.45 ACP**, **.40 S&W**, etc.
— One- or two-hand grip.
— Practical effective range often **~10–50 m** for most users (training-dependent).

**Semi-automatic (self-loading):**
— Magazine in grip.
— Each shot: slide cycles, ejects case, loads next round.
— Magazine capacity often **~10–17** (varies widely by model and law).
— Examples: Glock 17/19, Beretta 92, Sig P226, many military/police sidearms.

**Makarov-class (9×18 mm) pistols:**
— Still common in Eastern Europe and former Soviet-influenced regions.
— Typical magazine **~8** rounds; simple, robust layout.
— **Safety:** many variants use a **slide-mounted lever** — learn the specific model (up/down = fire/safe varies — **verify before handling**).

**Revolver:**
— **5–8** rounds in a rotating cylinder; no detachable magazine.
— Often very reliable feeding; slower to reload.
— Single-action (cock hammer) vs double-action (trigger cocks and releases).


SHOTGUNS (SMOOTHBORE)

Traits:
— **Smooth** bore (no rifling) for shot; some loads use rifled slugs.
— Shells with shot, buckshot, or slug.
— Shot pattern: often **~20–40 m** “effective” depends on choke/load; slugs farther (**~100–150 m** possible with skill).
— Very high **stopping effect** at close range.

**Gauges:** **12** most common; **16**, **20**, **.410** less so.

**Pump-action:**
— Handguard cycles action; one round per stroke.
— Simple, reliable; tube magazine often **~3–8** rounds (law and plug-dependent).
— Examples: Mossberg 500/590, Remington 870; many regional clones.

**Semi-auto shotguns:**
— Gas or inertia operation.
— Examples: Benelli M4, Beretta 1301 class.

**Break-action (side-by-side / over-under):**
— Two barrels; two shots, then manual reload.
— Very simple mechanics; common hunting guns worldwide.

**Shell contents:**
— **Shot** — many small pellets; birds/small game.
— **Buckshot** — large pellets — defense/large game (check local law).
— **Slug** — single projectile; Brenneke/Foster-style common in smoothbores.


RIFLES AND CARBINES

**Rifled** bore spins the bullet for stability → accuracy at distance.

**Semi-auto carbines (civilian-legal in many places):**
— Shortened rifles; often intermediate or full-power cartridges.
— Examples vary by region: AK-pattern semiautos, AR-15 pattern, hunting self-loaders.

**Bolt-action hunting rifles:**
— Manual bolt lift/pull/push — slow reload, high precision potential.
— Examples: Tikka, Browning X-Bolt class; many regional bolt guns.

**Bolt operation (concept):**
— Lift handle → pull back (extract/eject) → forward (chamber) → close (lock).

**Common cartridges (examples):**
— **.22 LR** — small, cheap training/small game.
— **7.62×39 mm** — very common globally (AK-family lineage).
— **5.45×39 mm** — AK-74 lineage where encountered.
— **7.62×54R** — older military/hunting long range in Eastern Europe and Asia.
— **5.56×45 mm NATO** — AR-15/M16 family.
— **.308 Win / 7.62×51 mm** — NATO/western hunting and battle-rifle class.


SELECT-FIRE RIFLES (E.G. AK FAMILY) — MILITARY CONTEXT

**AKM / AK-47 / AK-74 / AK-12** pattern rifles are widespread in some conflict zones and former military stocks.

**Military** versions may have **full-auto** or burst; **civilian** clones are often **semi-auto only** — **legality varies by country.**

**AK-pattern selector (typical military layout — verify each gun):**
— Often **top** = full-auto (where present), **middle** = semi, **bottom** = safe — **or** safe blocks the charging handle — **learn the specific firearm.**

**Carry with selector on safe** when moving unless immediate use is justified and lawful.

**Mag-fed semi-auto shotguns** (e.g. “Saiga-12” class): **5–10** round magazines common — same safety rules as any semi-auto.


SUBMACHINE GUNS / PDWS

Pistol cartridge; selective fire in military form; compact.
Examples in some militaries: Bizon, PP-2000 class — **civilian possession usually heavily restricted.**


RANGE COMPARISON (RULE OF THUMB)

| Type        | Typical practical range |
|------------|-------------------------|
| Pistol     | To ~50 m (most users less) |
| Shotgun (shot) | ~20–40 m |
| Rifle/carbine | ~100–600+ m (cartridge/optics/skill) |
| Military rifle (semi) | ~300–400 m area targets with iron sights |


IDENTIFYING A FOUND GUN — FIRST LOOK

1. **Safe direction.**
2. **Pistol?** Short barrel, grip below action.
3. **Shotgun?** Long smooth barrel; break-open or pump forend.
4. **Rifle/AK pattern?** Long barrel, magazine often ahead of trigger.
5. Locate **safety** and **magazine release** only **after** muzzle and finger discipline.
6. Then follow **15.4** to clear the weapon.
