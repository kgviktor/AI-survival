CHAPTER 15. FIREARMS: HANDLING BASICS
15.6 Malfunctions: misfire, jam, double feed

WHAT A “MALFUNCTION” IS

Any interruption in the firing cycle that is **not** a broken part — often fixable in seconds.

**Panic is the enemy.** Use **deliberate** steps, not guesses.


BASELINE: TAP–RACK (SEMI-AUTO PISTOL / RIFLE)

For many self-loaders:

1. **TAP** — Smack magazine base **upward** — seated fully?
2. **RACK** — Pull charging handle/slide **fully** back, **release** — ejects problem round/case, chambers fresh.
3. Re-acquire target; decide whether to fire again.

Fixes many **fail-to-feed** and some **stovepipes.** If not, go deeper.


MISFIRE (CLICK, NO BANG)

**Causes:** bad primer, weak mainspring, broken firing pin, oil in primer pocket, bad ammo.

**Hangfire risk:** shot may fire **late** (**1–30 s** possible — treat seriously).

**Steps:**
1. **Muzzle safe** direction.
2. **Wait ~30 seconds.**
3. **Tap–rack** or manually clear per manual — eject the round.
4. **Segregate** that round — do not mix with “good” ammo.
5. **Repeated** misfires: clean firing pin channel; inspect firing pin; try different ammo lot.


FAILURE TO FEED (ROUND DOES NOT CHAMBER)

**Signs:** bolt/slide stuck **out** of battery; cartridge visible at angle.

**Causes:** damaged round, dirty/damaged **magazine**, weak recoil spring, bad mag lips.

**Steps:**
1. Tap–rack.
2. If still stuck: **remove magazine**, **pull** stuck round clear by hand (muzzle safe).
3. Inspect round — dented? Discard.
4. Inspect **mag lips** — bent?
5. Reinsert mag; rack.
6. **Repeat** → try **another magazine.**


DOUBLE FEED (TWO ROUNDS FIGHTING FOR CHAMBER)

Serious; slower to clear.

**Steps:**
1. Muzzle safe.
2. **Strip magazine** (may require force).
3. **Rack hard** several times — clear both rounds/cases.
4. Verify **empty chamber.**
5. Insert mag; rack; continue.

**Often caused by:** weak extraction (case stuck) + new round trying to feed — clean **extractor**; check ejector.


STOVEPIPE / FAILURE TO EJECT

Case stuck partly in ejection port.

1. Tap–rack first.
2. If still there: **remove** case by hand (muzzle safe, action open).

**Repeat stovepipes** → clean extractor; check recoil spring; check ammo.


BOLT SHORT-STROKING (WON’T LOCK / WON’T FULLY EJECT)

**Causes:** dirty gas system, weak spring, heavy friction on rails.

**Field fix:** clean piston/tube (if applicable), lube rails lightly; long-term — replace springs.


PUMP SHOTGUN

Manual cycling avoids most auto-feed issues.

**Click, no bang:**
— Wait **30 s** (hangfire).
— Open action — eject shell; next from tube.

**Forend stuck rearward:**
— Find **action release** / **bolt release** (model-dependent) — then cycle.


AK-SPECIFIC TIPS

**Bolt won’t go fully forward:**
— **Palm-rack** forward on charging handle.
— If fails: mag out, rack back, clear obstruction, reseat mag.

**Chronic nose-up jams:**
— Often **magazine** damage or wrong mag for variant — swap mag.


PREVENTION

— Clean on schedule (**15.5**).
— **Good ammo**, dry storage.
— **Serviceable magazines** — lips straight, spring strong.
— **Light** correct lubrication.
— Function-check on a **known-safe range** before relying on a gun.

**Quick mag check:** load rounds; top round should press **smoothly**; mag **clicks** in solid; no rattles that suggest broken follower (varies).
