CHAPTER 11. TOOLS AND REPAIR
11.2 Clothing and footwear repair without stores

WHY IT MATTERS

Long crises wear out garments and boots; replacements vanish. Repair skill is the gap between hypothermia and working insulation.

Core skills:
— Sew tears and holes.
— Seal leaking footwear.
— Insulate or adapt layers.
— Improvise simple garments from scrap materials.


MINIMUM SEWING KIT

— Needles: ~#3–5 cloth; #1–2 heavy for canvas/leather.
— Thread: synthetic stronger than cotton when wet; cotton in several colors.
— Thimble.
— Scissors.
— Pins.
— Sewing awl / speedy stitcher for thick work.
— Awl to pierce leather.

Extras:
— Assorted buttons.
— Snaps.
— Zippers several lengths.
— Elastic bands.
— Hook-and-loop (sew-on or adhesive).
— Patch fabric scraps.
— Waxed or braided cord for boots and packs.


BASIC STITCHES

Running stitch:
— Needle weaves ~3–5 mm forward — fast, weaker.
— Temporary or light fabric.

Backstitch:
— Stronger than many machine seams for strain areas.
— Forward one stitch, back half, forward two — thread doubles back.

Flat seam (join two pieces):
— Face to face; backstitch ~1–1.5 cm from edge; press open.

Whip / overcast edge:
— Loop over edge every ~3–4 mm — stops fraying.


GARMENT REPAIRS

Small hole or cut:
— Backstitch from inside.
— Fraying edges: tiny dab **PVA** (white glue) on raw edge before sewing, or inner patch + perimeter stitch.

Inner patch:
1. Fabric ~3–4 cm larger than hole.
2. Place inside.
3. Small stitches through all layers around damage.
4. Flatten outside.

Outer (visible) patch:
1. Rounded corners — less peel.
2. Turn edge ~5–7 mm; press or finger-crease.
3. Blind or topstitch around.

Split side or shoulder seam:
— Unpick a few cm past tear; re-backstitch; **double-stitch** ends to lock.

Zipper faults:
— Slider jumped track: pliers to widen/narrow stop slightly, re-seat.
— Teeth gap: squeeze slider gently.
— Broken teeth: replace zip or sew closed and use buttons.
— Dead zip: sew snaps or buttons in line.

Button:
— X pattern through four holes or bar through two.
— Leave slight **shank** (thread stem) for thickness.
— Drop of clear nail polish on thread wrap — anti-chafe.


FOOTWEAR

Common failures: sole delamination, upper tear, blown seam, leaks.

Sole off — glue:
— **Shoe contact cement** (neoprene/rubber type): best bond.
— Degrease both sides (alcohol, acetone — ventilate, no flame).
— Coat both faces; open ~10–15 min until tacky; press; tap with hammer through cloth; **cure ~24 h** before hard use.

Sole off — no glue:
— **Waxed boot thread** + curved harness needle — stitch through sole and welt; lock every ~3–4 stitches so one break does not unzip all.

Leaks:
— Wax or heavy grease worked into dry leather.
— Silicone waterproofer spray/liquid.
— Improvise: birch tar (traditional), animal tallow — test stain first.
— Seal stitched sole edge with flexible adhesive if seams wick water.

Torn leather upper:
— Awl + heavy thread; leather patch overlap + glue.

Worn tread:
— Rubber sheet from tube, conveyor belt scrap — contact cement.
— Tire tread caps — grip on ice/snow (heavy).

Shoelaces:
— Cord, thin leather strip, twisted cloth strip, cable in emergency.


INSULATION AND ADAPTATION

Add warmth inside:
— Fleece or wool liner sewn in.
— Batting between shell and liner.
— **Newspaper** stuffed under jacket — emergency insulator.
— Mylar / foil survival blanket as inner radiant layer.

Wind/rain:
— Dense outer shell.
— Trash-bag poncho — holes for head/arms.

Fit hacks:
— Cuff trousers for boots; hem.
— Sleeve tubes as gaiters.
— Hand muff: ~30×20 cm tube from any warm scrap.


SIMPLE MAKE ITEMS

Mittens:
— Trace hand + ~1 cm; two layers face-to-face; sew; turn; optional insulation layer.

Balaclava from sweater sleeve:
— Cut sleeve; face opening; close one end.

Bandana / neck wrap:
— ~60×60 cm square; triangle fold.

Tarp poncho:
— ~1.5×2 m sheet; center head hole — rain cover without sewing.
