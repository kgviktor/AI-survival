CHAPTER 15. FIREARMS: HANDLING BASICS
15.4 Safely unloading an unfamiliar firearm

RULE BEFORE YOU START

**Rushing while unloading kills.**

Before any manipulation:
1. Muzzle — **safe direction** (into ground or open sky as appropriate).
2. Finger — **outside** the trigger guard, **off** the trigger.
3. Breathe. Slow down. Think.

Goal: **cleared** gun — **empty chamber**, ammunition source removed, **verified** by eye and touch.


UNIVERSAL ALGORITHM

**Step 1 — Identify type**
— Pistol? AK-pattern rifle? Break-action or pump shotgun?
— Where is the magazine? The safety?

**Step 2 — Safety ON** if you can identify it confidently.
— If unsure, **do not** fumble; keep finger off trigger and muzzle safe.

**Step 3 — Remove ammunition supply**
— **Box magazine:** magazine catch (often left side of pistol grip; AK-style catch in front of mag).
— **Revolver:** cylinder release; swing cylinder out.
— **Shotgun tube:** may need to cycle rounds out (see pump section).

**Step 4 — Open action; check chamber**
— Fully retract bolt/slide or open action.
— **Look** into chamber — should see empty, shiny chamber mouth.
— **Feel** with fingertip **only** if you can do so without covering muzzle or violating safe direction.

**Step 5 — Confirm**
— Bolt locked back or chamber visibly open; mag out or tube empty per checks.

**Step 6 — Store** firearm and ammunition **separately** when policy requires.


SEMI-AUTO PISTOL

1. Safe direction.
2. Find **magazine release** — often left side behind trigger guard.
3. Strip magazine; set aside.
4. **Rack slide fully rearward** — live round in chamber should eject.
5. **Visually and physically** verify chamber empty.
6. Use **slide lock** if present to hold open.
7. **Never** assume “mag out = unloaded” — **chamber can still hold one round.**

**Makarov-type note:** mag release often **heel** of grip (push up/in per model). Slide-mounted safety may **also** decock — **learn the specific pistol.**


REVOLVER

1. Safe direction.
2. Cylinder latch — usually left of frame.
3. Swing cylinder out to the side.
4. Muzzle **up** or **down** into safe cone; use **ejector rod** to dump rounds.
5. Verify **each** chamber empty.
6. Close cylinder.


AK-PATTERN (AKM / AK-74, ETC.)

1. Safe direction.
2. Selector to **safe** (often lowest position — **confirm on your sample**).
3. Mag catch: typically **rock and pull** or paddle — **remove magazine.**
4. If safe **locks charging handle**, move selector to allow **single-fire** position only **after** mag is out and you intend to open chamber — **extreme care.**
5. **Pull charging handle fully back** — eject any chambered round.
6. Inspect chamber; finger-check if safe to do so.
7. Return selector to **safe** when done.


PUMP SHOTGUN (TUBULAR MAGAZINE)

1. Safe direction.
2. If present, use **action release / bolt release / carrier cut-off** (often left side) so the action can cycle without feeding from tube — models vary.
3. Cycle **forend** to eject chambered round.
4. Repeat: with cut-off engaged, cycle to clear tube one round at a time — **count** rounds out.
5. Visually/tactile verify **chamber and magazine tube empty.**

If you cannot find the cut-off: **slowly** cycle until chamber and tube are clearly empty — keep muzzle safe **every** stroke.


BREAK-ACTION SHOTGUN

1. Safe direction.
2. Find **opening lever** (often ahead of trigger guard).
3. **Break** open; shells fall or pull free.
4. Visually verify both chambers empty.
5. Close gently.

**Stuck shells:** no brute force — pry carefully or get help; **muzzle safe** throughout.


DANGEROUS SITUATIONS WHILE CLEARING

**Click instead of bang (misfire):**
— Keep muzzle **safe** **~30 seconds** — **hangfire** possible.
— Then clear per normal procedure.

**Stuck round / stuck bolt:**
— No hammering on a loaded chamber in unsafe directions.
— Muzzle safe; retry full retraction; if still stuck — **expert help.**

**Cleaning rod from muzzle end:**
— Only with muzzle in **safe direction** and action open/cleared; used to push stuck bullet/shell — **know what you are doing.**

**“They said it was empty”**
— **Never** trust words alone. **Always** verify yourself.
