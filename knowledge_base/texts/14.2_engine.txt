CHAPTER 14. VEHICLE: SERVICE AND REPAIR
14.2 Engine: oil, coolant, belts, spark plugs

ENGINE OIL

Lubricates, cools internals, cleans, protects metal.

**No oil = seized engine in about a minute.**

**Grade** (e.g. 5W-30):
— First number + W — cold cranking viscosity (lower = easier winter start).
— Second number — hot operating viscosity (higher = thicker film, more protection when hot).

Common: 5W-30, 5W-40, 10W-40 — **use owner manual spec.**

**API / ACEA** ratings — match gasoline vs diesel and manufacturer.

Types: mineral (shorter interval), synthetic/long-life (longer interval, better extremes).

**Change** per schedule or **at least annually** even if low miles. After long storage, change.

**DIY oil change:**
1. Warm engine ~5–10 min.
2. Drain plug out (catch hot oil safely).
3. Drain fully; replace **filter** (lube gasket with fresh oil).
4. Reinstall plug; fill to mid-dipstick; run; recheck.

Capacity often ~4–6 L — verify manual.

Top-up: same spec preferred; in emergency **any correct-viscosity clean oil beats running low.**

**Crisis stock:** ~5 L spare in vehicle.


COOLANT

Transfers heat; freeze/boil protection; corrosion inhibitors.

Overheat can **warp head** — very expensive.

**Types:** Older silicate (often green), OAT (often red/orange), HOAT hybrids — **do not mix incompatible chemistries** — sludge risk.

Check **cold** level in reservoir.

**Freeze protection** — measure with **refractometer or test strips** — target your climate (e.g. −35 °C class in hard winter regions).

Top-up: same coolant or **distilled water** short-term only.

**Overheat en route:**
1. Safe stop; shut down.
2. Hood up; wait **20–30 min**.
3. **Never** open hot pressurized cap.
4. When cool: add coolant/water; limp gently if you must.

**Temporary radiator leak:** commercial stop-leak per label; egg white folklore — last-resort experiment only.


BELTS

**Timing belt/chain** — synchronizes cam(s) and crank. **Belt snap on interference engine** = bent valves — catastrophic. Interval per manual (~60–100k km typical belt). Inspect for cracks, missing teeth.

**Serpentine / V-belt** — drives alternator, often water pump and PS. Loss = no charge, overheat risk if pump on belt.

V-belt deflection ~10–15 mm mid span rule-of-thumb.

**If serpentine fails:** cut electrical load; battery dies in tens of minutes.

**Emergency V-belt:** pantyhose trick — **few km only** to help.


SPARK PLUGS

Worn plugs: hard start, rough idle, mpg loss.

Types: copper shorter life; iridium/platinum long life.

Gap ~0.8–1.1 mm typical — spec in manual.

**Read plugs:** tan/gray OK; soot — rich/oil; white/ash — lean/overheat; melted — severe detonation/overheat.

Replace **cold** with proper socket; torque or “snug + ¼ turn” per aluminum head caution.

**Spare set** in crisis kit (count cylinders).


EXHAUST

Routes gases away; reduces noise; **contains CO** — **never** idle in an enclosed space (see Ch. 8 CO).

**Leak before the catalytic converter** — noise; fumes in cabin if floor pans rust — **danger**.

**Flex pipe** cracks — replace section.

**Gasket leaks** — replace when cold.

**Temporary repair** — exhaust wrap/putty — short limp only.

**Field weld** — only outdoors with fire-safe practice.


COOLING SYSTEM (EXTENDED)

Radiator → pump → engine → thermostat → back to radiator.

**Thermostat stuck closed** — rapid overheat.

**Stuck open** — slow warm-up, weak cabin heat, longer rich running.

Replace per manual or ~100k km; **test in hot water** — should open near stamped temperature.

**Water pump** — weep-hole drip, bearing play, noise — fix before remote trips if marginal.

**Radiator** — keep external fins clear (mud/bugs); internal flush if chronic overheat.

**Hoses** — squeeze cold; spongy/crackle inside = failure coming; bulges = replace.

**Pressure cap** — holds ~1–1.4 bar, raises boil point; bad cap = premature boil-over.

**Electric fans** — should cycle in traffic; if not — fuse, relay, sensor, motor.

**Heater core** — cabin heat; clog = no heat, engine may be fine; leak = sweet smell/fog — temporary **bypass loop** = no cabin heat but may limp.


IF IT OVERHEATS

1. Heater **full hot**, cabin fan max — extra heat dump.
2. Safe stop; idle only if no heavy steam — if steam, **shut down**.
3. Cool **20–30 min**; **do not** open hot cap.
4. When cool: top up; find leak; drive gently with heat on if needed.

**Head gasket** clues — constant white vapor, coolant loss, oil “milkshake,” bubbles at radiator filler, compression test.


FIELD KIT (COOLING)

Spare upper/lower hose + clamps; coolant concentrate + distilled water; spare cap if common; thermostat if you can R&R.
