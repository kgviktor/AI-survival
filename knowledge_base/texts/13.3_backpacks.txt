CHAPTER 13. GEAR AND EQUIPMENT
13.3 Backpacks: volume, fit, packing, weight

WHY THE RIGHT PACK MATTERS

On evacuation or long foot movement the pack is your house. Poor fit or packing multiplies pain and fatigue.


VOLUME GUIDE

≤~20 L — day / urban EDC — too small for full bug-out.

~20–35 L — overnight ultralight.

**~40–60 L — main recommendation** for multi-day autonomy: sleep system, shelter, food, water, tools.

~60–80+ L — expedition / winter base loads; heavy empty; awkward for fast movement.

**Bug-out sweet spot** for many people: ~55–65 L.


CONSTRUCTION

**Internal frame** — modern standard; body-hugging.

**Frameless** — light; ≤~8–10 kg loads only.

**Hip belt** — carries ~60–70% of weight on pelvis; essential ≥~10 kg loads.

**Sternum strap** — stabilizes shoulder straps.

**Torso length** — sized from C7 vertebra to iliac crest — not overall height. Sizes: short / regular / long per brand chart.

**Hydration sleeve** — bladder compatible.

**MOLLE** — modular pouches for tactical / modular kits.


PACKING RULE

**Heavy close to spine and high-mid** — not low or dangling outward.

Zones:
1. **Bottom:** sleeping bag, camp-only light items.
2. **Against back (mid):** dense food, water, stove — center of mass near body.
3. **Outer mid:** clothes, pot.
4. **Top / lid:** rain shell, first aid, snacks.
5. **Side pockets:** bottles, quick items.

Mistakes: heavy low or far from back — lumbar pull; hard lumps against spine — bruises; one giant sack — chaos.

**Comfort target:** ~15–20% body weight; **max** ~30% for fit hikers — weigh the pack on a scale.


WATER CARRY

Rigid bottles in side pockets — no pack removal.

Bladder + tube — drink on the move; hard to gauge remaining; freezes in deep cold.

Hot work: ~0.5 L/h moderate climate — plan accordingly.


BUG-OUT BAG NOTES

— Low-visibility colors (gray, olive, black) vs hi-vis unless you want to be seen.
— YKK zippers and solid buckles are worth it.
— Reinforced bottom wears first.

**Core contents idea:** water + filter; 3–5 days food; sleep + pad or bivy; clothes layers; med kit; knife + multitool; light + cells; fire; comms; compass + map; docs copy; cash; paracord; tape.

Pre-built kit by door; **rotate** food and meds every ~6 months; **try on** yearly.


BRANDS (ORIENTATION)

Hiking: Osprey, Deuter, Gregory, many house brands.

Tactical: 5.11 Rush series, Tasmanian Tiger, Mystery Ranch, Maxpedition.

Budget: Decathlon / house brands — OK for moderate loads.
