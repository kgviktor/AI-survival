CHAPTER 15. FIREARMS: HANDLING BASICS
15.1 Four rules of safety — absolute, no exceptions

INTRODUCTION

This chapter is about **safe handling** of firearms you might encounter in a crisis: found, captured, or handed over by someone you do not know.

A gun in untrained hands is often **more dangerous** than no gun at all. Most accidents come from breaking basic rules — usually when the person believes the gun is “safe.”

**Rule zero:** start with this chapter.


THE FOUR RULES

These are not tips. They are **absolute**. Breaking one rule is bad; breaking **two at once** kills people.

**RULE 1: TREAT EVERY FIREARM AS LOADED**

— Always. No exceptions.
— Even if you just unloaded it yourself.
— Even if someone said “it’s not loaded.”
— Even if you checked ten minutes ago.

**Why:** A round can remain in the chamber after the magazine is removed. Someone may have rechambered. You may have made a mistake. The habit of “it’s unloaded” is a deadly habit.

**In practice:** Finger **off** the trigger, **along the frame**. Muzzle pointed in a **safe direction** — always.


**RULE 2: NEVER POINT THE MUZZLE AT ANYTHING YOU ARE NOT WILLING TO DESTROY**

— The bore line is a line of death. Whatever is in front of it is a potential target.
— “Joking” or “checking” by aiming at a person breaks this rule.
— When carrying: muzzle **down** into a safe surface or **up** (outdoors, with sky clear of aircraft/obstacles).
— Indoors: know **exactly** where the muzzle points at all times.

**Safe directions:**
— Down into earth/floor — **not** through walls/floors where people may be.
— Up into open sky (outdoor use only — know overhead).


**RULE 3: KEEP YOUR FINGER OFF THE TRIGGER UNTIL YOU HAVE DECIDED TO FIRE**

— Finger straight along the frame/receiver. **Outside** the guard.
— On the trigger only when the target is **identified** and you have chosen to shoot.
— Stress, fear, a fall → reflex grip → **unintentional discharge.**

**Common beginner error:** hand goes to the trigger as soon as the gun is picked up. Unlearn that.


**RULE 4: BE SURE OF YOUR TARGET AND WHAT IS BEHIND IT**

— A bullet often **does not stop** in the target; it can pass through.
— Before firing: what is **beyond** the target? Walls? People? Open space?
— At night: are you sure you see **the** target?
— Bullets can penetrate many interior walls, doors, and bodies.

**In practice:**
— Do not shoot at **noise** or **unidentified movement.**
— “Shooting at a sound” in a crisis can kill someone from your own group.
— Always know what is behind the target.


MECHANICAL SAFETIES DO NOT REPLACE THE RULES

A safety can:
— Fail.
— Be bumped off.
— Not work after impact or a drop.

The four rules apply **whether the safety is on or off.**

“Safety on” does **not** mean you can ignore the rules.


FIRST CONTACT WITH AN UNFAMILIAR GUN

Immediate sequence:

1. **Do not panic or rush.**
2. Point the muzzle in a **safe direction** — now.
3. Finger **off** the trigger — now.
4. Engage the safety **if** you can identify it.
5. Look at the gun: pistol? Shotgun? Rifle?
6. Find and remove the **ammunition source** (magazine, tube, etc.).
7. Open the action; **visually and physically** verify the chamber is **empty.**
8. Only then treat further handling as “cleared” for that moment.

Details: see **15.4** (safely unloading an unfamiliar firearm).


SAFE STORAGE AROUND PEOPLE

— For long storage: gun **separate** from ammunition when policy/safety allows.
— “Ready” posture: firearm **under control** of a designated responsible adult.
— Children: guns **out of reach** and secured.
— **Never** leave a loaded gun unattended.
— Handoff: **person to person**, state condition clearly (loaded/cleared).


PSYCHOLOGY

“I know what I’m doing” is one of the most dangerous thoughts around guns.

**There is no true “accidental” shot** — there is always a broken rule (or several).

Under stress with a found gun, the rules matter **more**, not less. Stress is not an excuse.
