CHAPTER 7. NAVIGATION AND MOVEMENT
7.4 Night movement

WHEN NIGHT MOVEMENT MAKES SENSE

Harder and riskier than day — sometimes necessary:
— High daytime detection risk (patrols, observers).
— Emergency evacuation.
— Summer heat — cooler at night.
— Must cross open ground unseen.

When to avoid:
— Unfamiliar terrain with cliff/bog hazards.
— Heavy urban ruin without light.
— Fog, whiteout — disorientation.
— If you can choose: travel by day, rest at night.


NIGHT VISION ADAPTATION

Dark adaptation:
— First ~5–10 min partial; full ~30–45 min.
— Bright light resets it instantly.

Preserve night vision:
— Dim light only for ~30–45 min before moving.
— For map/phone: close one eye — keep other dark-adapted.
— Red light preserves scotopic vision better than white — red lens or filter.

Peripheral viewing:
— Look ~10–15° off target — rods detect dim motion better than central fovea.
— Scan slowly with side vision.

Sudden flashes (explosion, headlights):
— Close one eye before/during flash — one retina keeps adaptation.


QUIET MOVEMENT

Walking:
— Toe contact, slow roll to heel.
— Slower than feels needed — sharp steps noise.
— Toe-test surface before loading weight.

Clothing:
— Cotton/wool often quieter than noisy synthetics.
— Pad or pocket metal (keys, buckles).
— Tighten pack straps — no flapping.

Packing:
— No rattles — pack tight, pad metal items.
— Shake pack test before leaving.

Listening halts:
— Every ~50–100 m in threat areas: stop ~30–60 s, listen.
— Human steps, voices, twig snaps, breathing.
— Normal wind/animal noise vs suspicious silence (birds stopped).


NIGHT NAVIGATION

Without light:
— Tree silhouettes against sky.
— Wind direction if known from day.
— Polaris if clear (Northern Hemisphere).
— Moon: rises east, sets west; apparent position moves through night — learn basic sky for your latitude.

Minimal flashlight:
— Red preferred; cup hand around lens; beam at feet only, not horizon.
— Prefer momentary on/off over long white flood.

Urban night cues:
— Window lights, fire glow.
— Moon on water.
— High-rise silhouettes.
— Smell: food, smoke → people nearby.


GROUP MOVEMENT AT NIGHT

Spacing:
— ~2–3 m if you can see lead silhouette.
— Very dark: light touch on forward pack strap.

Signals:
— Agreed hand squeezes: one = stop, two = go, three rapid = danger.
— Voice — whisper only when needed.

Point man:
— Slow; picks route.
— Hand down = step; arms spread = obstacle, bypass.

If separated:
— Freeze; quiet recall signal.
— Do not run randomly.
— Use prearranged rally point.


SPECIAL CONDITIONS

Moon:
— Full moon: you see better — you are also more visible.
— New moon: stealthiest, poorest vision.
— Moon behind cloud: you in shadow while open ground may still be lit — use edges.

Snow:
— Reflects light — brighter night; dark clothing silhouettes — white camo if hiding.
— Crunch is hard to silence — slow steps; morning crust may be quieter than fresh powder.

Fog:
— Visibility ~5–20 m common — easy to get lost; compass every ~50 paces.
— Sound carries oddly.
— Often better to wait.

Rain:
— Footsteps masked.
— Visibility drops; hypothermia risk if slow/wet.
— Guards may be less alert — not reliable.


TIME WINDOWS

For stealth, ~03:00–05:00 often cited:
— Human activity low.
— Many people’s alertness dips before dawn.
— Still dark in summer at mid-latitudes.

Higher risk ~20:00–23:00:
— Active looting/patrol periods in some crises.
— Most people still awake.
