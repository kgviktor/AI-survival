CHAPTER 12. GROUP AND SOCIAL SURVIVAL
12.9 Rules and justice without the state

WHY RULES EXIST

Without norms, the strong prey on the weak — group splits or becomes a predatory band. Surviving communities **always** generate expectations and enforcement.

Goals:
— Predictability.
— Protection from abuse.
— Conflict resolution short of violence.
— Trust and cooperation.

Rules should be:
— **Simple** — memorized by all.
— **Published** — agreed aloud or signed.
— **Executable** — not fantasy law codes.
— **Fair** — same for leader and led.


MINIMUM COMMUNITY CODE (EXAMPLE)

1. No internal violence except lawful self-defense.
2. No theft from common stores.
3. Required contribution of labor by ability.
4. Shared resources issued by rule — no private hoarding of communal food/meds without consent.
5. No unauthorized disclosure of shelter location, routines, or strength to outsiders.
6. Major decisions by vote or delegated leader per charter.
7. New members complete probation.

Write it on paper; mark or sign acceptance.


JUSTICE WITHOUT COURTS

Principles:
— **Facts**, not rumor.
— Hear both sides.
— Proportionate consequences.
— Transparency — no secret tribunals unless operational security absolutely demands, and then minimal circle.

Process:
1. What happened? Witnesses?
2. Accused speaks.
3. Council or full group deliberation.
4. Verdict and sentence announced.
5. Execute sentence without mob theater where dignity allows.


GRADUATED RESPONSES

**Minor** — missed watch, grabbed extra ration once:
— First: public reminder + norm restatement.
— Repeat: lose perks (extra ration, free time).

**Serious** — theft, sleeping on watch, dangerous lies:
— Full hearing; role loss; extra labor; temporary ration cut; restitution if possible.

**Grave** — assault, sexual violence, murder, betrayal to enemies:
— Immediate separation/isolation; full process; outcomes may include **expulsion**, secure confinement if you can run it safely, or other measures the group accepts under extreme circumstances.
— **Capital punishment** — if ever considered, requires overwhelming evidence, neutral process, and understanding it destroys souls and unity — historically rare and always controversial.

**Mob “justice” risks:**
— Strong members purge rivals; false accusations from grudges; rage killings you regret.

Guards:
— Cooling-off delay before severe punishment.
— **Three** trusted people minimum for major sanctions.
— Accused always speaks.
— **False accusation** proven → accuser faces the penalty that threatened the innocent.


PROPERTY

**Personal:** what they brought or earned under rules — inviolate without consent or due process.

**Common:** pooled production — quartermaster log, inspectable by members.

**Forage / raid haul:** decide **before** first run:
— All common + bonus to fetchers, or
— Personal gear to fetcher, bulk food/common — **write it down.**


GUESTS AND TRADERS

Guests:
— Not full rights; always escorted; no defense briefings or pantry tours; removable for cause without debate.

External traders:
— Neutral ground; witnessed deals; no unsecured credit on first contact; reputation is currency — cheat once, lose partners.


RECORDS

Log decisions, rules changes, major distributions, incidents. Stops “I never agreed” disputes and preserves continuity if governance returns.
