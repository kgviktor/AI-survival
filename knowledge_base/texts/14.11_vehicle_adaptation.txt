CHAPTER 14. VEHICLE: SERVICE AND REPAIR
14.11 Vehicle adaptation for survival: kit, low profile, protection

CHOOSING A CRISIS VEHICLE

If you can choose:

**Diesel:**
— Often **+20–30%** range per liter vs similar gasoline.
— Torque: better loaded/off-road.
— Can use biodiesel / SVO discussions in some regions (see 14.10).
— Often durable for long hours.

**Ground clearance:**
— **≥200 mm** helps rough tracks; trucks, SUVs, true 4×4s.

**Manual transmission:**
— Simpler field repair; bump-start; often less slip loss in mud.

**Body-on-frame (true trucks/SUVs):**
— Tougher in hard abuse than many unibody crossovers.
— Easier bumper/winch/bull bar mounting.

**Simpler electronics:**
— Fewer modules to fail; pre-**~2005–2010** often simpler (varies).
— **Carb + distributor:** nearly EMP-proof in theory — obsolete for most.

**Examples often cited for rough-country service** (parts/support vary by region):
— Simple 4×4s with solid axles (e.g. UAZ-class, Lada Niva-class where common).
— Toyota Land Cruiser / Hilux, Mitsubishi Pajero, Nissan Patrol, Land Rover Defender-class, full-size pickups.


STOCKS IN THE VEHICLE (BASELINE)

**Water:** **10–20 L** in cans.

**Food:** **3–7 days** — cans, dry staples, bars.

**Fuel:** **20–40 L** reserve when policy allows.

**Tools:**
— Jack + lug wrench.
— General tool roll (wrenches, screwdrivers, pliers).
— Kinetic strap + rated recovery points knowledge.
— Shovel (folding or full).
— Duct tape, hose clamps, wire.
— Tire repair kit.

**Medical:** expanded vehicle first aid.

**Comms:** radio; spare phone or power bank.

**Thermal:** blanket or sleeping bag; spare warm clothes; candles + lighter if stranded in winter cold.

**Spares / consumables (match your model):**
— Serpentine belt; timing belt kit if due.
— Engine oil **2–3 L**; coolant **1 L**; brake fluid **0.5 L**.
— Fuse assortment; spark plugs set; common bulbs.
— Tie-rod ends; wheel bearings if you know weak points.


PHYSICAL PROTECTION

**Bull bar / brush guard:**
— Protects lights/radiator from brush/animal strikes.
— Winch mount point.
— Changes how the vehicle is perceived — trade-off.

**Skid plates (sump / gearbox / transfer):**
— Steel or aluminum underbody armor for rocks.

**Fuel tank guard:**
— Metal shield — common on serious 4×4s.

**Steel bumpers:**
— Recovery points integrated; Hi-Lift jacking points.

**Window mesh / grills:**
— Reduces smash-and-grab and thrown-object risk; security vs visibility trade-off.


LOW PROFILE (NOT “STANDING OUT”)

Why:
— Flashy or “tactical” vehicles attract attention in unrest.

**Color:**
— Dull neutrals: gray, olive drab, earth brown.
— Pure black: hot in sun; very visible at night with dust.
— Avoid bright red/white/yellow if blending matters.

**Remove identifiers:**
— Stickers, club badges, government-style markings if they read as privilege/target.

**Camo nets:**
— Park under cover; net + local brush breaks outline from distance/drone casual view.

**Light discipline:**
— Headlights only when needed; parking lights vs full beams; **red** interior light preserves night vision and reduces external glow.


VEHICLE ELECTRICS AS A RESOURCE

**Running engine charges battery** — use **inverter 12 V → AC** for small loads **only with engine running** and adequate wiring/fusing.

**300–1000 W** inverters exist; cigarette-lighter types are **limited** — heavy loads need battery clamps and short thick leads.

**Second battery + isolator** — house loads without killing start battery.

**Roof solar 100–200 W** — trickle/maintenance charge for aux battery when parked.


WINTER PREP

Antifreeze rated for your worst night (e.g. **−40 °C** class where such nights occur).

Winter tires; studs where legal/useful.

**Snow chains** for extremes.

Winter diesel or anti-gel.

Battery with adequate **CCA** for your climate.

De-icer for locks/glass; shovel; bag of sand/gravel.

**Emergency thermal blanket** in trunk for breakdown in cold.
