CHAPTER 10. FOOD PRODUCTION
10.5 Compost and fertilizers from waste

WHY COMPOST

Intensive growing strips soil nutrients in 1–2 seasons without inputs.

Compost:
— Recycles organics into stable humus.
— Low or zero cash cost.
— Improves water holding, drainage, and aeration.
— Cuts landfill volume.

Process: microbes and worms break organics → humus plants can use.


HOT COMPOST (FAST — ~4–8 WEEKS)

Needs:
— C:N ratio ~25–30:1 overall.
  — **Brown / high carbon:** dry leaves, straw, paper, cardboard, wood chips.
  — **Green / high nitrogen:** food scraps, fresh grass, fresh manure.
— Moisture ~50–60% — “wrung sponge.”
— Oxygen — turn pile every ~3–7 days.
— Volume — ~1 m³+ holds heat better.
— Core temperature ~50–70 °C kills many pathogens and weed seeds (if maintained).

Greens — add:
+ Veg/fruit peels, coffee grounds, tea leaves.
+ Fresh grass clippings, green leaves.
+ Herbivore manure (chicken, rabbit, horse, cow).
− **Avoid:** meat, fish, dairy, lots of oil (odor, vermin), diseased plants.

Browns — add:
+ Dry leaves, shredded cardboard (minimal glossy ink), straw, sawdust (untreated).
+ Eggshell.
+ A little wood ash (alkaline; K and P).
− **Avoid:** charcoal briquettes (often not compostable), heavy glossy magazines.

Process:
1. Alternate green and brown layers ~1:2 by volume.
2. Moisten dry layers as you build.
3. Cover (lid or black plastic) to keep heat/moisture.
4. In ~3–5 days center should heat — active composting.
5. Turn every ~3–7 days.
6. Done when dark brown, crumbly, earthy smell — not rotten.


COLD COMPOST (SLOW — ~6–12 MONTHS)

— Pile and ignore; minimal labor.
— Smaller piles, less heat, weed seeds may survive.
— Fine for low throughput or winter dumps.


URBAN COMPOSTING

Bins:
— Wood slatted box ~60×60×80 cm.
— Plastic bin with side/bottom holes.
— Woven branch cage.
— Pit in yard if soil allows.

Odor control:
— Always cap food scraps with browns.
— No meat/dairy in open urban bins.
— Stink = too wet or too green — add browns, turn.

Balcony worm bin (`10.4_urban_animals.txt`):
— Nearly odorless if balanced; produces leachate (“tea”) and castings.

Typical timelines:
— Hot: ~4–8 weeks.
— Worms: ~2–3 months to usable castings.
— Cold: ~6–12 months.


HOMEMADE FERTILIZERS

Wood ash:
— From clean wood (not painted, not coal briquettes).
— ~K₂O 8–10% plus Ca, P, traces.
— Handful in planting hole or ~1 Tbsp per liter water, steep ~24 h, dilute to water plants.
— Alkaline — good on acid soil; easy to overdo on peat-heavy mixes.

Green manure tea:
— Nettle, comfrey, dandelion — high N.
— Pack bucket, cover with water, steep ~2 weeks (smells).
— Dilute ~1:10 before watering soil.

Banana peel:
— Potassium source — bury by roots or soak few days, water in.

Eggshell:
— Calcium for tomatoes/peppers (blossom end rot prevention).
— Crush to powder; work into soil.
— Sharp edges may deter slugs slightly.

Coffee grounds:
— Mild acidifier + some N — berries, strawberries, tomatoes — moderate use in compost or top-dress.

Manure:
— Chicken: very “hot” — **compost before** applying or it burns plants.
— Rabbit: milder — can use carefully fresh in small amounts.
— Cow/horse: classic compost accelerators and bulk feeders.


GREEN MANURES (COVER CROPS)

Grow only to till in:
— Mustard, phacelia, clover, vetch, lupin, rye — fix N or biofumigate or prevent erosion.

Use:
1. Sow after main crop.
2. At ~15–20 cm, chop and dig in.
3. Wait ~2–3 weeks decomposition → plant next crop.


STORED MINERAL FERTILIZER (OPTIONAL CACHE)

Urea (carbamide):
— High N; stores years dry sealed.
— ~10–20 g per 10 L water for soil drench (rough — follow label).
— Foliar: weaker solution if crop tolerates.

Calcium nitrate:
— Ca + N — tomatoes, peppers.

Superphosphate:
— P for roots and fruiting — band at planting.

Strategy:
— Small sealed bags of urea + superphosphate as backup.
— Long term: compost + manure + cover crops cycles toward self-sufficiency.
