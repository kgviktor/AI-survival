CHAPTER 13. GEAR AND EQUIPMENT
13.1 Knives: types, steel, sharpening, care

WHY A KNIFE MATTERS

A knife is among the most universal tools: food prep, shelter, woodwork, medical tasks, last-resort defense. A good blade replaces many single-purpose tools.

**Better one good knife than five junk knives.**


KNIFE TYPES

Fixed vs folder:
— **Fixed:** strongest, no pivot failure, needs sheath; less pocket-friendly.
— **Folder:** compact EDC; weaker for prying; lint can foul lock.

**Carry:** primary fixed ~10–15 cm; small folder as backup; optional large chopper/machete for brush.

Blade shapes:
— **Clip point** — classic pierce + slice.
— **Drop point** — tough tip; great all-around survival profile.
— **Scandi grind** — easy to sharpen; wood carving.
— **Tanto** — strong tip; less common need.
— **Bowie / large camp** — chop and fight roles.

Sizes:
— Small ≤~9 cm: EDC, food, detail.
— Medium ~10–15 cm: general survival sweet spot.
— Large ~15–25 cm: brush, heavy wood.


STEEL BASICS

Four axes:
1. **Hardness (HRC)** — edge retention vs chipping.
2. **Toughness** — bend before break under shock.
3. **Corrosion resistance**
4. **Ease of sharpening** — often inverse to wear resistance.

**Stainless examples:** 420 (budget, soft); 440C; AUS-8; Sandvik 14C28N; VG-10; Crucible S30V / S35VN.

**Carbon / tool steels:** 1075/1095; O1; semi-stainless D2; powder Elmax / M390 (premium).

**Pick:** stainless (14C28N, S30V class) if you neglect oil; carbon (1095, O1) if you want max sharpness and will wipe the blade dry.


SHARPENING

Sharpness = bevel angle + apex polish.
— ~10–15° per side — very slicey, fragile.
— ~20–25° — general field.
— ~25–35° — chop / hard use.

Stones:
— ~120–240 — repair chips / reshape.
— ~400–600 — establish bevel.
— ~1000–2000 — working edge.
— ~3000+ — polish / shave.

Diamond plates work dry; oil or water on stones per manufacturer. **Strop** leather finishes the apex.

Field touch-up: smooth river rock; leather belt; ceramic mug bottom.

Tests: catches thumbnail; clean paper slice; arm hair (careful).


CARE

Stainless: wash and dry after food/blood/acid; occasional light oil.

Carbon: dry immediately; oil regularly; gray **patina** is normal; red rust — scrub ~400 grit + oil.

Sheaths: leather needs conditioner; Kydex is low maintenance; long storage — light oil on blade.


BRANDS (ROUGH TIERS)

**Budget:** Mora (Sweden) — excellent value; Ontario 1095 lines; many national makers with decent heat-treat.

**Mid:** Cold Steel (thick blades); Spyderco folders; Ka-Bar classics.

**Premium:** Benchmade; Böker; Fällkniven; Chris Reeve-class customs.

**Legal:** carry and blade length rules vary by country — comply.
