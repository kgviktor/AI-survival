CHAPTER 13. GEAR AND EQUIPMENT
13.4 Sleep systems: bags, pads, bivies

WHY SLEEP GEAR = SURVIVAL

Sleep debt hits strength and judgment in 24–48 h; immunity drops; wounds heal slower.

**Hypothermia while sleeping kills** — insulation is non-negotiable.


SLEEPING BAG RATINGS

**EN 13537 / ISO 23537** labels:

— **Comfort** — typical cold sleeper (often women’s reference).
— **Lower limit** — curled male reference sleeper.
— **Extreme** — survival, not comfort — **not** a camping target.

**Buy comfort rating ~5–10 °C colder** than your expected low.

Seasons:
— Summer ~+10 °C and up.
— 3-season ~0 to +10 °C class.
— Winter serious negative temps.
— Arctic — expedition.


DOWN VS SYNTHETIC

**Down** — fill power (FP): higher = more loft per gram. 650 FP baseline; 800+ premium. Light, compressible, long life if kept dry. **Wet down collapses** — hydrophobic treatments help.

**Synthetic** (Primaloft, etc.) — heavier bulk for same warmth; **works wet**.

**Survival pick:** dry climate / careful packing → down; wet environment → synthetic or treated down.


SHAPE

**Mummy** — efficient warmth-to-weight; tight for restless sleepers.

**Rectangular** — roomier, heavier, colder for same fill.

**Hood** — essential cold weather — major heat loss from head.


SLEEPING PADS (CRITICAL)

Ground steals heat ~30× faster than still air. **Pad R-value stacks** with sleeping bag warmth.

**Foam (EVA/XPE)** — indestructible, cheap, bulky. R ~1.5–3.

**Self-inflating** — foam + air; R ~2–6; puncture risk.

**Air** — light; R up to ~7+ on cold models; needs inflation; puncture risk.

**R-value guide:** ~1–2 summer; ~3–4 three-season; **5+** winter.

**Examples:** Z-Lite foam; NeoAir XTherm-class air for cold.


BIVY SACKS

Water/wind shell over bag — adds warmth, protects down from condensation, enables minimalist shelter.

**Breathable** membranes cost more; cheap plastic traps sweat.

**Emergency bivy / mylar bag** — ~50–100 g; reflects radiant heat; **not** a full bag substitute — carry as backup.


STORAGE AND CARE

**Do not** long-term store compressed — hangs or large sack preserves loft.

Wash down per down detergent; **dry completely** with tennis balls in dryer to restore loft.

DWR spray on shell renews bead.


COLD SLEEP STACK

1. Base layers dry on body.
2. Optional fleece liner inside bag (+few °C).
3. Bag.
4. Bivy outside bag if storming.
5. Pad below — never sleep on bare ground.

Head: balaclava / hat (old “40% heat loss from head” is overstated but head still matters).

Feet: dry socks before bed; **Nalgene hot water** at feet — classic trick.
