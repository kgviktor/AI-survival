CHAPTER 8. ENERGY AND ELECTRONICS
8.12 Powering devices from a car battery (without the car)

OVERVIEW

A 12 V automotive battery off the vehicle is a standalone DC source.
Typical capacity ~55–90 Ah → ~660–1080 Wh nameplate; **use only ~50%** of that on a starter/flooded battery or you shorten life badly.

Physically: two terminals — positive (+) and negative (−). All wiring goes between those two poles.


IDENTIFYING TERMINALS

Usually:
— Plus: red boot or “+” mark, often slightly larger post.
— Minus: black, “−”, often thinner.

If unmarked: DMM — red probe on one post, black on other; ~12.x V display means red probe is on +.

POLARITY — CRITICAL

Swapped +/− → short, sparks, fire or explosion risk.
**Think twice, connect once.** Connect order: **+ first, − second.** Disconnect: **− first, + second** — reduces spark risk if a loose + lead touches chassis.


WIRES: WHAT MATTERS

1. **Cross-section (mm² / AWG)** — current capacity; thin wire + high current = heat and fire.
2. **Length** — shorter = less drop.
3. **Insulation** — intact; bare metal touching chassis = short.

Rough guide:

Load / current        | Min copper (approx.) | Example
Up to ~5 A (phone)    | 1.5 mm² (~16 AWG)    | Light hookup wire
Up to ~10 A (USB hub, LED) | 2.5 mm² (~14 AWG) | Auto primary wire
Up to ~20 A (~150 W inverter) | 4–6 mm² (~10–12 AWG) | Heavy auto wire
Up to ~80 A (~1 kW inverter) | 16–25 mm² (~6–4 AWG) | Jumper cable class

Laptop via inverter: ~6 mm², length ≤ ~1.5 m typical.
Phone via USB buck: ~1.5–2.5 mm².

Sources: auto parts store PVC/hookup wire; salvage from heavy extension cord (often ~1.5–2.5 mm²); jumper cables with clamps (~16–25 mm²).

DIY leads with alligator clips:
— Correct gauge wire + two clips; strip 5–10 mm, secure under screw or crimp; tape joints; red for +, black/blue for −.


FUSE — MANDATORY

Protects wiring from fire on short. Place on **+** lead, **within ~30 cm** of battery + post.

Examples:
— USB buck to ~5 A load: **5–7 A** fuse.
— ~150 W inverter (~15 A): **~20 A**.
— ~300 W inverter (~30 A): **~35–40 A**.

Types: automotive blade (ATM/ATC) + inline holder — cheap and common.

If no fuse: never leave unattended; if wire warms, disconnect immediately.


THREE PATHS FROM BATTERY TO LOAD

PATH 1 — DIRECT 12 V

For 12 V native loads: LED strip, auto lamps, 12 V pump, CB rig with cigar plug, etc.

**Battery + → fuse → load +**  
**Load − → wire → battery −**

Device polarity: red = +, black = −. Swapped LED often just off; pump may run wrong way — swap leads.


PATH 2 — USB MODULE (phones, tablets, USB-C laptops)

12 V → 5 V (or PD) DC-DC “car USB” adapter.

Variants:
— Cigarette-lighter USB adapter: power the socket’s +/− from battery with fused leads.
— Panel USB module: two wires to battery terminals, USB out.
— 12 V → USB-C PD (e.g. 65 W) for PD-charging laptops.

**Battery → 5–7 A fuse → module → USB cable → device**


PATH 3 — INVERTER (230/120 V AC loads, stock laptop bricks)

**Battery → fuse → inverter → AC outlet → laptop brick → laptop**

Inverter +/− usually heavy red/black with clamps or ring terminals. Pure sine (~150–300 W class) is safer for picky laptop chargers than modified sine.

Use supplied inverter leads if rated; else ~6 mm², ~1–1.5 m.


LAPTOP SETUP (STEP BY STEP)

You need:
— ~60–90 Ah battery.
— ~150–300 W **pure sine** inverter.
— Two ~6 mm² leads ~1.5 m with clamps (or rings).
— **~25 A** fuse on + within ~20–30 cm of + post.

Steps:
1. Battery on flat, ventilated surface (gassing when charging).
2. Red: battery + → inverter + input.
3. Black: battery − → inverter − input.
4. Power inverter; indicator on.
5. Plug laptop brick into inverter; connect to laptop.

Monitor:
— Battery voltage (DMM or meter): below **~12.0 V** at rest under load → stop and recharge.
— Inverter temperature; keep vents clear.
— Inverter idle draw (~0.5 A typical) — turn off when unused.


RECHARGING THE BATTERY (NO CAR)

1. **Mains charger** (if grid sometimes available): automatic “smart” charger to +/−; ~6–10 A → full charge many hours; follow manual.

2. **Solar**: panel (e.g. 100 W) → **MPPT** (or appropriate controller for your chemistry) → battery. Sunny day effective hours → partial to full charge depending on panel/controller. See `8.4_solar_panels.txt`.

3. **Generator AC**: generator → same mains charger → battery.

4. **Parallel with charged battery** (jumpers): charged + to depleted +, charged − to depleted −; ~30–60 min transfers some energy — not a full charge; prefer proper charger.

**Do not:** tip battery (acid spill); charge in sealed unventilated space (hydrogen); reverse polarity; charge frozen battery — warm first.


HOW LONG WILL IT LAST

Rough usable energy ≈ **Ah × 0.5 × 12 V** on a starter battery you care about.

Example **60 Ah** → ~**360 Wh** usable:

Load                    | Power   | Order of runtime
Laptop (light use)      | 30–45 W | many hours
Laptop (charging brick) | ~65 W   | ~5–6 h
Phone charge ~15 W      | 15 W    | many phone charges
20 Ah power bank equiv. | ~74 Wh  | ~4 full charges of that class
LED 10 W                | 10 W    | ~36 h
Radio receive           | 5–8 W   | long receive-only

Rule: **~12.0 V** under your normal loads → stop deep discharge; recharge.


MINIMUM KIT TO BUY AHEAD

Basic (phone + light):
— 12 V → USB module (2–4 ports).
— Two ~2.5 mm² leads ~1.5 m with clamps.
— 7 A fuse + holder.

Extended (laptop):
— 150–300 W pure sine inverter.
— Two ~6 mm² leads (often included).
— ~25 A fuse + holder.
— Multimeter for voltage checks.

Full autonomous:
— Above + ~100 W panel + MPPT controller.
— Second deep-cycle/AGM battery dedicated to house loads if budget allows.
