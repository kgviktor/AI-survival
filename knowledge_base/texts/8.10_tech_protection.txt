CHAPTER 8. ENERGY AND ELECTRONICS
8.10 Protecting gear: moisture, shock, storage

MAIN ENEMIES OF ELECTRONICS

Moisture → corrosion, shorts, board damage.
Shock and vibration → mechanical failure.
Dust → overheating, shorts, clogged mechanics.
Extreme temperatures → battery degradation, cracks, parameter drift.
Static electricity → instant damage to sensitive ICs.
Overvoltage → component breakdown.


MOISTURE PROTECTION

Storage:
— Sealed cases (Pelican-style, metal Zarges-type, or any plastic box with silicone gasket).
— Zip bags: cheap, adequate for many devices.
— Vacuum bags: better for long storage.
— Desiccant (silica gel) in the container with gear. Indicating silica: blue = dry, pink = saturated (regenerate: bake ~120 °C ~1 h).

Carry:
— Dry bags for pack in rain/fords.
— Phone dry pouches: fully waterproof.
— In pack: electronics high in bag (rain pools low), inside bags.

If wet:
1. Power off immediately (do not try to turn on).
2. Remove battery if possible.
3. Do not shake — drives water deeper.
4. Wipe exterior; remove visible liquid.
5. Rice or silica container 48–72 h (draws moisture).
6. Only after fully dry — power on and test.
7. Optional: hair dryer on lowest heat (overheat risk).

Corrosion:
— Green/white on contacts: copper oxide and salts.
— Clean: cotton swab + isopropyl alcohol (70–99%).
— Soft brush + alcohol in tight spots.
— After cleaning: dry fully; conformal coating on boards (not connectors).


SHOCK PROTECTION

Cases:
— Phone: rugged case (hard shell + rubber).
— Laptop: sleeve + hard shell.
— Tablet: frame case.
— Multimeter: rubber boot.

DIY padding:
— Bubble wrap: several layers.
— Foam: cut to shape.
— Plastic box + foam = solid transit pack.

After drop:
— Do not rush to power on.
— Inspect: cracks, broken parts.
— Cracked screen may still work if backlight/touch OK.
— Avoid opening unless needed — dust and moisture enter.


DUST PROTECTION

Dust is worst for:
— HDDs: particles on platters → errors.
— Fans: clog, lose cooling.
— Connectors: poor contact.

Measures:
— Closed storage.
— Dust caps on unused ports (USB, audio).
— Blow/clean vents every 1–3 months.
— Compressed air or cold blow-dryer.


TEMPERATURE

Cold (below 0 °C):
— Li-ion loses ~20–40% capacity around −10 °C.
— Very low temps: LCD can freeze/crack.
— Carry gear against body (inner pocket) for battery warmth.
— Do not power frozen gear immediately — warm to room temp first.

Heat (above 40–50 °C):
— Li-ion ages fast; RAM can glitch.
— Never leave gear in direct sun (car interior summer can exceed 80 °C).
— Do not block vents in use.

Condensation:
— Cold → warm transition: moisture on boards.
— Let gear sit powered off 30–60 min before switching on.


STATIC (ESD)

Static kills ICs instantly.

Risky when:
— Working on bare boards/components.
— Dry weather + synthetic clothing.

Protection:
— ESD wrist strap to ground (earthed metal).
— Without strap: periodically touch grounded metal (radiator, PC chassis, metal pipe).
— ESD mat or work on antistatic bags.
— Store ICs/boards in silver antistatic bags.


OVERVOLTAGE PROTECTION

Sources:
— Unregulated generator.
— Solar without proper controller.
— Lightning induction on lines.
— Poor inverter output.

Mitigation:
— 230 V stabilizer before sensitive loads.
— TVS diodes on 12 V inputs.
— MOVs for surge absorption.
— UPS with line conditioning for laptops/serious gear.

Lightning:
— Storm: disconnect from mains and antennas.
— Ground antenna mast with proper arrestor to earth.


LONG-TERM STORAGE (6–12+ months)

Li-ion:
1. Charge to ~40–60% (not empty).
2. Zip bag + desiccant.
3. Rigid container.
4. Cool (10–15 °C), dark, dry.
5. Every ~6 months: check SoC, top to ~50% if low.

Lead-acid:
— Store charged; float/top every 3–6 months.
— Do not freeze deeply discharged battery (~−10 °C risk).

NiMH:
— Any state OK; after long storage do full charge/discharge cycle before critical use.
