CHAPTER 8. ENERGY AND ELECTRONICS
8.6 DIY generators: principles and simple builds

ELECTROMAGNETIC INDUCTION

Moving conductor in magnetic field → EMF (Faraday).

Parts:
1. Prime mover (pedals, wind, water, engine).
2. Magnets or field winding.
3. Coils.

Rotating field past stator coils → AC; diode bridge → DC.


DC MOTOR AS GENERATOR

Spin the shaft → voltage at terminals.

Salvage motors from tools, printers, wipers, treadmills.
Higher rated motor voltage often means higher generated V per RPM.

Test: multimeter on motor terminals while spinning — any volts means it works.

Simple bike rig:
1. Bike on stand; roller on tire drives motor shaft.
2. Motor → bridge rectifier → battery (with charge control!).
3. Voltmeter watch; do not overcharge.

Roller diameter vs wheel sets step-up — small roller on large wheel = fast motor RPM.


WIND FROM CAR ALTERNATOR

Alternator + blades + tower + yaw tail.
Many alternators need rotor excitation (~12 V via lamp resistor to field) before self-sustaining.

PVC pipe blades — balance equally; furling tail or brake shorts to stop in storms — safety first.

Cut-in wind often ~3–5 m/s; useful ~5–10 m/s.


WATER WHEEL

Overshot most efficient (~70–85% theoretical); undershot simpler, lower η.

Slow wheel (~5–30 RPM) → belt/pulley step-up to generator RPM (~500–1500+).


PELTIER / THERMOELECTRIC

Seebeck: ΔT across module → small DC.

Hot side to stove/pipe; cold side heat sink + cooling.
Watts per module small; fragile — no flex impact.

Commercial camp stoves with TEG exist — phone trickle charge.


GENERAL BUILD TIPS

Bridge rectifier: 4 diodes — size for current (e.g. 1N540x ~3 A, higher for big builds).
Filter cap ~1000–4700 µF on DC bus (polarity!).
Buck/boost modules for 5 V USB or 12 V regulation.
Charge disconnect ~14.4 V relay or proper controller on lead-acid.

Safety:
— Insulate all connections.
— Anti-backfeed diode to battery when generator stops.
— Fuse at battery +.
