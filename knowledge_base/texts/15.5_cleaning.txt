CHAPTER 15. FIREARMS: HANDLING BASICS
15.5 Cleaning and basic field maintenance

WHY CLEAN

**Fouling:**
— Builds in chamber, bore, bolt group.
— Promotes **rust** in humidity.
— Slows the action → **stoppages.**
— Severe corrosion can **freeze** a case in the chamber.

**No / wrong lube:**
— Metal-on-metal wear; sluggish parts.
— In cold, wrong grease can **gum** the action.

**When to clean:**
— After shooting, as soon as practical.
— Every **1–2 weeks** in storage in damp environments.
— After rain, mud, dust — **immediately.**
— Guns “not fired” still rust if neglected.


MINIMUM KIT

— **Rod** with jag/slotted tip for patches.
— **Bore brush** (bronze/nylon) for caliber.
— **Patches / soft cloth.**
— **Gun oil** (or field substitutes below).
— **Solvent** for carbon (or substitute).

**Field substitutes (emergency):**
— Oil: light **engine oil** (thin), **vegetable oil** short-term, some use **petroleum jelly** very sparingly on externals — **not** ideal in cold.
— Solvent: **kerosene**, **alcohol**, **acetone** (care — finishes/plastics).
— Rod: wooden dowel, wire with patch — **do not scratch crown.**

**Avoid:**
— Pouring water into the action.
— **Heavy oil in chamber/bore** before firing — can cause pressure issues and **misfires.**
— **Abrasive** on polished surfaces or rifling.


BORE

1. **Verify unloaded.**
2. Wet patch with solvent.
3. Brush **chamber to muzzle** several strokes (direction debates exist; **avoid** damaging muzzle crown).
4. Dry patches until **light gray** is acceptable; **black** = keep cleaning.
5. Light **oiled** patch for **storage** only — run **dry patch** before **firing** if oil was left in bore.

**Rust in bore:** orange on patches, rough brushing — light rust may clean out; **heavy pitting** ruins accuracy — **gunsmith.**


BOLT GROUP / RECEIVER

1. Field-strip per **15.3** / manual.
2. Wipe carbon from bolt, carrier, **firing pin channel** (carbon here → light strikes / misfires).
3. Clean **extractor** hook with pick/patch.
4. Receiver rails / raceways — carbon wipes off.
5. Dry solvent residue.
6. **Thin** lube on **wear surfaces** — **not** in firing pin channel (many guns want that **dry** or minimal).


SHOTGUN

Bore as above; **chamber** heavily fouled with plastic wad residue — brush.
Pump: wipe **bolt, rails, forend** tracks; light lube on friction points.
Break-action: hinges, extractors — thin oil.


LUBRICATION — HOW MUCH

**Rule:** **thin film**, not puddles.

**AK-style rails, carrier, cam surfaces:** light oil.
**Pistol rails / barrel hood contact:** light oil.
**Recoil spring:** light.

**Do not** lube:
— Inside bore right before shooting (clean bore).
— **Magazine interior** (attracts grit).
— Drenching **ammo.**


COLD WEATHER (~**−15 °C** and below)

Standard oils thicken → sluggish actions.
— Clean off heavy lubes; use **cold-weather** gun lube or **very light** oil.
— Some users run **dry** (clean, minimal lube) in extreme cold — **test your system safely on a range.**


AFTER CLEANING

— Light oil film on **metal** for storage.
— **Avoid long-term** storage in **leather** cases (holds moisture) — prefer breathable synthetic/cotton.
— In humidity: inspect **weekly.**


FREQUENCY

— After range day: **same day or next.**
— Dry climate storage: **~monthly** wipe.
— Wet climate: **weekly** check.
— Rust can start in **hours** in bad conditions.
