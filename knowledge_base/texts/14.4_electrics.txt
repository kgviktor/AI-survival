CHAPTER 14. VEHICLE: SERVICE AND REPAIR
14.4 Electrics: battery, jump start, fuses, wiring

BATTERY

Starts engine; runs accessories engine-off; buffers charging spikes.

**Ah** capacity; **CCA/EN** cold cranking — critical in winter.

Healthy resting ~12.6 V; ~12.0 V ≈ half; &lt;11.8 V very low.

Running ~13.8–14.5 V at fast idle — if lower, alternator or belt issue.

**Terminals:** clean green/white corrosion; wire brush; grease/petroleum jelly.

**Serviceable batteries:** distilled water to plates if low.

**Charge:** smart charger ~0.1C (60 Ah → ~6 A). Cold battery — warm above **0 °C** before fast charge when possible.

**Winter:** capacity drops hard at −20 °C; block heater; battery blanket; smaller discharge before start.


JUMP START

Heavy cables **16–25 mm²**, short as practical.

**Order:** + donor to + dead; − donor to **engine block or bare metal** on dead car (away from battery gas).

Start donor; wait; crank dead.

**Remove black from dead first** — reverse order of install.

**Wrong polarity** can destroy electronics.

After start: run **20–30+ min** or use charger.

**Portable jump pack** — keep charged; check quarterly.


ALTERNATOR

Low voltage running; battery light; dead battery often — test belt tension first, then regulator/brushes repair on some units.


FUSES

Blown = open element. **Replace same amperage** — never oversized.

If repeats — find short, don’t keep feeding fuses.

**Spare kit** 5–30 A blade types in glove box.


WIRING REPAIR

Trace circuit; twist + tape **temporary**; solder + heat-shrink better.

**Bad grounds** cause ghosts — clean chassis/engine strap bolts.


ECU / IMMOBILIZER

Modern ECU failure usually needs pro tools.

**EMP note:** older carb/distributor cars simpler; modern ECU in metal box has some shielding — not a guarantee.

Immobilizer lost key — **legal** locksmith/dealer path only in peacetime; know your vehicle’s reality.


VEHICLE ELECTRIC KIT

Jumper cables 3 m, ≥25 mm²; jump pack; fuse assortment; tape; spare wire; multimeter; smart charger; common bulbs.
