CHAPTER 8. ENERGY AND ELECTRONICS
8.4 Solar panels: sourcing, wiring, maintenance

HOW A PANEL WORKS

Photons knock electrons loose in silicon → DC current.

Key specs:
— Pmax (Wp): under STC (1000 W/m², 25°C cell).
— Voc: open-circuit voltage (~18–22 V for “12 V” nom. panels).
— Vmp, Imp: peak power point values.
— Isc: short-circuit current.

Real daily yield (very variable):
— Strong sun hours ~3–5/day summer mid-latitudes; ~1–2 h winter north.
— 100 W panel might average ~300–500 Wh/day summer clear sky; near zero deep winter north / heavy cloud.


WHERE TO FIND PANELS

Roof installs on homes/cabins; garden/ag shops (~5–50 W kits); building supply; telecom repeater sites (ethics/law apply); electronics retailers.

Quick test:
1. Full sun, multimeter on terminals: Voc ~18–22 V for 36-cell “12 V” panel.
2. Current into ~known load or approximate Isc measurement — caution: heat in short.


NEVER PANEL DIRECT TO BATTERY

Overcharge and nighttime back-drain — always use a charge controller.

PWM controller:
— Simple/cheap; holds battery at charge voltage; wastes some panel voltage headroom.

MPPT:
— Tracks maximum power point — often ~15–30% more energy than PWM in suboptimal conditions; supports higher string Voc feeding 12 V bank.

Typical wiring:
PV+ → controller PV+ → controller BAT+ → battery+
PV− → controller PV− → controller BAT− → battery−
Optional LOAD terminals on some controllers — obey manual low-voltage disconnect rules.

Fusing:
— Panel to controller: ~125% Isc.
— Battery to controller: at battery, rated ≥ max controller charge current.


CONTROLLER SIZING

Controller amp rating ≥ total Isc × ~1.25 (parallel panels add Isc).


PARALLEL / SERIES

Series: voltage adds — needs MPPT tolerant of higher Voc; watch cold Voc limits.

Parallel: same voltage, I adds — parallel combiner with fuses per string; blocking diodes optional against shade back-feed (many panels have bypass diodes internal).


PLACEMENT

Tilt ≈ latitude is a starting rule (adjust steeper for winter sun height).
Face equator (south in northern hemisphere); ±30° azimuth often acceptable.

Shade kills output — one bad cell can drag a string — avoid tree/chimney shadow.

Low profile or hide panels in crisis if signature matters — balcony parapet, take in after charging.


MAINTENANCE

Wash dust — can cost 10–30% output.
Snow removal in winter.
Check connectors for corrosion.

Storage:
— Indoors; cover opaque — no light = negligible current.

Repair:
— Cracked glass may still work — seal edges; fix broken junction boxes.
