CHAPTER 4. HEAT, WARMTH, AND FIRE
4.8 Ventilation and carbon monoxide safety

CARBON MONOXIDE — INVISIBLE KILLER

Carbon monoxide (CO) is a leading cause of death when people use combustion indoors. No color, no smell, no taste. Poisoning feels like tiredness or flu at first — then unconsciousness and death.

Indoor CO sources:
— Any open flame (wood, coal, gas, kerosene)
— Gasoline/diesel generators
— Car engines
— Charcoal grills, hibachi
— Unvented gas heaters

Concentrations and effects (approximate):
— ~35 ppm: headache with long exposure (residential limits often ~9 ppm or lower)
— ~200 ppm: headache, nausea in ~2–3 h
— ~400 ppm: life-threatening in ~3 h
— ~800 ppm: unconscious in ~45 min
— ~1600 ppm: death in ~1 h
— ~6400 ppm: death in ~15 min

A small wood stove in a sealed ~15 m² room without airflow can reach lethal CO in ~2–4 h.


SYMPTOMS OF CO POISONING

Early (~35–200 ppm):
Headache (often frontal), mild dizziness, nausea, fatigue — often mistaken for cold or overwork.

Moderate (~200–800 ppm):
Severe headache, vomiting, confusion, sleepiness, fast pulse.

Severe (~800+ ppm):
Unconsciousness, seizures, respiratory failure, death.

Key clue:
Several people in same space sick at once — think CO, not “flu.” Flu rarely hits everyone simultaneously.

If CO suspected:
1. Move victim to fresh air immediately.
2. Lie down, loosen clothing.
3. If not breathing — rescue breathing / CPR per training.
4. Oxygen if available (speeds CO clearance).
5. Rest, warmth; no smoking, alcohol, exertion.
6. Even “mild” cases — hours of fresh air.

Severe cases need hospital care; hyperbaric oxygen is definitive treatment in many protocols.


VENTILATION PRINCIPLES

Minimum rule:
Any combustion indoors needs continuous fresh air intake. No intake = incomplete combustion = CO. Ventilation must be steady, not “open window sometimes.”

Basic pattern:
Low intake (floor level) for cool air; high exhaust (ceiling or chimney) for hot gas. CO molar mass ~28, air ~29 — CO mixes throughout the room; high and low vent both help.

Chimney is primary protection:
Proper flue carries products outside. Draft from temperature difference. Test draft: flame or smoke pulled toward flue opening. Flue should exit above roof line where wind will not push exhaust back indoors.

Without chimney:
Open window or door crack near heat source. Rough guide: ~200–400 cm² total vent area minimum for small room with small flame — more is safer. Some ventilation beats none.

Window slot chimney:
Metal pipe (e.g. stacked cans) through window gap — improvised exhaust.


CO DETECTION

Simple biological warning:
Small caged bird or rodent — more sensitive than humans in some conditions. Historical mine “canary” concept — they fail first.

Chemical indicators:
Special CO indicator materials exist — not DIY-friendly.

Electronic CO alarms:
If batteries or power available — best protection. Inexpensive; replace per manufacturer dates.

Relative risk by fuel (high to low, very general):
1. Bituminous coal — very high indoors without flue
2. Wood without chimney — high
3. Gas — moderate
4. Wood with good chimney — low if draft maintained
5. Alcohol — lower
6. Candles — lowest (not zero in sealed space)


VENTILATION BY HEAT SOURCE

Wood stove with chimney:
Relatively safe if draft is good. Stop burning if flue blocks or backdrafts.

Open fire indoors without flue:
Extremely dangerous. If forced: large permanent opening to outside and strong cross-ventilation — still marginal.

Gas camp stove:
CO₂ and water vapor; some CO. Brief use in ~20+ m² with window cracked may be tolerable for cooking — not for heating sealed rooms.

Generators:
Never indoors, garage, or enclosed stairwell. Outside, exhaust away from windows/doors — minimum ~5 m often cited; more is better.

Charcoal / BBQ coals:
Never indoors for heat — common fatal mistake.


NIGHT SAFETY

Sleeping while an unvented heater runs is highest risk — you will not feel warning signs.

Night rules:
— Stove with chimney: generally OK if maintained.
— No chimney: extinguish before sleep. Better cold than dead.
— Fire watch in shifts (~4 h) if you must have open flame.
— Buddy line from sleepers to watch.

If no watch and no chimney:
Insulate body heavily; let fire die. Cold is uncomfortable; CO is lethal.
