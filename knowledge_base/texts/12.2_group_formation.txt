CHAPTER 12. GROUP AND SOCIAL SURVIVAL
12.2 Forming a group: who to bring, who to avoid

WHY A GROUP BEATS SOLO

Long crisis solo is nearly a dead end:
— You cannot sleep and guard at once.
— Illness or injury can finish you alone.
— One skill set, one pair of eyes.
— Isolation drains morale fast.

Rough size:
— Minimum ~4–6 for watches and mutual aid.
— Sweet spot ~8–15 for skills mix and manageability.
— First-phase cap ~20–25 before logistics/leadership strain.
— 30+ needs village-level formal structure.


HIGH-VALUE SKILLS

Medicine (top tier):
— Any physician; surgeon especially.
— Nurse, paramedic, EMT.
— Veterinarian — anatomy, procedures.
— Pharmacist — drugs and substitutes.

Trades:
— Electrician, plumber, mechanic, carpenter/builder.

Food:
— Farmer, gardener, experienced hunter or angler.

Security:
— Military/police **with** ethical judgment and de-escalation — not “tacticool” alone.

Soft power:
— Teacher if children present.
— Calm, respected mediator.
— People bringing major stores or tools.
— Local knowledge (terrain, people, water).

Any healthy adult willing to work and follow fair rules has baseline value.


GREEN FLAGS

— Steady under stress or recovers quickly.
— Concrete useful skills.
— Works and shares fairly.
— Avoids needless drama.
— Accepts group decisions.
— Realistic about the situation.


YELLOW FLAGS — PROCEED CAREFULLY

Large dependent sets:
— Many young kids or high-care elders — more mouths, fewer hands.
— Weigh their skills and assets vs load.

Bad history:
— Chronic agreement-breakers; known theft or violence toward peers.

Unknowns:
— Trial period before trust (`12.5_trust_verification.txt`).
— No immediate access to armory, full stores, or full defensive plan.


RED FLAGS — HIGH RISK

Manipulators / freeloaders:
— Big promises, no delivery; manufactured “emergencies”; pit members against each other.

Destabilizers:
— Block every plan without alternatives; spread panic on purpose; undermine leaders.

Hard egoists:
— Won’t share but demands protection; hides group resources; “lone wolf” plus “feed me.”

Unstable behavior:
— Random violence; intoxication in active crisis; acute psychiatric crisis you cannot support.

**Reality:** perfect candidates are rare. Sometimes you take who is there — but **know the signs** to set boundaries early.


ONBOARDING PROCEDURE

1. Meet **off-site** — do not show full stocks first contact.
2. Interview: background, skills, recent story.
3. Trial ~24–72 h joint labor.
4. Group discussion.
5. Rules brief: expectations, duties, consequences.
6. Full membership only after trial.

State aloud:
— Work share by ability.
— Food: common pot and/or clear personal rules.
— Watches mandatory.
— No internal theft or assault.
— Decision process: vote vs delegated leader.


SIZE VS RESOURCES

Rule of thumb per added adult:
— ~+1 L water/day, ~+500–700 kcal/day (adjust for labor and body size).

**Combat load** ideal:
— ~80% able adults, ~20% dependents.
— 50%+ dependents without offsetting assets → group drains fast.

Each new member should **bring** resources or **earn** them quickly in the field.
