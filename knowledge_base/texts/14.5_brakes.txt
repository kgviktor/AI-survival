CHAPTER 14. VEHICLE: SERVICE AND REPAIR
14.5 Brakes: inspection, pad replacement, bleeding

BRAKE SYSTEM — BASICS

Principle: brake pedal → master cylinder → hydraulic pressure → calipers/wheel cylinders → pads/shoes clamp disc/drum → deceleration.

Components:
— Brake fluid (DOT 3 / 4 / 5.1).
— Master cylinder (under hood, reservoir on top).
— Hoses and hard lines.
— Calipers with pistons (disc) or wheel cylinders (drum).
— Discs or drums.
— Pads or shoes.

Front brakes (usually disc): do **~70–80%** of stopping; wear faster.
Rears: disc or drum; less loaded.


BRAKE FLUID

**DOT ratings (approximate dry / wet boil points):**
— DOT 3: ~205 °C / ~140 °C after moisture uptake.
— DOT 4: ~230 °C / ~155 °C — very common.
— DOT 5.1: ~260 °C / ~180 °C — harder use.
— **DOT 5 (silicone):** **do not mix** with glycol types — special applications only.

**DOT 3, 4, 5.1** are glycol-based — topping up across these may be acceptable in emergency — **follow manufacturer**.

Fluid **absorbs water**:
— In humid climates, boil point falls over **1–2 years**.
— Old fluid can boil under hard braking → vapor in lines → **long pedal**.
— Replace **~every 2–3 years** or per manual.

**Reservoir level:**
— Between MIN and MAX.
— **Low** — leak **or** worn pads (pistons extended).


INSPECTION

Through wheel spokes (disc):
— **Minimum friction material ~3–4 mm** — below that, replace.
— Many pads have squealers — metal noise when worn.

**Discs:**
— Light even wear OK.
— Deep grooves **&gt;~1–1.5 mm** — machine or replace.
— Minimum thickness often stamped on hat — measure with caliper.
— Surface rust after parking — normal; clears after a few stops.

**On the road:**
— Straight stop without pull.
— No pedal pulse (unless ABS self-test).
— Squeal only first stops after rain/rust OK.
— Pedal firm, not spongy or sinking to floor.


DISC PAD REPLACEMENT

Tools: jack, stands, lug wrench, caliper bolts (often 7–14 mm), piston retract tool or careful lever with old pad, brake grease **on metal slides only**.

Typical front disc job:
1. Lift; secure; remove wheel.
2. Remove caliper bracket bolts (layout varies) — **don’t hang caliper on flex hose** — wire to spring/strut.
3. Remove old pads from bracket.
4. Retract piston (watch reservoir overflow — loosen cap slightly if needed).
5. Lube **slide pins and pad ears** per service info — **never grease friction material**.
6. Install new pads/hardware.
7. Reinstall caliper/torque bolts.
8. Pump pedal until firm.
9. Check fluid level.

**First 200–500 km** — easy stops while pads bed.

**Drum (many rears, older cars):**
— Remove drum; inspect shoes, springs, wheel cylinder for leaks.
— Replace shoes; adjust; bleed if cylinder opened.


BLEEDING

**Why:** remove air — air compresses → spongy pedal.

**When:** fluid change; any opened line, caliper, cylinder; soft pedal with good level.

**Order:** usually **farthest from master** — RR → LR → RF → LF — **verify your manual**.

**Two-person:**
1. Reservoir full.
2. Clear hose on bleeder, jar end submerged in clean fluid.
3. Helper pumps **3–4×**, holds pedal down.
4. Crack bleeder **¼–½** turn; watch bubbles.
5. Close bleeder before pedal release.
6. Repeat until clear; **never run reservoir dry**.

**Solo:** pressure bleeder or one-way bleed bottle / check-valve hose.


PARKING BRAKE

Weak hold:
— Cable stretch — adjust at equalizer/rears.
— Worn rear shoes/pads.
— Frozen in cold.

**Frozen parking brake:**
— In hard frost, pulled shoes can ice to drums — gentle warm-up; avoid yanking.
— On manual trans, **gear + chocks** sometimes safer than relying on handbrake alone in extreme cold.


EMERGENCY STOPPING

**No ABS:** cadence braking — near lock, ease off, reapply.

**ABS:** **full pressure**, hold — pedal may pulse — **keep steering**.

**Brake failure moving:**
1. Downshift — engine braking.
2. Parking brake — **gradual** pull (rear lock can spin vehicle).
3. Use terrain — brush, gravel berm — not head-on unless no choice.
4. Horn/lights — warn others.
