CHAPTER 5. MEDICINE AND FIRST AID
5.4 Infectious illness: symptoms and care without a doctor

FIELD DIAGNOSIS BASICS

Without labs or X-ray, diagnosis is clinical: symptoms + timeline + context (food, water, contacts, travel). Treatment is often empirical — “most likely X, treat as X.”

Key history questions:
— Onset and progression?
— Fever height?
— Diarrhea, vomiting, cough, rash?
— Food and water last 48 h?
— Sick contacts?
— Insect/animal bites?

Red flags (aggressive care / evacuate if possible):
— Fever >39.5°C in adult.
— Altered consciousness.
— Respiratory rate >30/min.
— Pulse >120 or <50.
— Very low urine output, dark urine.
— Bleeding from unusual sites.
— Rash with petechiae/purpura.


GASTROINTESTINAL INFECTIONS

Common when sanitation fails.

Acute gastroenteritis / foodborne illness:
Onset: vomiting ± diarrhea ~2–12 h after meal; fever often low.
Causes: Staph (2–6 h), Salmonella (6–48 h), Campylobacter, E. coli, etc.
Care:
— Rehydration is #1 — small frequent sips.
— Activated charcoal 1–2 g q3–4h (limited evidence, traditional use).
— Loperamide if severe non-bloody diarrhea only.
— If severe: ciprofloxacin 500 mg ×2/day ×3–5 days (resistance varies by region).
— Rest, bland diet.

Dysentery (bloody mucoid stool):
— Bloody diarrhea + cramps, tenesmus, fever ~38–39°C.
— Ciprofloxacin 500 mg ×2/day ×3–5 days + rehydration.
— Do NOT use loperamide with bloody diarrhea.

Cholera (sanitation collapse settings):
— Profuse “rice-water” diarrhea, rapid dehydration.
— Massive ORS volumes — may need many liters per day.
— Doxycycline single 300 mg dose or 100 mg ×2/day ×3 days (local protocols vary).
— Without fluids, mortality is high.

Typhoid fever:
— Stepwise rising fever to ~40°C over days, relative bradycardia sometimes, rose spots on abdomen, often constipation > diarrhea early.
— Ciprofloxacin 500 mg ×2/day ×14 days or azithromycin per protocol (resistance patterns differ globally).

Giardiasis:
— Foul greasy chronic diarrhea, gas, bloating — bad water.
— Metronidazole 500 mg ×3/day ×7 days.

Amebiasis:
— Chronic bloody diarrhea, RUQ pain (liver abscess risk).
— Metronidazole 750 mg ×3/day ×10 days — then luminal agent in full care.


RESPIRATORY INFECTIONS

Common cold (URI):
— Runny nose, sore throat, cough, low fever — usually viral.
— Supportive: fluids, rest, acetaminophen if fever >38.5°C. No antibiotics for uncomplicated viral illness.

Strep throat (bacterial):
— Severe sore throat, exudate on tonsils, fever ~38.5–40°C, tender anterior neck nodes.
— Amoxicillin 500 mg ×3/day ×10 days + analgesia.

Pneumonia:
— Productive cough (yellow/green/rust), fever >38.5°C, pleuritic pain, tachypnea >25/min.
— Amoxicillin/clavulanate 875 mg ×2/day OR azithromycin 500 mg daily ×7–10 days (severity and local resistance guide choice).
— Semi-sitting, analgesia if pleuritic pain.

Bronchitis:
— Cough with sputum, mild fever — usually viral.
— Antibiotics only if severe or high-risk host.


FEVER AND SYSTEMIC ILLNESS

Malaria (endemic tropics):
— Cyclic chills → high fever → sweats every ~48–72 h by species. Headache, myalgia, anemia.
— Treatment depends on species and resistance — chloroquine only if known sensitive; artemisinin-based combinations where indicated.

Sepsis pattern:
— Fever >38°C or <36°C + HR >90 + RR >20/min + confusion. Source: wound, lung, gut.
— Field (desperate): broad coverage e.g. cipro + metronidazole + amoxicillin/clavulanate is an example pattern — must match training and stock; fluids aggressively.

Meningitis suspicion:
— Severe headache + neck stiffness + photophobia + fever ± petechial rash (meningococcal).
— Field: cipro + amoxicillin/clavulanate as desperate bridge — mortality high without hospital; isolate respiratory droplets.


PARASITES AND VECTOR-BORNE

Lyme disease (Borrelia — endemic regions):
— Expanding erythema migrans rash, later systemic symptoms.
— Doxycycline 100 mg ×2/day ×14–21 days (age/pregnancy restrictions apply).

Tick-borne encephalitis (TBEV areas):
— Biphasic: flu-like then neuro — supportive only; prevention is vaccine and tick avoidance.

Trichinellosis:
— Undercooked wild pig/bear meat — diarrhea then muscle pain, facial edema, fever.
— Mebendazole 200–400 mg ×3/day ×3 days + steroids if severe — hospital preferred.

Roundworm / pinworm:
— Fecal-oral hygiene failure; perianal itch (pinworm).
— Mebendazole 100 mg once, repeat in 2 weeks OR albendazole 400 mg once.


EMPIRIC SCHEMA WHEN UNKNOWN

GI symptoms (diarrhea, vomiting, abdominal pain):
→ Rehydration + charcoal. If severe — cipro + metronidazole.

Fever + sore throat/cough:
→ Purulent strep pattern — amoxicillin.
→ Pneumonia pattern — amoxicillin/clavulanate or azithromycin.
→ Simple URI — supportive only.

Fever + wound:
→ Wound care + cipro + metronidazole.

Fever + neuro signs:
→ Meningitis suspicion — immediate antibiotics if no hospital + urgent evacuation.


ISOLATION IN GROUPS

For contagious diarrhea, rash, bleeding:
— Separate sleep and eating utensils.
— Hand hygiene after contact.
— One designated caregiver when possible.
— Sanitize toilet after use.
— Boil bedding when feasible.
