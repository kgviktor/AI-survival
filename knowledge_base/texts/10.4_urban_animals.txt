CHAPTER 10. FOOD PRODUCTION
10.4 Poultry, fish, and small livestock in urban settings

WHY ANIMAL PROTEIN

Plants supply carbs and some protein, but animal foods:
— Complete essential amino acids.
— Vitamin B12 (animal sources).
— Omega-3 from fish.
— Eggs/dairy (where legal and possible) without slaughter each day.

In crises lasting **3+ months**, animal protein deficit becomes serious.


CHICKENS IN THE CITY

Why chickens:
— Hardy; layers ~150–300 eggs/hen/year (breed dependent).
— Hens are relatively quiet (**roosters** anger neighbors and may be banned).
— Eat scraps, grain, greens, insects.
— A few hens can cover household eggs.

Where (check **local laws**):
— Enclosed balcony — very small flock only.
— Courtyard, back lot.
— Lit basement — poor air, last resort.
— Roof pen — must be fenced; fall and predator proof.

Minimum housing:
— ~0.5 m² per bird indoors + run if possible.
— Roost: perch ~10–15 cm wide, ~50–80 cm high.
— Nest box ~30×30×30 cm, bedding.
— Ventilation: ammonia from manure kills productivity.
— Cold: below ~−10 °C extended — insulate or move birds.

Feed:
— Grain mix (wheat, barley, corn) as base.
— Vegetable scraps, bread, boiled peelings.
— Free-range insects if safe to forage.
— Grit + crushed shell for digestion and strong eggshells.
— Fresh water always.
— Rough guide ~100–150 g grain/hen/day.

Breeds for small flocks:
— Leghorn-type: high egg count.
— Brown-egg hybrids: calm, productive.
— Dual-purpose: meat + eggs when culled.
— Bantams: less feed/space; smaller eggs.

Manure:
— Excellent fertilizer — compost or dry-store.
— Manage odor: weekly litter change or deep litter with dry carbon.


RABBITS

Pros:
— Fast meat gain (~3 months to table on good feed).
— Eat grass, leaves, veg scraps, hay.
— Quiet.
— Hide + fur byproducts.
— High reproduction if managed.

Housing:
— Cage minimum ~0.5 × 1.0 m per adult; wire floor or frequent litter.
— Hide box inside.
— Heat stress >~30 °C; avoid drafts + rain.

Feed:
— Hay ad lib.
— Forage (dandelion, plantain, nettle — **no toxic plants**).
— ~50–80 g grain/day.
— Winter: tree bark (birch, willow, fruit woods) as supplement.
— Water always.

Harvest (graphic reality of meat):
— Typical age ~3–4 months for fryers.
— Humane kill: approved method for your jurisdiction (mechanical or captive bolt per local rules); skin “sock” style; meat is lean, mild.


PIGEONS

— Feral city pigeons: **trapping laws vary**; some places prohibit.
— Domestic pairs can raise squabs several rounds/year.
— Feed: grain, bread scraps.
— Small meat yield but real protein.

Do not house rabbits and poultry together — disease and stress.


FISH IN TANKS / TUBS

Why fish:
— Fast growth if fed; quiet; discreet; quality protein.

Carp / carp-class cyprinids:
— Tough; tolerate low DO and temperature swings.
— Season growth ~200–500 g possible with feeding.
— Without aeration: stock lightly — ~5–10 kg fish per m³ **very** approximate; watch gasping at surface.

Tilapia:
— Needs ~22–30 °C water — tropical; fast growth ~500 g in ~6 months on good feed.
— Omnivorous.

Aquaponics (fish + plants):
— Fish waste fertilizes plants; plants strip nitrogen — recirculating loop.
— Smallest loop: fish tank + grow bed above; water lifted by hand or small pump if power exists.

No electricity options:
— Fish low, grow bed high; hand pour/siphon ~1–2×/day.
— Aeration: cascade pour from height ~30–50 cm entrains air; or wind-treadle paddle; or brief manual bellows/pump morning and evening.

Fish feed:
— Commercial pellets — best to stock.
— Worms from compost (`10.5_compost.txt`).
— Insects, duckweed if you can grow it.
— Tilapia eats some green waste.

Health:
— Gasping at surface → low oxygen or ammonia.
— White spots → ich — improve water, reduce density; salt ~1–3 g/L sometimes helps as bridge measure — research species tolerance.


WORMS (VERMICULTURE)

Not food directly but:
— Castings = top fertilizer.
— Protein snack for poultry/fish.
— Fishing bait.

Bin ~60 × 40 × 30 cm, vent + drain holes.
Bedding: damp shredded paper/cardboard + small amount of soil.
Worms: red wigglers / Eisenia species preferred.
Feed: veg/fruit scraps, coffee grounds, eggshells. **Avoid** meat, dairy, oily food, onion/citrus heavy loads.
Moisture: like a wrung sponge; temp ~15–25 °C.
Harvest castings in ~3–6 months from bottom tray or migration harvest.
