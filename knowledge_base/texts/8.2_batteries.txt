CHAPTER 8. ENERGY AND ELECTRONICS
8.2 Batteries: types, charging, storage, recovery

BATTERY TYPES

Lead-acid (flooded automotive):
— 12 V (6 × 2 V cells); ~40–100+ Ah common.
— High cranking current; **not** deep-cycle rated — avoid deep discharge.
— Life ~3–5 years with care.

AGM:
— Sealed; less gas in normal charge; more cycle life than basic flooded.
— UPS, solar, marine use.

GEL:
— Gel electrolyte; good deep-cycle tolerance; costly.

Li-ion / LiPo:
— Phones, laptops, tools — high energy density.
— Needs BMS/charge control — avoid overcharge and deep discharge per cell (~2.5–3.0 V minimum).
— Store ~40–60% SOC for long shelf; mechanical damage/heat = fire risk.

LiFePO4:
— Safer, very long cycle life (~2000–4000+ cycles).
— Higher cost, heavier than Li-ion per Wh.
— Popular for solar storage.

NiMH:
— Rechargeable AA/AAA; ~500–1000 cycles; higher self-discharge than Li-ion.


CAPACITY MATH

Ah × V = Wh (usable Wh is less than nameplate if you respect depth-of-discharge).

Example: 60 Ah × 12 V = 720 Wh.
If only 50% usable (starter lead-acid) → ~360 Wh.
10 W LED → ~36 h; ~10 Wh phone → ~36 charges from that usable Wh (ideal — ignores losses).

Lead-acid guidance:
— Below ~12.0 V resting often ~50% or less; below ~11.5 V hurts life badly.

AGM/GEL: deeper discharge often tolerated (~80%).
LiFePO4: often ~80–90% usable window.


CHARGING

Flooded/AGM/GEL:
— Absorption ~14.4–14.7 V (check battery spec); float ~13.6–13.8 V.
— Charge current rule of thumb ~0.1C (60 Ah → ~6 A) unless manufacturer allows more.
— Resting full ~12.6–12.8 V after ~1 h off charger.

Solar:
— Always charge controller (PWM or MPPT) between panel and battery.

From vehicle:
— Jumper cables to running vehicle — ~10–20 A possible — hours to meaningful charge.

Li-ion / LiFePO4:
— Use correct CC/CV charger profile — never raw unregulated source.
— LiFePO4 4S full ~14.4–14.6 V pack typical.

NiMH:
— Slow ~C/10 ~14 h safe; smart chargers preferred.


STORAGE

Lead-acid:
— Store charged; top monthly ~quarterly — self-discharge ~1–3%/month.
— Cool dry; avoid deep discharge while stored; keep upright (flooded).

Li-ion:
— ~40–60% SOC; cool place.

NiMH:
— Any state; after 6+ months idle, cycle before critical use.


RECOVERY

Lead sulfation (flooded):
— Symptoms: won’t hold charge, fast voltage sag under load.
— Smart chargers “recondition” modes; very slow charge C/50–C/100 for days.
— Charge/discharge cycles between ~10.5 V and ~14.7 V — limited recovery.
— If low electrolyte — **only** distilled water to proper level — never tap water in cells.

Deep-discharged Li-ion:
— May need “wake” trickle to ~2.5–3.0 V then normal charge — **damaged cells can be hazardous**.

NiMH:
— Little memory; deep discharge/charge cycles can help lazy packs.


SERIES AND PARALLEL

Series: voltage adds (two 12 V → 24 V); Ah same — use matched age/condition cells.

Parallel: Ah adds (two 12 V 60 Ah → 12 V 120 Ah); + to +, − to − — similar condition packs only.


SAFETY

— Never short terminals — hydrogen explosion risk when charging flooded batteries.
— Ventilate when charging lead-acid indoors.
— Gloves/eye protection with acid batteries.
— Swollen Li-ion — do not charge; safe disposal outdoors.
— No impacts on lithium packs.
