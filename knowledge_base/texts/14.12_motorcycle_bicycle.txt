CHAPTER 14. VEHICLE: SERVICE AND REPAIR
14.12 Motorcycle and bicycle: basic repair and maintenance

MOTORCYCLE IN A CRISIS

Advantages:
— Maneuver: gaps cars can’t use.
— Fuel: often **~3–6 L/100 km** — much better than many cars.
— Bypass jams and rough tracks.

Disadvantages:
— Weather exposure.
— Little cargo.
— High injury risk in crashes.
— Winter: limited use in ice/snow.

**Good crisis types:**
— Dual-sport / enduro: clearance, tough wheels.
— **250–650 cc** often balances power vs fuel.
— **Carbureted** bikes: simpler field repair where still available.


ROUTINE MOTORCYCLE CARE

**Engine oil:**
— Change **~3–5k km** (often sooner than cars under hard use).
— Level: dipstick or sight glass.
— Viscosity per manual (**10W-40** common).

**Air filter:**
— Paper: blow out gently; replace **~15–20k km**.
— Foam (off-road): wash in solvent, dry, re-oil.

**Spark plugs:**
— Check **~5–10k km**; gap often **0.6–0.8 mm**.
— Dark fouling — rich/oil; white — lean/overheat.

**Drive chain:**
— Slack **~20–30 mm** mid-span (spec in manual).
— Lube every **500–1000 km** with proper chain lube.
— Clean with kerosene or chain cleaner when gritty.
— Sprockets wear with chain — replace as a set.

**Brakes:**
— Fluid level; change fluid **~2 years**.
— Pads: **≥2–3 mm** friction material typical minimum.
— Drums: cable adjustment.

**Electrics:**
— Small battery dies fast if lights left on.
— Long storage — disconnect or tender charge.
— Spare bulbs.

**Tires:**
— Pressure: often **~2.0–2.5 bar** front, **~2.2–2.8 bar** rear — **follow sidewall/bike manual**.
— Tread minimum often **~1.5–2 mm** legal zones — replace earlier off-road.
— Puncture: rope plug similar idea to cars (tread only).

**Carb (if fitted):**
— Clean jets with acetone/carb cleaner; compressed air on passages.

**Forks:**
— Leaking seals (oil on fork legs) — service before long trip.


FIELD MOTORCYCLE FIXES

**Won’t start:**
1. Fuel on? Petcock/reserve?
2. Spark: pull plug, ground, crank.
3. Clogged pilot/main jets.
4. Weak battery: bump start in **2nd** gear — clutch dump at **~10–15 km/h**.

**Broken chain:**
— Shorten with master link spare; carry **master link + tool**.

**Snapped throttle/clutch cable:**
— Route spare cable; temporary wire/zip-tie **only** to limp.


BICYCLE IN A CRISIS

Advantages:
— No fuel — human power.
— Simple repair.
— Quiet.
— Cheap.
— Path flexibility.

Disadvantages:
— **~15–30 km/h** typical.
— Cargo **~20–40 kg** with racks; more with trailer.
— Physical effort.

**Types:**
— **MTB:** tough frames, wide tires, often disc brakes.
— **Touring:** speed + racks.
— **Cargo bikes:** heavy loads (model-dependent).
— **Fat bike:** soft surfaces/snow.

**Suggestion:** hardtail MTB with **hydraulic or mechanical discs**, **1×** or **3×8** drivetrain simplicity.


ROUTINE BICYCLE CARE

**Chain:**
— Lube every **200–400 km**; wet lube rain; dry lube dust.
— Wear gauge or **12-link rule:** **12 inner links** should measure **~304.8 mm** (12 in) when new; elongation = replace chain (and often cassette).

**Cables:**
— Brake engages around **⅓** lever travel after adjustment.
— Barrel adjusters on levers; light oil inside housing.

**Brakes:**
— Mechanical disc: pad gap **~0.2–0.5 mm** — adjust pad bolts.
— Hydraulic: bleed like small automotive brake (nipple + lever strokes).
— V-brake: pad toe and height to rim.

**Flat repair:**
1. Remove wheel.
2. Tire levers — one bead off.
3. Remove tube.
4. Inflate tube slightly; water dip or listen/feel for leak.
5. Scuff area; glue + patch (or peel-stick patch).
6. Reassemble; inflate to sidewall range.

**Derailleurs:**
— Barrel adjuster for indexing; **H/L** limit screws stop over-shifts.

**Pedals:**
— Right pedal **right-hand** thread; left pedal **left-hand** thread — don’t force wrong way.

**Hubs / bottom bracket:**
— Front often long-life sealed; rear cassette body clean; BB replace if crunch/play.


CYCLING KIT FOR CRISIS TRAVEL

Spare tube(s); patch kit + levers; pump (gauge preferred); multi-tool / hex keys; chain tool; **2–3 quick links**; tape; lights if night riding.

**Load carrying:**
— Rear rack + panniers ~**20 kg** practical.
— Trailer **~80–100 kg** class exists.
— Front basket for quick access.
