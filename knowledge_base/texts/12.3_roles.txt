CHAPTER 12. GROUP AND SOCIAL SURVIVAL
12.3 Roles and responsibilities

WHY ROLES EXIST

Without them:
— “Someone should…” = nobody does.
— Duplicated effort in one lane, zero in another.
— Resentment: “I work, they don’t.”
— In crisis that kills people.

With roles:
— Clear ownership.
— Fewer dropped tasks.
— Purpose and visibility.
— Fairer contribution tracking.


CORE ROLES

1. **Leader / coordinator**
— Final call when delegated; ties together workstreams; conflict resolution; morale; horizon thinking (tomorrow, next week).
— Best fit: prior leadership experience, calm under pressure, listens, delegates.
— **Same rations as everyone**; accountable to group; replaceable if incompetent.

2. **Deputy / XO**
— Steps up if leader absent or lost; drives execution on the ground.

3. **Medic**
— First aid, hygiene oversight, drug log and rationing, trains others.
— If no doctor: best-trained person with manual and kit.

4. **Scout / forager**
— Outside-perimeter tasks; routes; threat reads; fit, quiet, observant.

5. **Quartermaster**
— Inventory food, water, meds, tools; issue rations; **daily** log updates; run-out forecasts.
— Needs integrity and detail habit.

6. **Security chief**
— Watch roster; perimeter SOPs; contact protocols for outsiders.

7. **Cook / kitchen lead**
— Meals; spoilage control; kitchen sanitation; tracks usage vs plan.

8. **Tech / facilities**
— Repairs, gensets, heat, water rigging, shelter hardening.

9. **Child supervisor** (if kids)
— Day structure, teaching, safe room during alerts — frees labor.


ASSIGNMENT PRINCIPLES

— Match **skills to role**, not ego to title.
— Dependents do light suitable work — not primary security or heavy scout alone.

**Backup every critical function:**
— Two people minimum who can do medic basics, cook, lead a watch, run inventory.

**Cross-train** adjacent skills.

**Rotate** heavy physical jobs, night shifts, and psychologically brutal tasks (dangerous recon).


SAMPLE DAILY RHYTHM

06:00 — Wake; night watch handoff.
06:30 — Quick perimeter check.
07:00 — Breakfast; **5–10 min** plan: today’s jobs.
08:00–12:00 — Work blocks: water, wood, repair, recon.
12:00 — Lunch.
13:00–17:00 — Work; rest where possible.
17:00 — Dinner.
18:00 — Debrief: intel, problems, tomorrow.
19:00–21:00 — Social time.
21:00 — Night watches begin.

Predictable rhythm cuts anxiety.


TRACKING CONTRIBUTION

**Freeloading** destroys morale.

Mitigations:
— Public task board or verbal roll-call of who did what.
— Daily meeting mention of completed work.
— Optional: base ration + small “earned” bonus for extra duty — group must agree.


WHEN ROSTER CHANGES

**Loss or departure:** immediately reassign critical roles; recalc rations; grief support.

**New arrival:** trial period — no keys to everything on day one.
