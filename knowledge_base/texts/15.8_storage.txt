CHAPTER 15. FIREARMS: HANDLING BASICS
15.8 Storing and transporting firearms

SAFE STORAGE — THREE GOALS

1. **Inaccessible** to children and unauthorized people.
2. **Protected** from rust and damage.
3. **Accessible enough** for lawful defense if that is your plan.

These goals **conflict** — a locked steel safe is slow; a bedside gun is fast. **Choose for your situation and local law.**


LONG-TERM STORAGE (WEEKS TO YEARS)

— **Cleared** or stored per local law: magazine out, chamber empty.
— **Cleaned and oiled** before storage.
— **Dry, dark, cool** space.

**Rust prevention:**
— Thin oil film on metal; **oiled patch in bore** for storage — **remove before firing.**
— Silica gel in case/bag.
— **Monthly** check in humid climates.

**Packing:**
— Oiled wrap + **plastic** box with desiccant beats damp basement alone.
— **Leather** holds moisture — poor for **long** metal contact.

**Surface rust care:** oil types (Ballistol, CLP class products) or light mineral oil; field substitutes as in **15.5**.


READY STATE (LAWFUL DEFENSE CONTEXT)

When quick access is needed:
— Magazine **near** or **in** gun per training and law.
— **Only** responsible adults; **never** children’s reach.

**Pistol readiness jargon (training terms — verify with your instructor):**
— **Condition 4:** mag out.
— **Condition 3:** mag in, **chamber empty** — rack to fire.
— **Condition 1:** chamber loaded, hammer/striker ready, **safety on** where applicable.
— **Condition 0:** ready to fire — **maximum** risk; **trained users only.**

**With children in the home:** many instructors prefer **3 or 4** for storage; **1** only on-body by trained carrier.


TRANSPORT

**Follow all applicable laws** — they vary widely by country/state/city.

**General safety regardless of law:**
— **Case or bag** prevents trigger snags in a vehicle.
— **Secure** the gun — not sliding in the trunk.
— If you need readiness, balance **legal** locked storage vs. access.

**On foot:**
— Long gun: **muzzle safe**, sling adjusted, not flagging the person ahead.
— Pistol: **holster** that covers trigger — **not** loose in a pocket.
— **Sling rule:** adjust length so the muzzle **never** covers you or teammates when slung.

**Pocket carry without holster:** **high** negligent-discharge risk — avoid.


GROUP STORAGE

**Arms room concept:**
— Central secured storage; **one accountable person** (armorer).
— **Inventory:** what, serials if recorded, condition.
— Issue and return with **verbal state** (“cleared” / “loaded”) and **personal** verification.

**Rules:**
— **No** unattended loose guns.
— **Hand to hand** only; state condition.
— **Don’t** handle others’ weapons without permission.
— **Duty weapon stays with** the person on watch.

**Children:** clear teaching — **do not touch**; **tell an adult** if they find a gun.


SIGNS OF NEGLECT

— Rust spots.
— **Bulged** barrel (do **not** fire).
— Loose sights, cracked stock, packed mud in action.

**Surface rust + deep clean** may restore utility.
**Bulged bore, cracked receiver, broken fire control** → **gunsmith** or retire the weapon.

**If in doubt — do not shoot** until inspected.
