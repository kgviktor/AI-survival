CHAPTER 8. ENERGY AND ELECTRONICS
8.1 Finding and using ready-made power sources

WHY ELECTRICITY MATTERS IN CRISIS

Even small power enables:
— Phone charging (comms, maps, flashlight).
— Radio (information).
— Shelter lighting (night safety).
— Some medical devices.
— Charging power banks.

Load priority (typical):
1. Comms and information.
2. Lighting.
3. Medical needs.
4. Food prep.
5. Comfort.


SCAVENGED DC SOURCES

Car batteries:
— Very common early in crisis.
— Typical 12 V ~60 Ah ≈ ~720 Wh if fully charged (usable fraction lower for starter batteries).
— Garages, abandoned cars, shops, warehouses.
— Multimeter: ~12.6 V full, ~12.0 V ~50% SOC, ~11.5 V very low.
— Flooded lead-acid self-discharges over months without maintenance.

UPS units:
— Offices, clinics, server rooms — 12 V blocks inside (or 24/48 V strings).
— Small units ~7–12 Ah; large server UPS ~40–100+ Ah.
— Open case, disconnect terminals safely.

Power tool packs:
— Li-ion ~18–21 V, ~2–5 Ah — usable with proper buck converter to 5 V USB.

Power banks:
— Homes/shops — ~10,000–30,000 mAh typical; 5 V USB output.


FUEL SOURCES

Gas/diesel generators:
— ~230/120 V AC; fuel ~0.3–0.8 L/h per kW load (varies).
— Loud — signature risk. **Never run indoors** — CO kills. Use outside, optional baffle box/shed with airflow — see 8.5.

Inverter generators:
— Quieter, efficient at partial load (Honda EU, Yamaha EF class, etc.).

Propane generators:
— Cylinders widely sold in many regions.


PASSIVE / ENGINE SOURCES

Solar panels:
— Roof installs, garden stores (~5–50 W kits). See 8.4.

Small wind:
— ~12–24 V turbines on some rural sites.

Vehicle alternator (engine running):
— ~12–14 V, high amps while engine runs — tap at battery with engine on.
— Idle fuel ~0.5–1 L/h typical.


BEFORE YOU USE A FOUND SOURCE

Ask:
— Voltage? (6, 12, 24, 48, 120/230 V)
— DC or AC?
— Capacity or power rating?
— Physical damage? Leakage?

Checks:
— Multimeter on terminals.
— Inspect case for cracks, swelling, leaks.
— Smell: burnt insulation, sulfuric acid.

Safety:
— Do not short + and − (arc, fire).
— Damaged flooded battery — gloves/eye protection.
— Swollen/hot Li-ion — fire risk; isolate outdoors.


CONNECTING 12 V LOADS

Auto accessories:
— Cigarette socket plug → many 12 V gadgets.
— 12 V → 5 V USB adapters for phones.
— 12 V LED lamps direct connect.

Phone from 12 V:
— Car USB adapter or buck module ~5 V.
— ~5–10 W (~0.4–0.8 A at 12 V) typical charge.

Inverter (12 V DC → AC):
— Modified sine cheaper; pure sine better for sensitive chargers.
— ~10–20% conversion losses.
— ~150–300 W class covers many small AC needs.
— Short thick leads from battery (e.g. ≥~6 mm² copper for ~150 W — verify length and inverter manual).
