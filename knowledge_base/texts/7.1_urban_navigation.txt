CHAPTER 7. NAVIGATION AND MOVEMENT
7.1 Urban navigation without GPS

WHY GPS MAY FAIL IN CRISIS

GPS/smartphone navigation can fail because:
— Dead battery (no charging).
— Jamming or interference (military or security events).
— No data for map downloads.
— Broken device.

Conclusion: know how to navigate without electronics. Paper map + compass + principles = baseline skill.


PREPARATION — HAVE THIS EARLY

Maps:
— City paper map (~1:10,000 or 1:25,000): streets, blocks, landmarks.
— Regional maps (~1:100,000–1:200,000) for routes beyond the city.
— Offline maps on phone (OsmAnd, Maps.me, Google offline tiles, etc.).
— Print key routes with landmarks marked.

Compass:
— Liquid-filled baseplate compass is more stable than dry pivot types.
— Carry separately from ferrous metal clutter.
— Check: needle points north; matches morning sun azimuth in your hemisphere.


URBAN ORIENTATION PRINCIPLES

Dominant landmarks:
Cities often have tall features visible 1–5 km away:
— TV towers, stacks, bell towers, minarets.
— Distinctive high-rises, bridges, major interchanges.
— Rivers and lakes — linear features, easy on maps.

Cardinal directions in built areas:
— Many grid-planned cities align main avenues roughly N–S or E–W (verify locally).
— At solar noon, shadow points roughly north (Northern Hemisphere; reverse in south).
— Satellite dishes often face equator-ward (south in northern mid-latitudes).
— Traditional church altars often face east (culture-dependent).
— Moss thicker on shaded (often north) side of trees — rough cue only.

House numbering:
— Many regions: odd one side, even the other; numbers often rise away from old center — pattern varies by city — learn yours.

Subway (if running or from memory):
— Line map + “where am I on the network” fixes position.
— Station exits often list street addresses.


USING THE MAP

Orienting the map:
1. Hold map horizontal.
2. Rotate until map north matches compass north.
3. Match two visible landmarks to the map — you lie between them.

Fixing position:
— At intersection read street names if signs survive.
— Find intersection on map.
— If no signs: walk toward a visible landmark, plot it on map, pace count distance (pace ~0.75–0.8 m for many adults).

Pacing:
— Moderate pace ~0.75–0.80 m per step for many men; women often slightly less — calibrate yourself.
— ~100 steps ≈ ~75–80 m; ~125 steps ≈ ~100 m (rule of thumb).
— Count only one foot (e.g. every left) to avoid double counting.

Compass error:
— Knives, keys, rebar distort needle — hold compass ~30+ cm from metal.
— Cars, steel structures — step away for a clean reading.


MENTAL MAP WHEN NO PAPER

Build a running memory:
1. Direction at each turn (N/S/E/W).
2. Approximate distance.
3. Key landmarks (distinctive building, corner store).

“Breadcrumb” method:
— “Turned south at blue house → ~300 m → west at broken traffic light.”
— Return: reverse the sequence.

Terrain tie-in:
— Water flows downhill — rivers are linear guides.
— Following a river downstream often leads toward settlements historically built on water.
— Remember rises and dips — helps prevent loops.


CARDINAL DIRECTIONS WITHOUT COMPASS

Sun:
— Rise ~east, set ~west (seasonal offset).
— Near solar noon in Northern Hemisphere, sun is south; your shadow points north.
— Watch method (analog watch, local solar time): hour hand toward sun; bisector between hour hand and 12 (adjust for DST) approximates south in northern mid-latitudes — verify with training.

Night sky (Northern Hemisphere):
— Polaris ≈ north: Big Dipper pointer stars → ~5× their separation → Polaris.

Other cues:
— Ant hills often on sunnier (southern in north temperate) side of trees/stumps — not universal.
— Birch bark often darker on north side in open forest.
— Snow melts faster on south-facing slopes.


DAMAGED / RUINED CITY

Landmarks vanish: signs gone, streets blocked, blocks reshaped.

Rely on:
— Large fixed objects: rivers, rail lines, parks, power-line corridors.
— Surviving high-rises if stable.

Tactics:
— Gain high ground for overview when safe.
— Move along linear features (river, rail, main road spine).
— Do not maze into collapsed blocks without a known exit.
