CHAPTER 13. GEAR AND EQUIPMENT
13.5 Clothing: layering, fabrics, membranes, care

THREE-LAYER SYSTEM

**1 — Base (next-to-skin):** wick sweat outward. **No cotton** in cold/wet field use — it holds water and chills.

— Synthetic (polyester, polypropylene) — cheap, fast dry, durable.
— Merino wool — odor resistant, warm when damp, pricier.

Fabric weight (g/m²): light ~120–150 active; ~170–200 general; ~230–260 cold static.

**2 — Mid (insulation):** traps heat.

— Fleece Polartec 100/200/300 — higher number warmer/heavier.
— Down jacket — best warmth/weight dry; poor wet.
— Synthetic fill — heavier but damp-tolerant.

**3 — Shell:** wind and rain/snow protection.

— Should **breathe** — non-breathable rain coat = you wet from inside.
— Softshell — wind, light rain, very breathable.
— Hardshell — max rain; less airy.
— Rain jacket — on only when raining.


MEMBRANES

**Water column** (hydrostatic head): ~5000 mm light rain; ~10 000 mm solid; ~20 000+ storm.

**MVTR** (moisture vapor): higher = less clammy — manufacturer specs vary wildly by test method.

Known stacks: Gore-Tex family; eVent; Polartec NeoShell; Pertex Shield; brand house membranes (DryVent, etc.).

**DWR** outer coating beads rain — **reapply** spray or wash-in after detergent strips it.


FIELD DON’TS

— Cotton base in cold wet.
— Jeans — slow dry, chafe.
— Sealed shells during hard march — vent or you soak yourself.


KEY ITEMS

— **Two base sets** — rotate dry/wet.
— Fleece ~200–300 weight.
— Membrane jacket; optional membrane pants.
— Warm hat; balaclava for cold.
— Glove liner + insulated shell or mittens.
— **Merino socks** ×2+ minimum; never sleep in damp socks.
— Gaiters for mud/snow.


FIELD CARE

Wash base cold, gentle soap. Fleece — avoid hot dryer shrink.

Membranes — **no fabric softener**; tech wash + DWR refresh.

Dry off-body fire — not in flame; synthetics melt.

Repair: membrane patches (Gear Aid-type); duct tape emergency.
