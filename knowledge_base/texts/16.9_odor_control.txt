CHAPTER 16. PERSONAL HYGIENE IN THE FIELD
16.9 Odor: stealth considerations

TWO REASONS TO MANAGE ODOR

**1. Low profile:** Human scent (sweat, food, smoke, waste) carries **tens of meters** in still air to people; **farther** to many animals.  

**2. Group living:** Strong body odor or **rot/decay** smells hurt morale, cause conflict, and may signal **illness.**


MAIN SOURCES

**Sweat:** fresh sweat is nearly odorless — **bacteria** on skin break it down → smell. **Armpits and groin** matter most — **wash** those zones even without full bath.

**Clothing:** holds bacteria and oils — only **washing** fully resets; airing helps partially.

**Cooking:** smell travels **hundreds of meters** — fish, meat, onion, garlic, spices especially strong.

**Human waste:** **bury** far from camp; cover each deposit; latrine discipline.

**Smoke:** clings to hair and fabric — distinctive.

**Breath:** garlic/onion/alcohol; **infection** or metabolic disease can alter breath — **brush** teeth and tongue when possible.


BODY ODOR CONTROL

**Daily minimum (stealth):** wipe armpits and groin with damp cloth; **change or wash** underwear — **~10 min** effort helps a lot.

**Baking soda:** pinch on damp armpit — reduces acidity bacteria like — dilute if skin stings.

**Oak bark decoction:** tannins — mild antibacterial wipe.

**Dilute apple cider vinegar (~1:2)** — acidic; vinegar smell fades.

**Wood ash:** alkaline scrub — kills some bacteria — rinse skin after if it irritates.

**Plants:** fern, wormwood, conifer tips — weak antiseptic wipes; mostly **masking.**

**Breath:** brush; chew **mint, parsley**, young **pine** tips; **activated charcoal** tablet (chew) absorbs some odors — use per product guidance.


STEALTH-SPECIFIC HABITS

**Cooking:** work **downwind** of likely observers; **lidded** pots; avoid **fish/meat** when hiding; **dry** foods (biscuits, grains) smell less.

**Fire:** dry **hardwood** — less smoke; **rocket / Dakota** fire pits — less signature; **gas** stoves — minimal smoke/odor.

**Masking (hunting context):** some rub **soil, duff, conifer** on clothing — **animal** scent mask — test for skin irritation. **Natural fibers** sometimes hold **less** synthetic “plastic” smell than some fleeces.

**Wind:** moving **into** the wind relative to a threat reduces **your** scent reaching it; **downwind** movement **broadcasts** you.

**Urine:** do **not** urinate at **sleep site** — odor lingers; **≥30–50 m** away, downhill from water.


ANIMALS

**Large predators:** may smell humans **far off** with favorable wind — **do not run** on surprise encounter (predator response); **calm voice**, **slow retreat**; **fire** often deters wild animals; **food smells** attract — **sealed** or **hung** food storage.

**Insects:** **CO₂** and sweat attract mosquitoes — **DEET** and **picaridin** repellents most reliable; **sleeves/pants** physical barrier; **smoke** helps some; plant oils weaker.


SHELTER SMELL

Enclosed spaces pick up **body, food, toilet, smoke** — **signature** from outside.

**Reduce:** ventilate; **remove** food scraps fast; **latrine** outside or **lidded** bucket; **pine boughs** may freshen air slightly; **charcoal** in open containers may absorb some odor — marginal.
