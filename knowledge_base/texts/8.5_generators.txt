CHAPTER 8. ENERGY AND ELECTRONICS
8.5 Generators: fuel types, human power, alternators

GAS AND DIESEL GENERATORS

Engine spins alternator → AC output (~120/230 V depending on region).

Types:
— Open frame: cheaper, loud (~65–80 dB).
— Inverter: quieter (~50–60 dB), cleaner waveform, better part-load efficiency.
— Diesel: often more efficient on long runs; fuel stores longer treated.

Pros: kW-scale power, reliable with maintenance.
Cons: fuel logistics, **noise/signature**, exhaust — **never operate indoors or in attached garage** — fatal CO risk. Minimum spacing from windows/doors per manual; earth grounding per code.

Fuel use (rough):
— ~0.4–0.5 L/h per kW gasoline at full load; inverter better at light load; diesel ~25–30% better than gasoline per kWh in many cases.

Maintenance:
— Fresh fuel + stabilizer for storage.
— Check oil each run; change ~50–100 h.
— Air filter service.
— Spark plugs ~100 h gasoline.
— Carb: drain for storage or use stabilizer — varnish clogs jets.

Long storage prep:
1. Drain tank and carb (if applicable).
2. Fog cylinder with spoon of oil, crank without fuel — per engine manual.
3. Cover; dry storage.

Safety:
— Refuel only when stopped and cool.
— Ground stake where recommended.


BIKE GENERATOR

Pedal spins wheel or shaft → generator.

Types:
— Tire friction roller: ~5–30 W.
— Bottle dynamo ~3–6 W AC ~6 V.
— Hub generator.
— Stationary trainer driving motor-as-generator — best efficiency.

Human steady output ~50–100 W; athlete bursts higher.
~1 h pedaling ≈ ~50–100 Wh — several phone charges with losses.

Wiring:
— Rectify AC dynamo → charge small battery or buck to 5 V USB.
— Commercial USB dynamo hubs exist.


HAND CRANK

~10–30 W sustainable; tires arm fast.
~1 h ≈ ~15–30 Wh — emergency phone/radio top-up.

DIY: DC motor as generator + crank + rectifier + 5 V regulator.


VEHICLE ALTERNATOR (ENGINE RUNNING)

Idle engine charges battery at ~12–14 V — attach loads at battery terminals.
Fuel ~0.5–1 L/h idle typical.

Removed alternator:
— Needs RPM ~1000–1500+ and excitation (rotor field) on many units — belt drive from engine/wind/water.
— Raw output may need rectifier/regulation — study your unit.


WIND WITH ALTERNATOR

Blades + automotive alternator + charge control — common DIY; excitation feed from small battery to start generation.


MICRO HYDRO

P = η × ρ × g × Q × H (η ~0.5–0.7 rough).
Continuous power if stream reliable — no fuel.
Simple paddle wheel + belt step-up to motor/generator possible.

Example: Q = 0.01 m³/s, H = 1 m, η = 0.7 → ~68 W theoretical.
