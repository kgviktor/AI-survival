CHAPTER 14. VEHICLE: SERVICE AND REPAIR
14.1 No-tools diagnostics: look, listen, smell, feel

VEHICLE IN A CRISIS

A running car is strategic: mobility, cargo, shelter, 12 V power. A dead car at the wrong time is an anchor.

**Any change from normal — investigate before it fails.**


VISUAL CHECKS (WEEKLY UNDER HOOD)

Engine oil:
— Dipstick: wipe, fully reinsert, read. Between MIN/MAX. Below MIN — top up **now**.
— Dark brown/black — normal for aged oil. **Milky** — coolant in oil (serious). Frothy yellow-gray — same family problem.

Coolant (expansion tank):
— Cold level between marks. Green/blue/red per spec OK. Rusty brown — neglected system. White crust on cap neck — possible combustion leak.

Brake fluid:
— Reservoir at firewall. Between marks. **Low** — leak or worn pads (pistons extended).

Belts:
— Cracks, fraying, glaze. Long spans: ~10–15 mm deflection thumb pressure typical for V-belts (check manual).

Under vehicle (after parking):
— **Black/brown** under engine — oil.
— Green/yellow/red streaks — coolant or some ATFs.
— Clear water under A/C condenser — normal summer drip.
— Rust-brown at wheels — brake fluid.

Tires:
— Even wear. Outer/inner only — alignment/camber. Sidewall bulge — **replace**. Cracks — age. Legal minimum tread **~1.6 mm** many regions — use gauge; coin tests vary by coin.


LISTEN — ENGINE RUNNING

Light rhythmic tick — valve lash or injector tick (model dependent).

Loud knock under load — detonation (fuel/octane/heat) or serious bearing knock — **ease off**.

Deep knocking rising with RPM — rod/main bearing — stop soon.


LISTEN — MOVING

Brake squeal — worn pads to metal = **stop and fix**.

Front-drive click on turn — CV joint.

Clunk over bumps — shocks or bushings.

Clunk on brake — loose caliper or warped rotor moving.

Vibration at one speed — wheel balance or bent rim.

Vibration always — tire damage.

Vibration braking — overheated/warped rotors.

Speed-related hum — wheel bearing (often changes loading in turns).


SMELLS

Burning rubber — rubbing tire/liner or **riding brakes** on long descent.

Burning plastic — **electrical** — find before fire.

Gasoline in cabin — **leak** — shut down, no sparks.

Rotten eggs — overcharged battery or cat working hard.

Sweet — coolant leak (heater core or engine).

Burning oil — leak onto exhaust or oil consumption.


FEEL — DRIVING

Pulls — tire pressure, alignment, or **brake drag** one corner.

Steering wheel shake — tire damage low speed; balance high speed; rotors if braking.

Soft brake pedal — air or leak.

Pulsing pedal when braking — warped rotors.

Clutch slip — revs rise, speed doesn’t (manual).

Spongy clutch — hydraulic issue or adjustment.

Hard shifting — cold thick gear oil or syncro wear.

Poor cold start — battery, plugs, fuel delivery.

Poor hot start — fuel pressure, injectors, vapor lock (carb era).


DASH WARNING LAMPS

**Check engine** solid — fault stored; flashing — often misfire — reduce load.

**Oil can red** with engine running — **stop** — no oil pressure.

**Coolant red** — overheating — safe stop, **do not** open hot cap.

**Battery** — charging system fault.

**TPMS** — low tire(s).

**ABS** — ABS off; brakes still work, longer skids possible.
