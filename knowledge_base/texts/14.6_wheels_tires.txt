CHAPTER 14. VEHICLE: SERVICE AND REPAIR
14.6 Wheels and tires: replacement, field puncture repair

TIRE MARKINGS

Example: **205/55 R16 91V**

— **205** — tread width (mm).
— **55** — aspect ratio (sidewall = 55% of 205 ≈ 113 mm).
— **R** — radial construction.
— **16** — rim diameter (inches).
— **91** — load index (e.g. 91 ≈ 615 kg per tire — use charts).
— **V** — speed rating (e.g. V ≈ 240 km/h max sustained — informational).

**Useful load indices (examples):** 84 ≈ 500 kg; 87 ≈ 545 kg; 91 ≈ 615 kg; 96 ≈ 710 kg; 100 ≈ 800 kg.

**Speed letters (examples):** Q 160; R 170; S 180; T 190; H 210; V 240; W 270; Y 300 km/h.


PRESSURE

**Cold** pressure on **door jamb placard** or manual — not always the max on sidewall.

Typical cars: **~2.0–2.5 bar** front/rear (follow placard).

Winter: some drop **~0.2 bar** for soft-surface grip — **your call**; monitor wear.

Off-road: **~1.2–1.5 bar** increases footprint — **slow speed**, risk of debeading if too low + sharp steering.

Check monthly and before long trips; measure **cold**.

Carry accurate gauge; **12 V compressor** or pump for remote travel.


WHEEL CHANGE (FLAT)

Tools: jack, lug wrench, spare (full-size or space-saver).

1. Safe stop away from traffic; hazards on.
2. Chock opposite wheels.
3. **Crack lugs slightly** on the ground.
4. Jack at manufacturer lift point (not body sheetmetal).
5. Lift until tire clears; remove lugs; swap wheel.
6. Hand-start lugs; lower until tire touches; **torque star pattern**.
7. Full lower; final torque to spec (**~80–120 N·m** common — **use manual**).

**Space-saver:** speed/distance limits — get proper repair soon.

**Pre-check spare pressure** before you need it.

Keep: gloves, flashlight, warning triangle where required.


FIELD PUNCTURE — ROPE PLUG (STRING)

Kit: reamer, insertion needle, vulcanizing cement (if included), plugs.

**Budget kits** are widely available — worth carrying.

**Tread punctures only** roughly **≤6–8 mm**; **never sidewall.**

Procedure:
1. Locate leak (sound/bubble test).
2. Object may be holding air — note place; remove when ready.
3. Ream hole several strokes — clean, size hole.
4. Thread plug through needle; coat per kit instructions.
5. Insert until ~½ plug inside; **yank needle** leaving plug.
6. Trim tails; reinflate; soap-test.

Works inflated or deflated — skill-dependent.


SEALANT AEROSOLS (E.G. SLIME, FIX-A-FLAT–TYPE)

Foam through valve seals small tread holes and may inflate.

Temporary **~50–100 km** class fix; tire often needs professional clean-out after.

Fast but **less reliable** than a proper patch.


INTERNAL PATCH (BEST)

Requires demounting tire: buff, cement, patch, roll — shop or skilled field with tools.

Cold-vulcanizing kits exist; hot patch needs equipment.


MANUAL MOUNT / DEMOUNT

Deflate; break bead (stand on sidewall, bead breaker, or careful levering).

Levers around rim — **don’t pinch tube** if tubed.

Remount: soap/lube beads; lever second bead; install valve; inflate until beads **seat** (loud pop) — **use cage or stand aside**.


SEASONAL TIRES

Summer / performance: harder compound; poor when cold **below ~7 °C**.

Winter: soft compound, sipes/studs where legal — much better ice/snow.

All-season: compromise.

Studded: best ice; noisy on dry; **banned or restricted** in some jurisdictions.

**Crisis planning:** two sets if storage allows; else quality all-seasons for your climate; **full-size spare** beats donut when possible.


OFF-ROAD PRESSURE

Air down **~1.0–1.5 bar** for traction — keep **≤30–40 km/h** until reinflated.

Very low pressure + hard steering can **debead** the tire.

**Portable compressor** — match output to tire volume; small 12 V units are slow but recover pressure after trails.
