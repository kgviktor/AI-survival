CHAPTER 7. NAVIGATION AND MOVEMENT
7.7 Transport: bicycle field repair, motor vehicle use

BICYCLE IN CRISIS

Advantages:
— No fuel required.
— Fits paths and wrecked streets cars cannot use.
— Quiet vs engine noise.
— Cargo ~30–40 kg on rack plus pack possible.
— ~12–20 km/h sustained — several× walking speed.
— Easy to hide or abandon if needed.

Disadvantages:
— Fitness required.
— Deep snow or thick mud may stop you.
— Flat tires common.
— Visible valuable — theft risk.

Prep checklist:
— Tires inflated to pressure for rider + load.
— Lube chain.
— Brakes both wheels functional.
— Derailleurs adjusted if fitted.
— Rear rack + panniers.
— Two spare tubes; patch kit; pump with gauge.


BASIC BIKE REPAIR

Flat tire (most common):

Remove wheel:
1. Open quick-release or axle nuts.
2. Rear: shift to smallest cog; loosen if needed.
3. Release rim brakes or deflate to clear calipers.

Tube repair/replace:
1. Pry tire bead with levers (or careful spoons) opposite valve.
2. Pull tube; inflate slightly to find leak (listen, or water dip).
3. Patch: clean area, cement, wait, press patch.
4. Or swap whole tube (faster).
5. Reinstall — ensure tube not pinched under bead; seat tire evenly.

No patch kit:
— Tie knot at hole — limp short distance.
— Pack tire with cloth, leaves, moss — “solid” tire emergency roll.

Chain off front ring:
— Lift back onto ring by hand; turn cranks forward slowly while guiding.

Broken chain:
— Chain tool + quick link ideal.
— Without: remove damaged links, shorten chain — may become single-speed; avoid cross-chaining.

Brakes:
— Worn pads: replace or advance shoe position.
— Cable slack: barrel adjusters.
— Bent rotor: careful gentle re-bend with pliers — avoid cracks.

Derailleur failure:
— Barrel adjuster tweaks; if destroyed, lock to one usable cog with limit screws or zip-tie.


MOTOR VEHICLE IN CRISIS

Advantages:
— Moves heavy load and several people.
— Weather protection.
— Speed on open roads.
— Possible sleep-inside shelter.

Disadvantages:
— Needs fuel.
— Useless in deep rubble or total gridlock noise.
— Loud, visible.
— Without fuel — dead weight.

Fuel discipline:
— Keep tank ≥ half in unstable times.
— Jerrycans ~20–40 L — store legally; in vehicle secure in trunk/hatch, upright.
— Gasoline ages ~3–6 months without stabilizer; longer with stabilizer treatment.
— Diesel ~6–12 months typical before quality issues.

Scrounging fuel (legal/ethical context varies):
— Abandoned vehicles — siphoning risks fire, law, and contamination.
— Station tanks may need specialized pumps if power out — hazardous.


FIELD AUTO REPAIR BASICS

Flat tire:
— Jack + spare — spare must be inflated and fits.
— No jack: lift point with lever and cribbing carefully — crush risk.
— Plug kits can seal puncture without full removal — follow kit instructions.

Dead battery:
— Jump from good vehicle: + to dead +, + to good +, − to good −, last − to dead chassis ground (sparks away from battery).
— Run ~10–15 min to recharge.
— Manual transmission: bump start in 2nd gear ~5–10 km/h.

Overheat:
— Pull over; do not immediately open hot radiator cap.
— Idle to cool if safe; then check coolant level and leaks.
— Water temporary substitute for coolant in emergency — flush/repair later.

Broken serpentine belt:
— Alternator/water pump stop — battery dies and/or engine overheats.
— Spare belt best; improvised belt lasts minutes to rarely longer — limp to help only.

No brake fluid / pedal to floor:
— Do not drive normally.
— Refill correct DOT spec; find leak; bleed if trained.
— Emergency: careful engine braking + parking brake only — high risk.

Stuck mud/snow:
— Spinning digs deeper — stop.
— Traction mats, boards, brush under tires; rocking gentle throttle.
— Air down tires ~15–20 psi temporarily for float — reinflate on hard road.
— Keep shovel in vehicle.

Ignition without key:
— Modern immobilizers and chipped keys make unauthorized start nearly impossible and illegal. This text does not teach vehicle theft — only: carry spare key; know legal recovery options.


MINIMUM IN-VEHICLE TOOL KIT

— Wrenches ~8–22 mm open/box.
— Screwdrivers flat/Phillips.
— Pliers, adjustable wrench.
— Electrical tape.
— Jumper cables.
— Tow strap/rope.
— Factory jack + lug wrench.
— Spare fuses.
— Flashlight.
— Warning triangles or flares (where legal).
— Shovel.
— Short rope ~5–7 m.
