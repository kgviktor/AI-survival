CHAPTER 12. GROUP AND SOCIAL SURVIVAL
12.6 Security watches: shift organization

WHY 24/7 COVERAGE

Threats do not sleep. Many probes hit **night or first light**. One unguarded hour can end everyone.

Security provides:
— Early warning time.
— Deterrence against quiet entry.
— Situational updates.
— Group reassurance.


PERIMETER LAYERS

Define what you **control** — smaller solid beats large porous.

Typical rings:
— **Outer:** yard, approaches.
— **Inner:** entries, stairs, halls.
— **Core:** living space / safe room.

Hardening references: shelter chapter (e.g. `1.2` if in your library) — blocked doors, noise trips, observation windows.


WATCH SYSTEM

Rules:
— **Continuous** coverage — no gaps.
— **Rotation** — nobody on watch forever.
— Aim **≥6 h** sleep between shifts when possible.
— Minimum **1** awake; **2** is better (one can micro-nap while partner scans).

Shift length:
— **2–3 h** ideal alertness window.
— **4 h** max before effectiveness drops.
— Nights: keep shifts short — sleep pressure wins.

Example — six people, three pairs (illustrative):
— Rotate pairs through night blocks; next day shift pairs so same people are not always on the worst slot.

Tiny group (3–4):
— ~3 h on / ~6 h off pattern; daylight may relax to interior observation only; **never** bare night post.


DUTIES ON WATCH

— Scan observation arcs every **15–20 min**.
— **Listen** — night audio beats night vision often.
— Stay mobile; avoid warm silent chair trap.
— **Raise alarm** on ambiguous threat — false positive beats silent failure.
— Log time, sightings, sounds.

Alarm triggers:
— Human movement near wire.
— Footsteps, voices, tools on fence.
— Smoke, nearby fire.
— Changed markers (moved object, cut trip).
— Gut “wrong” — still report.


ALARM SIGNALS

Pre-brief:

**Quiet alert** — e.g. three knocks — “check it out.”

**Full alert** — whistle, shout code, metal on rail — “everyone up **now**.”

Handoff:
— Verbal pass: “Quiet. Saw… Heard…”

**Duress words** — e.g. “table” = OK, “chair” = danger — rotate periodically.


NIGHT VISION HABITS

— Avoid bright lights 15–20 min before post.
— Use **peripheral** gaze for shapes — rods see motion better off-center.
— Motion pops before static objects.

Stay awake:
— Stand, pace, light snack mid-shift.
— Splash cold water if nodding off.

**Sleeping on watch** — first: warning; repeat: lose night privileges; pattern: group review — it endangers all.


OBSERVATION POSTS

Kit:
— Cover from silhouetting; view of main approaches.
— Seat or knee pad; binocs if available; notebook; drink.
— Whistle or noisemaker.

Multiple posts (larger group):
— Primary best field of fire/view; secondary rear; inner door guard.

**Dead zones** — string noise traps or periodic patrol.


OUTSIDE RECON

— ≥**daily**, better **twice**, **variable times** — predictability is weakness.
— **Pairs**; stated route; **latest return time** — overdue = assume trouble.

Returning:
— **Signal approach** — agreed knock or light — so watch does not engage friendlies.
