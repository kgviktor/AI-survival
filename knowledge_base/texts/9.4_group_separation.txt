CHAPTER 9. COMMUNICATIONS AND INFORMATION
9.4 Communications when the group splits

WHY SEPARATION IS DANGEROUS

Split teams are weaker:
— Fewer fighters and workers.
— No mutual status → panic and bad calls.
— Everyone burns time and calories searching instead of surviving.

**Rule:** agree the plan **before** separation. Under stress people default to rehearsed SOPs, not new genius plans.


PRE-BRIEF BEFORE ANY SPLIT RISK

Every member should know:
— Primary shelter (how to find it without phones).
— **Rally Point A** (near home).
— **Rally Point B** if A is unsafe.
— **Rally Point C** outside the neighborhood / town.
— Radio channel, tone, sked times.
— Trail-mark SOP.
— Names and **photos** of all members (each carries copies).

Paper copy:
— Waterproofed card: rally points, freqs, call signs, emergency contacts.
— **On body**, not only in a pack you may lose.


RALLY POINTS

Point A (near):
— ~100–500 m from main shelter.
— Unmistakable: corner + landmark, big tree, known shop sign.
— Wait window: **30–60 min** (twice daily if SOP says).

Point B (fallback):
— ~1–3 km out if A is blocked (police cordon, fire, rubble).
— Wait: **2–4 h** per plan.

Point C (evacuation):
— Outside urban risk — relative’s farm, bridge name, village landmark.

Progression rule:
— Not at A → go B → not at B → go C.
— Leave a note in the **agreed hide** at each stop pointing next movement.


HOW LONG TO WAIT

Typical SOP:
— A: 30–60 min, **morning and evening** windows.
— B: once per day check.
— C: every 2–3 days if long evasion.

Leaving criteria:
— Wait the agreed time; leave **signed** message; move on.
— **Do not** wait past SOP — stationary too long draws risk.


RADIO SKED

Schedule:
— e.g. 08:00 and 20:00 local; **5–10 min** max.
— No answer → retry +30 min; after **3** fails execute “lost comms” plan.

Call signs:
— Short handles: “One, Two, Three…” or neutral codenames.

Status codes (example — make your own):
— “**40**” = all OK (pre-briefed).
— “**99**” = need help.
— **Duress:** agree a phrase that means “I am compromised” (e.g. wrong meeting place name).

Alternate frequency:
— Primary + backup; rotation SOP if jammed or busy, e.g. primary → +1 h alt → +1 h primary.


CHILDREN

Prep:
— Full name; parents’ names; **Rally A** described in kid words.
— Age ~8+: radio channel if they carry one.
— Sewn / laminated contact card in clothing.
— Photo card of family faces.

On separation:
— “Go to A, wait, **do not go with strangers**.”
— Exception only with **family duress codeword** only insiders know.

Reunion with adults:
— No leaving with unknown people without codeword.


TOTAL COMMS LOSS — ALGORITHM

1. Rally A, wait agreed time, leave note in dead-drop.
2. Rally B, repeat.
3. Rally C.
4. If still alone → proceed to **final fallback** (relative, cabin) per plan.
5. Each move: dated note “Name → next point → ETA.”

If captured:
— Do not reveal rally scheme or group strength.
— Feed false directions if forced to speak.


REUNION AFTER SPLIT

Recognition:
— Challenge/response passphrase for strangers at rally sites.
— Visual backup: hat color, pin, gesture.

Duress checks:
— Odd escort or behavior → **no** shelter disclosure.
— Example handshake SOP: right = free, left = under control (pre-brief **your** signal).

After merge:
— Injury and tail check; quick SITREP; inventory; **update the plan.**
