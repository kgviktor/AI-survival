CHAPTER 12. GROUP AND SOCIAL SURVIVAL
12.7 Vulnerable members: children, elderly, chronically ill

PRINCIPLE

Dependents cost calories and attention — they also carry meaning, motivation, and long-term continuity.

Balance:
— Cover **basic needs first** for those who cannot fend alone (especially children).
— Rations sized to real needs (kids ≠ adult portions).
— Everyone who **can** contributes something visible — prevents resentment and preserves dignity.


CHILDREN

Physiology:
— Hypothermia and dehydration hit faster (high surface/mass).
— Hunger harms growth — some effects do not reverse.
— Immune weakness under ~3 years.

Feeding (rough maintenance — adjust for size and activity):
— ~1–6 y: ~1000–1400 kcal/day.
— ~7–12 y: ~1400–1800 kcal.
— Teens: near adult or higher if working hard.
— Fats and protein matter for brain growth <3 y.
— Breastfeeding: add ~500 kcal/day to mother; cache formula if nursing may fail.

Safety:
— Named adult attachment (stable caregiver).
— **Never** unsupervised in crisis.
— Alert → kids to **core** room immediately.
— Simple honest talk: “We are safe here. Adults are watching.” No false promises.

Psychology:
— Routine reduces fear; play and stories are not optional luxuries.
— They read adult anxiety — partial truth beats silent dread.

Chores by age:
— ~6–8: fetch, simple watch-with-adult tasks.
— ~9–12: kitchen help, sorting supplies.
— ~13+: real light labor like a junior adult.


ELDERLY

Issues:
— Thermoregulation weaker; mobility limits; infection risk; common HTN, diabetes, heart disease; stress can worsen cognition.

Food:
— Smaller, more frequent meals often tolerated better.
— Adequate protein preserves muscle = mobility.
— **Remind to drink** — thirst sense fades.

Meds:
— Pre-crisis **90-day** chronic med stock where possible.
— Written med list: drug, dose, schedule.
— Med shortage planning with clinician **before** crisis if you can.

Contributions:
— Experience: preserving, sewing, animal care, teaching.
— Child-minding trade.
— Light sorting, knitting, record-keeping.


CHRONIC ILLNESS

Assess dependency on **tech and drugs:**
— Insulin-dependent diabetes without insulin — days-scale crisis.
— Hypertension without pills — slower risk.
— Asthma without inhaler — severity-dependent.

Priority:
— Life-sustaining meds for search, trade, and guard.
— Central med log known to medic.

Lifestyle:
— Reduce avoidable triggers and overload.

Psychology:
— Ill members often feel “burden.” **Name their contributions** aloud to the group.


SERIOUSLY INJURED AND DYING

Injured:
— Dedicated carer rotation; wound care, food, water, warmth.
— If stocks critical, ration choices are brutal group decisions — decide transparently.

Dying:
— Presence matters; pain control if available.
— Burial or respectful disposal when possible — closure for the group.
— Record name and circumstances for kin.

After a death:
— Brief acknowledgment ritual; return to necessary work — action heals some grief.


FAIRNESS

Avoid:
— **Over-protection** — “patient does nothing” breeds anger and deconditioning.
— **Equal labor demands** — loading the sick like the fit is cruel and stupid.

Aim: **from each ability, to each need** — explain that policy to the group so envy does not fester.
