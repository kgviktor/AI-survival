CHAPTER 8. ENERGY AND ELECTRONICS
8.7 Power budgeting when energy is limited

ENERGY BUDGET MINDSET

Treat watt-hours like cash.

Steps:
1. Estimate daily Wh in (battery usable + solar/generator).
2. List loads and watts.
3. Allocate runtime hours.
4. Keep ~20–30% reserve.


PRIORITY LEVELS

1 — Critical (life/safety):
— Phone, radio; medical devices (pump, CPAP); minimal safety lighting; flashlight charge.

2 — Important:
— Evening room light; power bank topping; water pump; group comms.

3 — Useful:
— Laptop for maps/docs; small fan.

4 — Cut first:
— Electric kettle, space heaters, microwave, large AC fridge, idle wall warts.


TYPICAL DEVICE POWER (ROUGH)

<5 W: phone charge ~5–15 W; 1–5 W LED lamps; radio receive ~0.5–1 W.

5–50 W: laptop ~20–65 W; tablet ~10–25 W; 12 V fan ~5–15 W; small 12 V pump ~5–20 W; 12 V compressor fridge ~30–60 W average.

50–300 W: TV; inverter tare ~5–20 W; loaded inverter + losses ~+20%.

300+ W: kettles, irons, heaters, hair dryers, washers — avoid on small systems.


EXAMPLE DAY (~500 Wh BUDGET)

Phones 2× ~10 W × 2 h = 40 Wh
Radio ~2 W × 4 h = 8 Wh
LED 5 W × 3 lamps × 4 h = 60 Wh
Pump ~12 W × 0.5 h = 6 Wh
Laptop ~40 W × 2 h = 80 Wh
Total ~194 Wh — remainder reserve or extra tasks.

Bad weather plan: drop laptop; cut lamps; phone only essential use.


SAVE POWER HABITS

— On only when needed; kill standby.
— Charge during solar/gen windows when possible.
— Batch charging sessions.
— Phone: min brightness; radios/WiFi/GPS off; airplane mode when idle.
— Laptop: low brightness; battery saver.
— LEDs only; task light not whole room.

Battery care:
— Shallow cycling extends lead-acid life; stop heavy loads before ~12.0 V resting on starter types.
— LiFePO4: follow BMS low-voltage cut.


MONITORING

Voltmeter on battery:
— Charging ~13.5–14.7 V (lead absorption).
— Rest full ~12.6–12.8 V.
— ~12.0–12.2 V — reduce loads.
— <~11.8 V — critical — shed nonessential.

Ammeter shunt or clamp — see who draws current.
Plug-in watt meter — inventory AC loads.
