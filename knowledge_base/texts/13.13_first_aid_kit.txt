CHAPTER 13. GEAR AND EQUIPMENT
13.13 Medical kits: levels, rotation, meds

KIT LEVELS

**Level 1 — On-body EDC:** stop bleeding and cover in first minutes.

**Level 2 — Pack / team bag:** multi-day trauma + illness for small group.

**Level 3 — Shelter / clinic stock:** volume refills and advanced items only if **trained**.


LEVEL 1 — EDC

— **Tourniquet** (validated design: CAT, SOFT-T-W, etc.) — apply high and tight; mark time; max ~2 h without medical direction unless life trade-off.
— **Pressure bandage** (Israeli-style or combine pad + wrap).
— **Hemostatic gauze** (packing) where legal and trained.
— Burn gel dressing.
— **Nitrile gloves** ×2.
— **Permanent marker** — tourniquet time.


LEVEL 2 — GROUP BAG

Dressings: sterile gauze rolls; elastic wraps; silk/cloth tape rolls; assorted adhesive bandages; hydrocolloid blister pads.

Tools: **trauma shears**; tweezers; tongue depressor; digital thermometer; small light; syringes 2/5/10 ml if you know use; irrigation syringe.

Antiseptics: **chlorhexidine** wash; **povidone-iodine**; **70% alcohol** for tools; optional hydrogen peroxide **initial** rinse debate — don’t delay definitive care.

Oral meds (examples — **match to your doctor’s advice and local law**):
— **Ibuprofen** — pain/fever/inflammation.
— **Acetaminophen/paracetamol** — pain/fever; gentler stomach for some.
— Stronger NSAID only if prescribed.

**Antibiotics** — legally prescription-only in many countries; stock **only with physician plan**. Common emergency-course ideas discussed in wilderness medicine texts include amoxicillin-clavulanate, azithromycin, ciprofloxacin — **wrong drug = harm**.

GI: **oral rehydration salts**; activated charcoal for some ingestions (call poison control when possible); **loperamide** for temporary motion stop — not if invasive infection suspected.

Allergy: **antihistamine** (cetirizine, loratadine).

Cardiac risk members: **aspirin** chewable for suspected MI per protocol; **nitroglycerin** if prescribed; BP crisis meds **only** under medical plan.

Wound closure: **Steri-Strips**; tissue adhesive; **suture kit** only with training; skin stapler with training.

Topicals: antibiotic ointment; zinc paste diaper/rash; eye drops if indicated.

**Training beats stuff** — take a stop-the-bleed and wilderness first aid course.


LEVEL 3 — ADD-ONS

Bulk duplicates; surgical instrument set; drains; large irrigation; IV fluids and giving sets — **advanced life support training** required.

Controlled drugs (opioids, benzodiazepines) — legal and documentation strict; store with written indication sheets.


ROTATION AND STORAGE

**FIFO** stock; check **every 6 months**.

**Expired tablets** — many retain partial potency years out; **exceptions:** **tetracycline-class degradation can be toxic — discard**; insulin; nitroglycerin; epinephrine — follow strict dates.

Store cool, dark, dry; **not** bathroom humidity; keep **inserts** with drugs.


GROUP MED CARD

Per person: allergies; chronic conditions; daily meds; blood type if known; **last tetanus** date.

Copy in kit and secure digital/remote copy if possible.

**This file is not medical advice** — build kits with your clinician.
