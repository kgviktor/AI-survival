CHAPTER 16. PERSONAL HYGIENE IN THE FIELD
16.3 Laundry without a washing machine

WHY LAUNDRY MATTERS

Dirty clothes carry bacteria, **lice**, fungus. Oils and sweat can cut insulation **~40%** by some estimates. Body odor draws insects. In a long crisis, **clean layers** support health and low-profile living (**16.9**).


BASIC HAND-WASHING

**Gear:** bucket, basin, pot, or **plastic bag** liner; **warm** water when possible; soap, ash, or powder detergent if found.

**Steps:**
1. Soak in **warm soapy** water **~15–30 min.**  
2. Scrub **collar, armpits, cuffs, groin** zone.  
3. Agitate — twist, rub, knead.  
4. Wring.  
5. **Rinse** in clean water — soap residue irritates skin.  
6. Wring again.  
7. Dry.

**Water heat:**
— **~30–40 °C** — safe most fabrics, good on grease.  
— **~60 °C+** — helps kill **lice eggs** on cotton/linen — **not** for many synthetics or wool.  
— Cold — mostly refresh only.

**Synthetics:** less detergent; dry faster. **Wool:** lukewarm only — hot shrinks/felts.


“BAG” METHOD

Large sturdy bag: clothes + bit of soapy water → **shake, knead, roll** **~5–10 min.** Good for socks and underwear with less scrubbing.


RIVER METHOD

Soap on shore; dip in current to rinse. **Scrub** on flat rock **below** drinking-water intakes. In winter, work fast; stir with stick to save hands.


BOILING LINEN

**~15–20 min boil** kills lice/eggs and many germs — important for **underwear/socks** during lice outbreak. **Cotton/linen OK** — avoid boiling delicate synthetics and wool.

Large pot; some soap or ash; **stir**; do not pack tight; rinse after.


NO-WATER EMERGENCY

**Beat** garments with stick or against tree — dust and some grime out.  
**Air** hours in wind — odor reduction, not deep clean.

**Snow pack:** knead snow into fabric; shake out — better than nothing, worse than soap and water.


DRYING

**Wet clothes in cold** = danger — dry fast.

— **Fire/stove:** fastest — **≥~40–60 cm** from flame; synthetics **melt**; turn often; **never** leave unattended.  
— **Wind/sun** — best in summer.  
— **Body heat:** thin socks inside layers; **inside parka** (careful with moisture next to skin).  
— **Line** in warm shelter.

**Frost:** ice can sublimate from fabric in dry cold; shake ice off; finish drying in warmth.


WASH ORDER WITH LIMITED WATER

**Cleanest → dirtiest** in same water:
1. Handkerchiefs, underwear  
2. Socks  
3. Shirts/base layers  
4. Pants, outer jackets  

Change water when very dark.

**Field kit:** soap bar; bucket; **~5 m** cord; clips or sticks for line.


CLOTHING REPAIR

Holes reduce insulation and hide lice.

**Needle + thread** baseline; **sinew** substitute.  
**Whip stitch** edges; **patches** from scrap cloth.  
**No needle:** safety pins, twists, ties; **glue** (e.g. PVA) — weak temporary fix.
