CHAPTER 16. PERSONAL HYGIENE IN THE FIELD
16.7 Hygiene when sick and caring for the ill

ONE SICK PERSON CAN INFECT A SHELTER

In closed spaces, **fecal–oral**, **respiratory droplet**, and **contact** routes spread disease fast. Without vaccines and antibiotics, **isolation + hygiene** are the main tools. Highest vigilance: **diarrhea**, **vomiting**, **fever + cough**, **unknown rash**.


WHEN TO ISOLATE

— Diarrhea (possible infectious)  
— Unexplained vomiting  
— High fever with cough  
— Unknown skin rash  
— Suspected **cholera**, dysentery, typhoid — **maximum** precautions  

**How (shelter):** separate corner or room; **dedicated** dishes and bedding; **one** designated caregiver; **minimal** visitors; **separate** toilet area or **container** emptied only by caregiver with protection.


CAREGIVER HYGIENE — MOST IMPORTANT

The caregiver must **not** become the next patient.

**Handwashing:** after **every** contact with patient or body fluids — **hot** water + soap or ash; **no** face-touching before wash. **Alcohol-based hand rub ≥60%** if no water.

**Barriers:** layered cloth mask for **respiratory** illness; **gloves** (rubber, nitrile, or plastic bags) for **stool/vomit**; any **impermeable** barrier beats bare skin.

**Clothing:** do not wear **patient’s** clothes; after care, **change** outer layer or scrub to elbows.


PATIENT HYGIENE

**Moisture on skin** from sweat, stool, vomit → **diaper rash**, **bedsores**. **Pat dry** with clean cloth; pay **buttocks, groin, skin folds.**

**Soiled linen/clothes:** change **immediately**; **boil** or soak in **chlorine** solution (**~1 tsp** household bleach per **~1 L** water, **~30 min**) where appropriate — **ventilate**; never mix bleach with ammonia.

**Mouth:** rinse with clean water after vomiting — acid damages enamel; brush teeth if possible.

**Fluids:** offer water or **ORS** often; **dehydration** kills faster than many infections. **Dark urine** = too little fluid; **pale** = better intake.


DISINFECTING SPACE

**Chlorine:** **~10 ml** **~5–6%** bleach per **~1 L** water for surfaces (rough guide — adjust for concentration on label).  
**Boiling:** dishes, some tools.  
**~70% alcohol:** small surfaces.  
**Vinegar:** weaker than chlorine but available.

**Target:** surfaces touched by patient; **toilet container** after each use; **patient dishes** after each use; area around sleeping place.

**Ventilation:** for respiratory illness — **air changes** reduce droplet load. **Sun** on bedding helps reduce some pathogens.


BY DISEASE TYPE

**Gut infections:** **fecal–oral** — strict handwash after toilet; **bury** or disinfect feces; **boil** dishes.

**Respiratory:** masks, distance, **cough into elbow**, ventilation.

**Open wounds with pus:** pus is **infectious** by contact — gloves for dressings; **burn or bury** used bandages if safe.


BEDRIDDEN / SEVERE ILLNESS

**Turn patient ~every 2 h** — bedsores start **~2–4 h** on **sacrum, heels, elbows, shoulder blades.** Pad bony points; alcohol rub or mild saline on pressure points if appropriate.

**Food:** if no appetite, still **push fluids**; thin soups; advance diet as tolerated.

**Fever:** **cool** compresses forehead/neck/axillae; **do not** over-bundle; **hydrate**; **acetaminophen / ibuprofen** if available and appropriate.

**Morale:** regular calm contact improves outcomes — fear and isolation worsen recovery.
