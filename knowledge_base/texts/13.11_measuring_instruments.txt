CHAPTER 13. GEAR AND EQUIPMENT
13.11 Instruments: radiation, weather, misc

WHY MEASURE

Objective data beats guess: invisible radiation, fever, pressure trends, wind chill on exposed skin.


RADIATION MONITOR

You cannot see gamma/beta risk until sick — often too late.

**Geiger-Müller** — clicks with intensity; dose rate µSv/h common; some show cumulative dose.

**Personal dosimeter** — integrated dose badge style for long exposure logging.

**Scintillator** — sensitive, expensive.

**Units:** sievert (Sv) biological; **µSv/h** field rate. Old units: rem (0.01 Sv); roentgen ≈ tissue approximation varies.

**Very rough scale (context-dependent):**
— ~0.1–0.3 µSv/h — typical natural background many places.
— Rising above background — investigate source, limit time, increase distance, shield.
— **Very high rates** — leave area; follow emergency broadcast guidance.

**Care:** annual battery check; functional test with known check source if available (some minerals, calibrated lab source).

**Brand class:** consumer Geigers from established vendors; avoid fake “phone app only” toys for real decisions.


THERMOMETERS

— **Spirit** glass — no mercury toxicity; wide cold range.
— **Mercury** — accurate but hazardous if broken; avoid if possible.
— **Digital** — fast; needs battery.
— **IR forehead** — screening speed; lower absolute accuracy.

**Survival set:** digital + one non-powered backup.

**Food safety** — probe thermometer for pasteurize / canning temps.


BAROMETER

Falling pressure — worsening weather trend; rapid fall — storm signal; rising — often improving.

**Units:** hPa = mbar; 1013 hPa sea-level standard; mmHg still seen in some regions.

**Field:** ABC watch (altimeter/barometer/compass); handheld weather meter; standalone aneroid.


ANEMOMETER / WIND CHILL

Wind strips heat — **wind chill** tables show effective cold.

**Beaufort** quick scale: light breeze → hard walk → storm damage risk.

**Cup or vane meter** — Kestrel-class units bundle wind + temp + humidity + sometimes ballistic solver.

Example: −10 °C + 10 m/s wind ≈ very harsh effective cold — frostbite timing drops fast.


OTHER USEFUL GADGETS

— **pH strips** — water, hydroponics, pool chemistry.
— **Glucometer + strips** — if diabetics in group.
— **Bubble level** — construction.
— **Multimeter** — 12 V systems, solar, troubleshooting (see electronics chapter).

**Priority kit sketch:** radiation monitor if nuclear risk plausible; med thermometer ×2; multimeter if you run power; baro watch if budget fits.
