CHAPTER 16. PERSONAL HYGIENE IN THE FIELD
16.8 Women’s hygiene in the field

WHY THIS SECTION EXISTS

Crisis survival texts often skip **menstrual and genital health** — yet they affect roughly half the population. Lack of facilities does **not** stop physiology; you need **workable options.**


MENSTRUAL HYGIENE — LOW-RESOURCE OPTIONS

**Menstrual cup:**
— Reusable **years**; **~20–30 mL** capacity; empty **~6–12 h** depending on flow.  
— **Boil ~5 min** between cycles for sterilization if possible; rinse with **clean** water each change.  
— Store in **clean** bag or closed container.

**Reusable cloth pads:**
— Layers of **cotton/flannel** ~**7–10 cm** wide, **~20–25 cm** long; pin or tie to underwear.  
— **Cold rinse** first (blood sets less in hot), then **hot** wash with soap.  
— **Sun-dry** — UV helps sanitize.  
— **4–6 pads per cycle** if washing daily.

**Improvised disposables:**
— **Sphagnum moss** — absorbent; use **clean** moss, not from roadsides; air-dry slightly.  
— **Gauze/cotton** layers if available from medical supplies.

**Homemade tampons:**
— Rolled cotton in thin cloth + string — **change ~4–6 h** — **toxic shock syndrome** is rare but real with **prolonged** use of internal products — **do not** leave in too long.

**During menstruation:** wash external genitals **1–2×/day** minimum — **~0.2–0.3 L** careful pour or cloth. **Plain water** is often enough externally — harsh soap can disturb balance.  
**Change schedule:** cup **6–12 h**; pads **~3–4 h**; tampons **4–6 h.**

**Disposal:** bury used materials **away** from water and shelter like other human waste.


DAILY GENITAL HYGIENE

**Wipe front to back** — reduces gut bacteria near urethra.  
**Soap only on outside** — **not** inside the vagina. **Unscented** mild soap if used.  
**Avoid** washing internals with **contaminated** water.

**Underwear:** **cotton** breathes better than synthetics for many people; **change daily** or when damp. **Wet synthetic** + warmth = fungal risk.


COMMON INFECTIONS IN THE FIELD

**Yeast (candidiasis):** triggers — antibiotics, moisture, synthetics, stress, poor diet. **Signs:** thick white discharge, itch, burn. **If meds:** clotrimazole cream/suppositories. **Without:** external **dilute vinegar** dab; **plain yogurt** with live cultures externally (folk); **keep dry**, cotton, airflow.

**Bacterial vaginosis:** fishy odor, thin gray discharge — often needs **metronidazole** when available; some cases fluctuate on their own — **not** self-diagnose reliably in crisis.

**Prevention:** cotton underwear; **dryness**; **no** internal soap; **no** washing with dirty water; **no** long wear of wet bottoms.


PREGNANCY IN CRISIS

**Higher hygiene bar:** immune changes — **food and water safety** critical — **boil** drinking water.

**Watch for:** severe headache, vision changes, swelling — **possible pre-eclampsia** — needs care when accessible. **Regular contractions before term** — seek help if any exists.

**Nutrition:** **~300–500 kcal/day** extra typical; **protein**; **folate** from greens/legumes important in first trimester.


PERIMENOPAUSE / MENOPAUSE

Without hormone therapy: **hot flashes** — cool cloths, ventilation, layered clothing. **Dry skin** — oils. **Sleep/mood** — routine, shared understanding in group reduces conflict.


BUG-OUT MINIMUM FOR WOMEN

— Cup **or** substantial pad supply  
— Several **cotton** underwear  
— **Clotrimazole** if prone to yeast  
— **Metronidazole** only if prescribed/stored knowingly  
— Mild **unscented** soap  
— Small mirror  

**Before crisis:** learning **cup** use reduces long-term disposable dependence.
