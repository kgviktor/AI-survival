CHAPTER 11. TOOLS AND REPAIR
11.4 Cordage, knots, improvised lashing

ROPE MATERIALS

Synthetics:
— **Nylon:** strong, ~10–15% stretch, shock-absorbing — good lifelines and dynamic loads.
— **Polypropylene:** floats, cheap; weaker, brittle in deep cold.
— **Polyester:** low stretch, UV-stable.
— **Paracord 550:** ~250 kg break (type-dependent), seven inner yarns — compact utility line.

Natural:
— Manila, jute, hemp: rot when wet; stiff dry.
— Sisal: harsh on hands.
— Cotton: weak wet.
— Long crisis: natural cordage needs constant replacement.

Sources to cache:
— Climbing/accessory cord (rated where life depends on it).
— Builder’s line, mason’s twine.
— Dock lines — very strong.
— Nylon-jacket copper cable — strip for sheath + wire.
— Fishing line and braided superline.

Improvised fiber:
— Basswood / willow inner bark strips, twisted.
— Nettle retted fiber.
— Spruce roots (whips).
— Leather thongs.
— **3-strand twist** from any fibers — strong enough for many camp tasks.


3-STRAND ROPE FROM THREE BUNDLES

1. Cut three equal-length bundles.
2. Tie one end; anchor (nail, foot trap).
3. Alternate outer strand over middle — like braiding hair; keep tension high.
4. Tie off end.

Strength ~75–80% of sum of strands if well laid.

Splice extension: overlap new bundle ~20–30 cm into braid and continue.


KEY KNOTS

Joining two ropes:

**Reef (square) knot** — same-diameter cord, non-critical loads; can capsize wrong if mis-tied.

**Sheet bend** — different thicknesses; tail through bight correctly.

**Double sheet bend** — extra security.

**Zeppelin / Hunter’s bend** — jam-resistant join (learn if you rely on cord).

To a fixed object:

**Bowline** — fixed loop that does not slip under load; “rabbit out of hole, around tree, back in.”

**Highwayman’s / slipped variants** — quick release if you need escape.

**Timber hitch + half-hitches** — drag a log.

**Clove hitch** — start a lashing; may slip on smooth poles — back up.

**Round turn + two half hitches** — reliable post tie.

**Figure-8** — stopper or clipped loop for climbing-rated systems (use proper PPE training).

Friction / utility:

**Slip knot / running noose** — adjusts under load — animals, snares — ethical/legal rules apply locally.

**Barrel / double fisherman** — join two rope ends compactly.

**Prusik** — 3–4 wraps of thin cord on thick rope; grips under load, slides when slack — adjust tensioners, ascend with training only.


FIELD USES

Stretcher improv:
— Two poles through jacket sleeves (zippered) — carries adult if fabric strong.

Mechanical advantage ~2:1:
— Rope through fixed anchor + pull free end.

Rope ladder:
— Two vertical lines; rungs ~30–35 cm — marline spike hitch or ladder lash per rung.

Tarp ridge:
— Trucker’s hitch or Prusik tensioner for tight pitch.


IMPROVISED CORD

Plastic bags:
— Cut spiral → long plastic strip — weak but ties light loads.

Cloth strips:
— **Tear** (not cut) — stronger edges; braid three strips.

Wire:
— Twist pairs with pliers — stiff lashing, not shock line.

Belts:
— Leather / nylon belts; slice lengthwise for longer lashing.
