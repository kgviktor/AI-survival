CHAPTER 15. FIREARMS: HANDLING BASICS
15.9 Group firearms discipline: safe directions and zones

WHY GROUP RULES EXIST

One armed person can control their muzzle.

**Several** armed people **without** rules → high **blue-on-blue** (friendly fire) risk.

Most civilian “wrong target” shootings involve:
— Violating **know your target and backstop.**
— Not knowing **where friends are.**
— Shooting at **noise** or **shadows** in the dark.
— Careless **handoffs** and **muzzle** discipline during movement.


MUZZLE DISCIPLINE AND ZONES

**Cone of fire** — everything in front of the bore.

In a group: **no one’s** muzzle may **sweep** teammates.

**Sector rule (concept):**
— Each shooter owns a **forward arc**; **do not** turn with a ready gun **across** the group’s position without a rehearsed procedure.

**Safe lanes:**
— When static: agree **who** covers **which** direction.
— Movement **behind** the line: use a **predetermined path**; announce **“Moving behind — hold fire.”**


MOVEMENT IN FILE OR COLUMN

— Muzzle **~45° down** or **~45° up** — **not** horizontal into the back of the person ahead.
— **Finger off trigger.**
— **Spacing ~3–4 m** if practical — reduces **muzzle blast** to the next person’s face on halt.

**Indoors:**
— **Low ready** along floor line in halls; **do not** lazily cover **door cracks** where a friendly might step through.
— **Raise** to target only for **identified** threats.


HANDING OFF A FIREARM

1. **Clear** if policy is “always clear for handoff” — or **state exact condition** if not.
2. Muzzle **safe.**
3. **Receiver** grips frame/pistol grip; **giver** controls muzzle direction until transfer complete.
4. Say aloud: **“Cleared”** or **“Loaded, chamber full, safety on.”**
5. Receiver **re-checks** personally.
6. Receiver: **“Got it.”**

**Never:** throw a gun; hand off **without** stating condition; accept **without** checking.


SECTOR ASSIGNMENTS (PERIMETER)

— **360°** divided among shooters; **overlap** sectors slightly — no gaps.
— **3 people** ≈ **120°** each; **4 people** ≈ **90°** each — tune to terrain.
— Agree **who** engages a threat on a **boundary** between sectors.

**Verbal calls when shifting:** **“Shifting left”** — neighbor acknowledges.


CONTROLLED FIRE VS SELF-INITIATED

**On command:** commander authorizes shots — saves ammo, reduces panic fire; slower.

**Self-initiated:** each shooter engages **clear** threats in **own sector** — faster; risk of confusion.

**Crisis default many teams use:** shoot **only** at **identified** threats in **your** sector; **hold** if unsure.


IDENTIFICATION — ESPECIALLY AT NIGHT

— **No** shooting at **sound alone.**
— **No** shooting at **unidentified** movement.
— **Challenge:** **“Stop! Who’s there?”** before firing if time allows.
— **Password / challenge–reply** for your group at night; **change** it periodically.

Friendly fire **kills** like enemy fire. Stress makes people shoot at **silhouettes.** The fix is **identification before** trigger press.


AMMUNITION ACCOUNTABILITY

— Each person knows **round count**; reports to leader when practical.
— **Don’t** burn everything in one contact — keep **emergency** reserve.

**Rule of thirds (informal):** after **⅓** expended, report; after **⅔**, prioritize **disengage** and resupply.

**Common caliber in a group** simplifies sharing; mixed calibers complicate logistics.


HALTS AND REST

— Weapons **on body** or **under watch** — not lying in the dirt muzzle-down packed with mud.
— **One person** pulls security while others eat or clean guns.
— **Watch change:** outgoing briefs incoming; weapon state **shown.**


SHORT VOCABULARY (AGREE IN ADVANCE)

Examples — **define yours** before stress:
— **“Up”** — ready to move.
— **“Clear”** — no threat seen in sector.
— **“Contact” + direction** — threat seen.
— **“Safe” / “All clear”** — stand down from alert.
— **“Fall back”** — begin withdrawal.

Practice words **out loud** in training so they work under adrenaline.
