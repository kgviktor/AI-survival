CHAPTER 14. VEHICLE: SERVICE AND REPAIR
14.9 Towing and self-recovery: winch, Hi-Lift, improvised methods

BASIC IDEAS

You can get stuck on a good road. Off-road in a crisis — it’s a matter of time. Knowing self-recovery and towing is essential.

**Main rule: don’t spin.** You dig deeper. Assess — then act.


FIRST — ASSESS

Before doing anything:
1. Get out, look: what’s holding you (belly on ground, wheels in holes, what exactly)?
2. Is there a solid jack point?
3. Which way to drive out (forward or back)?
4. Is there a solid anchor point nearby for a winch?
5. How many people and what gear do you have?

**Shovel (first tool):**
— Dig mud, snow, sand from under the belly and ahead of the wheels.
— Always carry one in the vehicle.


TOW STRAP VS KINETIC (SNATCH) ROPE

**Tow strap (static):**
— Stiff-ish: transfers pull smoothly.
— OK for on-road towing.
— **Not** for yank-style extractions.

**Kinetic / snatch strap:**
— Elastic: stores energy and delivers an impulse.
— For pulling out a stuck vehicle with a running helper vehicle.
— Length: **6–10 m**.
— Rating: **6–12 t** typical for cars and SUVs.
— Attach to **rated recovery points** (tow eyes/hooks) — **not** bumpers or random hooks.
— **Recommended:** carry one in every capable vehicle.

**Tow eyes / screw-in rings:**
— Screw-in type (common Euro spec): install before pull.
— Learn where they mount on **your** car (threaded hole often behind bumper cover).


Snatch pull (two vehicles):
1. Connect kinetic strap to rated points on both.
2. Stuck vehicle: neutral or low gear, **no** throttle unless coordinated.
3. Helper: **10–15 m** run-up, controlled snatch → pulls stuck vehicle.
4. **Never** snatch with a non-elastic tow rope/chain: breakage = projectile.


If you’re alone:

SELF-RECOVERY

**Method 1 — Traction boards (sand ladders):**
— Ribbed plastic or metal plates.
— Wedge under driven wheels.
— Gentle throttle; roll onto them; retrieve boards.
— Among the best field tools for mud/sand/snow.
— MAXTRAX, ARB Tred HD: proven; cheaper copies vary in quality.

**Method 2 — Jack + fill:**
— Jack the vehicle.
— Pack hard material under wheels (planks, brush, rocks).
— Lower and try to drive out.

**Method 3 — Shovel + hands:**
— Dig out wheels.
— Clear belly.
— Add hard base.

**Method 4 — Rocking:**
— Forward/back in low range or 1st/reverse.
— On manual: build momentum with clutch control.
— Passengers rocking body — marginal in deep mud.

**Method 5 — Line to fixed anchor:**
— Long line to tree or post; other end to vehicle.
— Winch or block-and-tackle to multiply pull.


WINCH

Best self-recovery tool when rigged correctly.

**Electric winch:**
— Bumper mount common.
— Pull rating: **4,000–12,000 lb** (about **1.8–5.5 t**). Many cars: **~8,000–10,000 lb** class.
— Draw: **200–400 A** when working — **engine running**, good battery/charging.
— **Synthetic rope:** lighter, safer when it breaks (less stored energy than steel), keep clean.
— **Steel cable:** stronger/cheaper, heavy, **dangerous** if it snaps — dampen line, stand aside.
— Sequence: free spool → pull rope → anchor → engage → winch in under load.
— Anchors: tree strap, another vehicle, ground anchor.

**Hand winch / come-along (lever hoist):**
— Often **1–3 t** class; grips rope/cable; pull by lever.
— Slow; works without vehicle electrics.

**Block and tackle:**
— Two blocks ≈ **2×** mechanical advantage (half speed).
— Three blocks ≈ **3×**.
— Anchor block fixed; moving block to vehicle.
— Improvise with pulleys and carabiners **only** if rated for load.


HI-LIFT JACK

High-lift farm/jack style. Can raise a vehicle **~1.2–1.5 m** for packing under wheels.

Uses:
— Lift for traction packing.
— **Lifting** as a winch (adapter kits) — learn before you need it.
— Lift for underside work.

**Hazards:**
— Vehicle is **unstable** when high.
— **Never** work under vehicle on Hi-Lift without proper stands/chocks.
— Needs **solid base** — sinks in soft ground.

**Base plate:**
— On soft soil the foot sinks — use wide board or dedicated Hi-Lift base.


TOWING ANOTHER VEHICLE

**On static strap:**
— Length **4–6 m** typical tow strap.
— Towed driver: steer, watch strap slack, brake smoothly to avoid shock-loading.
— **Automatic trans:** fluid may not circulate engine-off → damage risk.
  — Tow A/T car: **engine running** if manufacturer allows, or **neutral short distance only**, or **dolly/trailer** — **read the manual.**

**If no tow strap:**
— Rope or chain (chains are rigid — snatch risk).
— Rigid tow bar (pipe): predictable, less slack slap.
— **Duct tape:** **no** — won’t hold.

**Signals:**
— Lead vehicle signals before braking.
— Towed vehicle keeps tension — strap shouldn’t go slack then snap tight.


CRISIS VEHICLE RECOVERY KIT (MINIMUM)

Folding shovel; **kinetic strap** 6–10 m; **two** screw-in tow eyes if your car uses them; traction boards (pair); Hi-Lift or strong bottle jack; come-along **1–2 t**; long rope **15–20 m**; work gloves (rope cuts hands); snatch block + rated shackles for pulley systems.
