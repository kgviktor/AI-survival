CHAPTER 12. GROUP AND SOCIAL SURVIVAL
12.4 Decisions, leadership, and conflict

LEADERSHIP IN CRISIS

No leader → fragmentation. Pure consensus → slow death under time pressure.

**Two modes:**

**Mode 1 — Calm (no immediate threat):**
— Discussion, vote, consensus where possible.
— Everyone speaks; majority or agreed rule picks the path.

**Mode 2 — Active threat:**
— Leader **orders**; group executes **now**.
— After-action review when safe.

Switch is **announced:** “Alert — Mode 2.”


LEADERSHIP STYLES

Authoritarian:
— Fast, clear chain; risk if the one person is wrong or abusive.

Democratic:
— High buy-in; too slow under panic.

**Situational (recommended):**
— Shift styles as conditions change.

**Technical lead:**
— Medic decides clinical; electrician decides wiring — coordinator delegates expert lanes.


DECIDING UNDER UNCERTAINTY

**“Good enough” method:**
1. You have 2 minutes or 2 hours — use the window.
2. Gather max useful intel in that window.
3. List 2–3 options.
4. Pick **least catastrophic** downside — not fantasy-perfect.
5. Move. Paralysis is often worse than a mediocre plan.

**Reversibility:**
— Prefer choices you can undo.
— Irreversible (abandon shelter, start fight) — only under necessity.

Communicate:
— “We do X because Y.”
— Dissent heard **before** execution, then compliance.


CONFLICT TYPES

1. **Resources** — “Why did they get more?”  
   Fix: transparent logs, posted norms, public distribution.

2. **Strategy** — stay vs go.  
   Fix: argue evidence → vote → **everyone backs outcome**.

3. **Personality** — two people cannot coexist.  
   Fix: split tasks/shifts; private talks; mediation.

4. **Power** — leadership challenge.  
   Fix: open legitimacy talk; vote; orderly handover.


DE-ESCALATION STEPS

1. **Separate** combatants; cool-down ≥30 min; no audience circus.
2. **Each side** speaks without interruption; mediator summarizes.
3. **Find shared goal** — usually survival — disagree on method.
4. **Written or announced** resolution if it affects the whole group.

**Do not:** pick sides publicly without hearing both; humiliate; ignore — it explodes later.


REPLACING A LEADER

Triggers:
— Paralyzed or dangerous decisions; steals rations; systematic threats; majority trust lost.

Process:
— Open discussion (not back-room coup).
— Vote.
— Orderly transfer of duties.
— **Safety and dignity** for outgoing leader if they remain in group — prevents civil war inside.


LEADER BURNOUT

Signs:
— Impulsive or frozen decisions; irritability; isolation; skipping food/sleep.

Fixes:
— Delegate; **scheduled** rest; trusted debriefer; deputy ready to carry command temporarily.
