CHAPTER 16. PERSONAL HYGIENE IN THE FIELD
16.10 Improvised soap and cleaners

WHY DIY SOAP MATTERS

Commercial soap runs out. In a long crisis, **handwashing** is still the main brake on **diarrheal disease.** Soap = **fat + strong alkali** → **saponification** → soap + glycerin. **Fat** is findable (animal, plant). **Alkali** historically came from **wood ash leachate (lye).**


LYE FROM ASH (POTASH)

**Best ash:** hardwoods (birch, oak, ash) — richer in potassium carbonate.

**Leaching:**
1. Ash in container with **cloth filter** or holes in base.  
2. **Pour water slowly** through — collect **dark runoff** = **lye solution.**  
3. **Boil** gently to concentrate if needed.

**Strength test (folk):** raw **egg** floats in strong enough lye for soap-making; sinks = too weak — concentrate more.

**Safety:** concentrated lye is **caustic** — **gloves**, **eye protection**, **no** drinking, **no** casual skin contact.


SOFT (LIQUID) POTASSIUM SOAP — SIMPLE FIELD APPROACH

**Rough recipe concept:** **~1 L** concentrated lye + **~300–400 g** **rendered** animal fat.

1. Heat lye in **metal** pot (not aluminum if lye attacks it).  
2. Add fat **gradually**, stir.  
3. **Simmer low** **~1–2 h**, stir often.  
4. Thickened, uniform mass = saponification progressing.  
5. **Skin test:** tiny dab — **soapy** slip, not pure grease.  
6. Store in jar.

**Result:** brown liquid soap — hands, dishes, laundry.


HARD (BAR) SOAP

**Sodium hydroxide (NaOH)** makes **hard** bars — **rare** in nature. **Wood-ash lye** is mostly **potassium** → **soft/liquid** soap.

**If you obtain NaOH** (drain cleaner in some regions — **check legality and purity**): classic cold-process uses **precise** weights — **~100 g NaOH** dissolved in **~330 mL water** (**always add lye to water**, never reverse — **heat splash**), then **~750 g** oils — **only with exact recipe, scales, PPE, ventilation** — **dangerous if guessed.**


OTHER CLEANERS

**Ash + water paste:** weaker than finished soap — still cuts grease on hands/pots.

**Horse chestnut / soapwort (*Saponaria*):** **saponins** foam with water — wash hair, delicate fabrics.

**Animal bile:** emulsifies fat — spot treatment on greasy stains (when butchering).

**Stale urine (historical laundry):** ammonia content — **alkaline** wash water — works on stains; **sanitize** context mentally if used.

**Fern ash:** often **high potash** — good lye feedstock.

**Horsetail (*Equisetum*):** silica — **scrub** pots with bundled wet stems — natural abrasive.


HAIR WASH (SEE ALSO 16.5)

**Egg yolk** — beat with water; rinse **cool.**  
**Rye flour slurry** — mild scrub.  
**Nettle rinse** after washing — folk “tonic.”  
**Weak ash wash** + **vinegar rinse** after.


DISH WASHING

**Ash + grit + rag** — cuts grease.  
**Sand** — abrasion for baked-on food — rinse well after.  
**Homemade liquid soap** — general purpose.


STORAGE AND RATIONING

**Liquid ash soap:** sealed jar, cool place — **months** possible; may **separate** — shake before use.

**Use tiny amounts** — **friction** matters more than foam. **Dilute** to stretch supply.
