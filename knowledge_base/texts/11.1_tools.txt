CHAPTER 11. TOOLS AND REPAIR
11.1 Basic tool kit: what to acquire and store

HOW TO BUILD THE KIT

Tools in a crisis support:
— Shelter repair (roof, windows, doors).
— Making essentials (furniture, containers, more tools).
— Gear and clothing repair.
— Resource work (firewood, metal).

Priorities:
— **Hand tools** first — work without mains power.
— Durable, field-serviceable, simple mechanics.
— General-purpose (one good knife beats ten odd gadgets).
— Consumables stock: blades, abrasives, files, fasteners.


CATEGORY 1 — CUTTING (TOP PRIORITY)

Knife:
— Fixed blade stronger than folders for hard use.
— ~10–15 cm blade is versatile.
— Steel: stainless (e.g. 440C, AUS-8 class) vs carbon (1095, O1) — carbon sharper/easier to sharpen, rusts.
— Carry: primary + backup + small folder.
— Sharpening: dual-grit stone; honing rod for touch-ups.

Saws:
— Wood saw: building, firewood.
— Hacksaw + **10+ spare blades** (they snap).
— Folding camp saw: pack-friendly.

Axe:
— ~1–1.5 kg forest axe: splitting, building.
— ~0.5–0.7 kg pack axe for carry.
— Linseed oil handles; keep edge sharp.

Chisels / gouges:
— 1–2 sizes for wood joinery and fitting.


CATEGORY 2 — STRIKING AND PRY

Hammer:
— ~500–800 g claw or carpenter hammer.

Pry bar / wrecking bar:
— **High value:** doors, crates, rubble.
— ~40–60 cm balances leverage vs carry.

Cold chisel:
— Metal vs masonry types differ.

Sledge:
— ~2–4 kg: stakes, demolition.


CATEGORY 3 — FASTENERS AND LASHING

Fastener stock:
— Nails ~50 / 80 / 100 mm — kg each size as budget allows.
— Screws ~3.5×35, 4×50, 4×70 — hundreds.
— Bolts M8/M10 + nuts + washers.
— Staples; wall plugs if you work concrete.

Wire:
— Soft tie wire ~1.5–2 mm.
— Stiff steel ~3–4 mm for frames.
— Copper electrical scrap.

Tape:
— **Duct tape** — multiple rolls.
— Electrical tape — many rolls.
— Painter’s / masking tape.
— Self-amalgamating rubber — pipes, seals.


CATEGORY 4 — LAYOUT

— 5 m tape.
— Folding rule.
— Spirit level.
— Square.
— Pencil / marker.
— Plumb bob optional.


CATEGORY 5 — WRENCHING AND TURNING

Wrenches:
— Open/box set ~8–24 mm.
— Adjustable (“crescent”) ~200 mm and ~300 mm.
— Socket set + ratchet if you can.
— Pipe wrench for plumbing.

Screwdrivers:
— Phillips Ph0–Ph3.
— Slotted ~4, 6, 8 mm.
— Torx T20, T25, T30.

Pliers:
— Linesman / combination with cutters.
— Needle-nose.
— Clamps (F-style) ×2–4 minimum.

Drill:
— **Hand drill / brace** + twist bits ~3–12 mm for grid-down work.
— Or cordless + spare batteries + 12 V charging path.


CATEGORY 6 — BUILDING AND FINISH

— Trowel; putty knives ~100 / 200 mm.
— Brush + small roller.
— Manual chain saw (fool’s capstan type) for logs if relevant.
— Brace and bit optional.
— Hand plane.


CONSUMABLES — STRATEGIC

Without these, edges and jobs stall:

— Sandpaper ~80, 120, 240 grit.
— Files: flat, round, half-round, coarse/medium.
— Needle files.
— Sharpening stones.
— Solder + flux (if you have heat source).
— Hacksaw blades ~10–20.
— Spare drill bits.
— Adhesives: PVA wood glue, contact cement, 2-part epoxy, cyanoacrylate.
— Sealants: silicone, acrylic, polyurethane.
— Soldering iron + spare (if power available).


WHERE TO FIND TOOLS IN CRISIS

— Hardware / builder merchants (full sets).
— General stores.
— Apartments — almost everyone has basics.
— Garages and workshops — often complete kits.
— Job-site shacks.
— Auto shops — mechanic’s tools.

When scavenging:
— Hand tools over corded-only if power uncertain.
— Quality pays: look for known professional brands vs disposable junk.
— Portability: what you can move.


STORAGE AND CARE

— One primary box/bag; fixed places — find by touch in dark.
— Inventory list on lid.

Care:
— Oil steel against rust.
— Linseed wooden handles.
— **Sharp tools are safer** than dull — dull forces slips.
— Dry storage.

Grab-go core:
— Knife + pry bar + hammer + pliers + tape measure + screwdrivers + duct tape.
