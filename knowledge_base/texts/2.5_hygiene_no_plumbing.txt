CHAPTER 2. WATER
2.5 Hygiene and sanitation without running water

WHY HYGIENE MATTERS IN SURVIVAL

In historical and modern crises most deaths come not from violence but disease — dysentery, typhoid, cholera. They spread fecal-orally via dirty hands, contaminated water, and poor waste disposal. Good hygiene on minimal water is preventive medicine.

Hygiene priority order:
1. Handwashing (prevents most illness)
2. Safe disposal of human waste
3. Food-prep hygiene
4. Body hygiene
5. Dental care
6. Wound care hygiene


HANDWASHING

Minimum water use:
Thorough soap wash can use ~0.5–1 L if careful. Save-water technique: wet hands (~0.1–0.2 L), stop water, soap 20 seconds, rinse (~0.2–0.3 L).

Always wash hands:
— Before and after eating
— After using the toilet (critical!)
— Before and after wound care
— After handling trash, rodents, animal carcasses
— After blowing nose or coughing

Soap:
Any soap works — “antibacterial” is not better than plain soap with proper technique. No soap: wood ash (alkali) + water. Hand sanitizer 60%+ alcohol: when no water. Sanitizer does not replace soap if hands are visibly dirty — rinse dirt off first.

DIY water saver:
Plastic bottle with a small hole in the cap — squeeze for a thin stream. Tilt-can: hole in the bottom of a can; tilt to pour, upright to stop. Saves 3–5× vs a wide-mouth jug.


TOILETS AND WASTE DISPOSAL

Rules:
Human feces must be disposed of away from water, shelter, and food storage. Minimum ~60 m from water sources, ~30 m from shelter. Bury ~15–20 cm deep — enough to limit disease spread.

Pit latrine:
Dig ~1×1 m, 60–90 cm deep. Each use: cover with ~2–3 cm soil or ash. Lid the pit against flies between uses. When fill is ~30 cm from the top — bury and dig a new pit.

Indoor toilet (no sewer):
Lidded bucket. Trash-can liner simplifies emptying. After each use: cover with peat, sawdust, soil, ash, or shredded paper — reduces odor and speeds breakdown. Empty daily into a buried pit.

Urine:
Separate from feces — lower risk. Outdoors: drain 30+ m from water. Indoors: pour into sewer line (even if dry), or into a pit.

Toilet paper alternatives:
Large smooth leaves (not nettle). Crumpled newspaper. Moss. Snow. Pour water.

After toilet:
Handwashing — mandatory, no exceptions. Minimal water: rinse and use sanitizer. If no water or sanitizer: rub hands with ash, then brush off — weak, but better than nothing.


BODY HYGIENE

Economy wash:
“Bird bath”: ~0.5–1 L warm water + cloth or sponge. Face first, then hands, neck, armpits, groin — in that order; separate cloths or rinse between zones. Surprisingly effective with good technique.

Body-part priority:
Hands — several times daily. Face and neck — daily. Armpits and groin — daily (sweat + bacteria = odor and rash). Feet — especially if walking a lot. Hair — less often (1–2×/week).

Without water:
Dry shampoo: starch, flour, or talc in hair, brush out — absorbs oil. Wet wipes for groin and armpits. Wipe skin with cloth dampened in vodka/spirit — some disinfection.

Feet — special care:
If hiking daily: wash feet, change socks, let feet dry. Blisters and hot spots: early care prevents infection. Athlete’s foot: keep dry and aired.


FOOD-PREP HYGIENE

Surfaces:
Wipe cutting boards with boiling water or dilute bleach before use. Separate boards (or wash thoroughly between) for raw meat vs other foods.

Dishes:
Hot soapy water or ash scrub; rinse with clean water. If water is scarce: wipe with cloth, then dry cloth. Slightly clean beats obviously dirty.

Food storage:
Closed containers protect against flies and rodents. Flies carry gut pathogens — especially in warm weather. Do not leave leftovers open.


DENTAL CARE

Why it matters:
Toothache without a dentist is serious. Dental infection can spread and become life-threatening.

Without paste and brush:
Chew one end of a hard twig (willow, birch, hazel) into fibers — brush. Baking soda as paste — mild abrasive and antiseptic. Salt — weaker but common. Gauze wrapped on finger — polishes teeth. Salt rinse (1 tsp per cup water) 2×/day.

Tongue scraping:
Bacteria live on tongue coating — use a metal spoon edge.

Toothache:
Warm salt rinse. Clove oil on cotton — mild temporary relief. Aspirin against gum (not on the tooth) — folk method, weak. Prefer ibuprofen/paracetamol if in your kit.


MENSTRUAL HYGIENE

Alternatives to disposables:
— Menstrual cup: reusable; boil between cycles; low water use
— Cloth pads: wash in hot soapy water
— DIY pads from clean cloth, gauze, cotton

Sterilize cup: boil 5 minutes.


INSECT VECTORS

Flies:
Cover food and waste — primary control. Trap: bottle with sugar water and narrow neck.

Mosquitoes:
Window screens (any fine-weave cloth). Lemon balm, wormwood, mint near shelter — weak natural repellent. Long sleeves and pants. Activity drops at ≤~10 °C.

Lice and ticks:
Lice: tight clothing, daily head checks, fine comb. Ticks: full-body check after outdoors. Removal: tweezers at the mouthparts, steady pull without twisting.
