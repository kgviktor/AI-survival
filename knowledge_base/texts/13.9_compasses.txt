CHAPTER 13. GEAR AND EQUIPMENT
13.9 Compasses: types, use, declination

WHY COMPASS BEATS PHONE-ONLY GPS

GPS needs: power, sky view, intact electronics — all can fail.

**Magnetic compass:** no battery, hard to jam, works under canopy (if you read it correctly).

**Rule:** map + compass baseline; GPS optional boost.


TYPES

**Liquid-filled needle** — damped; standard hiking quality.

**Baseplate** — ruler edge for map work; global standard.

**Mirror / sighting** — more precise bearings; mirror for signaling.

**Button / zipper** — backup only.

**Wrist** — quick rough heading.

**Surplus military** — often rugged; verify accuracy vs known line.


USING THE COMPASS

Level the housing; rotate bezel until **red needle aligns with orienting arrow** (red in the shed); direction-of-travel line now points your **heading**.

**Follow an azimuth:** set desired degree on bezel, align needle, walk keeping alignment; **pick landmarks** along the way to reduce cumulative drift.


MAGNETIC DECLINATION

Magnetic north ≠ true north. **Difference** is declination — **location and time dependent**.

Your topo map margin shows **true north grid**; compass reads **magnetic**.

**East declination** (magnetic east of true): common in much of Europe, Asia, North America varies by coast — **always read the map legend**.

**Rule of thumb:** errors &lt;~5° matter little for short hikes; **long legs or artillery-precision nav** — apply correction.

**Adjustable-declination** compasses bake offset into bezel — set once per region.

**Sources:** map footnotes; national geological survey web tools; GPS app offline note.


MAP WORK

**Orient map:** rotate paper map until grid north matches compass north (with declination handled per your method).

**Resection:** take bearings to 2–3 known features; back-plot lines on map — intersection ≈ your position.

**Route plan:** edge of compass from A to B on map; read bearing; apply declination; walk.


INTERFERENCE

Keep compass **30–50 cm** from steel, knives, weapons, batteries, radios, vehicles, rebar.

Hold **flat** — tilted needle sticks or lies.

**Verify:** compare two compasses; or Polaris / known road alignment.


MODELS (ORIENTATION)

Sighting: Silva Ranger-class; Suunto A-30; Brunton TruArc; USGI lensatic pattern.

Budget reliable: simple baseplate from major outdoor brand.

**Thumb compass** — orienteering speed tool.
