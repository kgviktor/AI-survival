CHAPTER 6. SECURITY
6.5 Contact tactics: talking vs leaving

CONTACT OR WITHDRAWAL

Decide in seconds.

Withdraw (avoid contact) if:
— They clearly outnumber you and are armed.
— Already aggressive or chasing someone.
— Signs of organized criminal group.
— You carry critical gear you cannot protect.
— You can vanish unseen.

Consider contact if:
— They look neutral or need help.
— Mutual benefit possible (information, trade).
— Avoiding meeting is impossible.
— Potential allies.


SAFE CONTACT PRINCIPLES

First seconds matter — control:
— Body language: open palms, no sudden moves.
— Distance: keep ~3+ m.
— Voice: calm, confident — not panicked or hostile.

Never:
— Show panic (it invites predation).
— Flash valuable gear or full packs.
— Reveal shelter location, group size, or stockpiles.
— Turn your back without control.

Goal of any contact:
End peacefully without giving dangerous information.


NEGOTIATION

“Nothing to take, nowhere important” frame:
— “We came from [vague/wrong direction], heading [vague].”
— “We have almost nothing — still looking.”
— Signals you are a poor target.

Neutrality technique:
— Origin vague: “from the west,” “that district” — no addresses.
— Neither confirm nor deny shelter.
— Safe topics: weather, roads, distant observations.

Mutual interest:
— “Have you seen [neutral thing — people, vehicles]?”
— “We heard there might be [resource] that way — any info?”
— Small information trade builds neutral tone.

When they demand resources:
— “Only what we carry — rest is far away” (protect true cache).
— “We just got this from others — no more.”
— Give a small, expendable item if it buys safe separation.

Safety concession:
If clearly violent and refusal likely triggers attack — yield goods. Stuff replaces; life does not. Exceptions: dependents, irreplaceable moral lines — personal call.


SPOTTING LIES AND SETUPS

Common manipulations:
— “We only need help” + probing your shelter.
— Fake distress (injury, sick child) to get inside.
— Pity play to lower guard.
— Talk while partners flank.
— “I’m alone” — three more around the corner.

Negotiation as cover:
— They move while talking (closing distance or angling).
— Eyes scan for partners.
— Only questions about you, vague about themselves.

If you sense manipulation:
— “We need to go” — leave immediately without debate.
— Do not let the talk drag.
— “Stay there — talk from there.”


HOSTILE CONTACT

If clearly aggressive or threatening:
— Calm voice, no shouting match.
— Do not escalate with ultimatums first.
— “We’re not looking for trouble. We need to pass. Let us go our way.”
— Symbolic concession (bit of food, harmless info) to de-escalate.

If weapon threatened:
— No sudden moves.
— Hands visible.
— Slow speech: “Okay. I hear you. We’ll do what you say.”
— Look for safe break to withdraw — not Hollywood disarms unless you are trained.

If ordered to go with them:
— Major red flag. Going voluntarily with hostile strangers is extremely dangerous.
— “We need time — we’ll come back” — buys pause if credible.
— Break contact at first real chance, even with some risk.


GROUP PROCEDURES AT CONTACT

Two or more people:
— One talks; others silent (single story).
— Others watch flanks and rear.
— Prearranged “we leave now” word or gesture.

Roles:
— Speaker holds attention.
— Observer scans for exits and threats.
— If children: one adult keeps kids back and ready to move.

Withdrawal signal:
— Agreed word (“let’s go,” “okay, we’re leaving”) or touch signal.
— On signal everyone moves out cleanly — no arguments after.


AFTER THE MEETING

Debrief:
— Remember or write: numbers, appearance, arms, directions.
— Share with group.
— Adjust routes and watch schedules if needed.

Do not repeat the same pattern:
— If tense, they know roughly where you came from.
— Change movement patterns temporarily.
— Increase perimeter watch.
