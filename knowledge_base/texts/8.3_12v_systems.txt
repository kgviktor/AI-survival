CHAPTER 8. ENERGY AND ELECTRONICS
8.3 12 V systems: wiring, fuses, distribution

WHY 12 V

De facto standard for small off-grid:
— Vehicle batteries are 12 V (most light vehicles).
— Huge accessory market (lamps, chargers, pumps, fridges).
— Low touch voltage — still can burn wiring if shorted — respect fuses.

Basic diagram:
Source (battery/solar) → fuse → load.
Or: Panel → charge controller → battery → fuse → loads.


WIRE GAUGE (COPPER, ROUND GUIDE)

I (A) ≈ P(W) / V(V) — e.g. 60 W @ 12 V ≈ 5 A.

Approximate minimum cross-section (short runs; increase for long runs):
— To ~5 A: ~1.5 mm²
— To ~10 A: ~2.5 mm²
— To ~20 A: ~4 mm²
— To ~30 A: ~6 mm²
— To ~50 A: ~10 mm²
— To ~80 A: ~16 mm²
— ~1000 W inverter @ 12 V can exceed ~80 A — very short heavy leads per inverter manual.

Thicker wire = less heat and voltage drop.

Long runs: upsize one step or calculate drop (target often <~3% loss).

Color convention:
— Red (+), black or blue (−/return).

Terminations:
— Crimped lugs or soldered; screw twists only temporary — tape well.


FUSES — REQUIRED

Fuse saves wiring from overload/short — fuse blows, not your harness.

Placement:
— Within ~30 cm of battery + on each main feed.
— Each branch fused separately.

Rating:
— ~110–130% of steady load current — not huge oversize.

Types:
— Automotive blade (ATO/ATC), ANL for high current, glass for small loads, DC breakers convenient.

Main battery fuse:
— Slightly above realistic max simultaneous load — still protects feeder cable gauge.


DISTRIBUTION

Bus bars or fused blocks:
— + bus with fuse per tap; common − return.

Marine/auto fuse blocks — compact and reliable.


TYPICAL LOADS

12 V LED strips (marked DC12V): ~0.5–2 A/m — fuse ~5–10 A branch.
12 V lamps (G4, MR16, etc.): ~3–10 W each.
USB adapters from 12 V: ~1–3 A per port.
Car stereo head unit: ISO wiring — constant +, switched +, ground.
12 V water pump: ~3–10 A.
12 V compressor fridge: ~2–5 A average (cyclic).


12 V → AC INVERTER

Size ~120–130% of max load.
Pure sine for picky electronics; modified OK for many resistive loads.
Thick short DC leads; fuse at battery.
No-load draw ~0.5–1 A common — turn off when idle.
Keep ventilated.


GROUND / RETURN

Common DC negative return to battery −; star grounding reduces noise issues.
Poor ground = flicker, charge faults — clean and tight.


TROUBLESHOOTING

Load dead:
1. Fuse
2. Battery voltage
3. Ground return
4. Terminal torque

Hot wire:
— Undersized conductor or high-resistance joint — fix gauge/crimp.

Fuse pops instantly:
— Short or wrong fuse rating vs load.
