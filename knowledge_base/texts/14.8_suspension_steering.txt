CHAPTER 14. VEHICLE: SERVICE AND REPAIR
14.8 Suspension and steering: what you can fix in the field

SUSPENSION

Functions:
— Absorbs road impacts.
— Keeps tires on the ground.
— Allows steering and braking to work.

**Shocks / struts:**
— Damp spring oscillation.
— Worn: **float** after bumps, oil on body, knocking.
— **Bounce test:** push corner down — **1–2 rebounds** OK; more = weak dampers.
— Replace **in pairs** (both front or both rear).
— Field: swap if you carry spares; otherwise drive gently to help.

**Springs / leaf packs:**
— Support ride height and load.
— Broken coil: **side sag**, noise — **no real field fix** — tow or spacer limp only with extreme caution.
— **Leaf springs** (older trucks, some 4×4s): temporary **U-bolt / clamp** repairs possible with skill.

**Sway bar end links:**
— Connect bar to control arm/strut.
— Worn: **clunk** on bumps, worse in corners.
— Usually **two bolts** — easy spare to carry.

**Control arm bushings:**
— Rubber cracks → clunk, vague steering, tire wear.
— Replacement needs presses — **hard in the field**.

**Ball joints:**
— Pivot between arm and knuckle.
— Worn: clunk, **vertical play** at wheel.
— **Dangerous** — separation can drop corner — **do not drive** if loose.
— Check: lift, hands at **6 and 12**, push/pull — **play &gt; ~1–2 mm** → replace.

**Control arms:**
— Bend in big hits — pull, uneven tire wear.
— Minor bend: **never “good”** — straightening with a hammer is guesswork — limp slowly to alignment shop.

**CV boots** — see 14.7 (same as axle boots).


STEERING

**Rack-and-pinion** — most modern cars: precise, compact.

**Recirculating ball / worm** — older trucks and some 4×4s — adjustable play; heavier feel.


SYMPTOMS

**Excessive free play:**
— Large dead zone before wheels move.
— Engine on, rock wheel — **&gt;~10–15 mm** at rim often too much — causes: tie rods, rack, column joints.

**Clunk steering:**
— **Tie-rod ends** — shake joint, feel play.
— **Inner tie rod / rack** — clunk while turning rolling.

**Heavy steering:**
— **Hydraulic PS (HPS):** check reservoir — clear/red OK when new; **dark** = overdue; leaks at pump/lines/rack.
— **Electric PS (EPS):** fuses, power to module, torque sensor faults.
— Pump failed — may limp **slowly** with high effort.

**PS fluid leak:**
— Oily spots; often ATF-type fluid in HPS systems — **top up with correct spec**; many HPS tolerate labeled ATF — **verify manual**.


TIE-ROD END — FIELD SWAP

Outer ball stud at steering arm — **two per axle** typical.

1. Count **threads** or measure exposed shank so new end is close to same toe.
2. Loosen jam nut; remove cotter; unscrew end (puller or careful shock — don’t damage boot).
3. Install new to **same turn count**; jam nut; cotter.

**After any tie-rod change → alignment shop ASAP.** Until then: **moderate speed**, expect pull; recheck toe roughly (below).


TOE (ROUGH FIELD ALIGNMENT)

**Toe** — wheels pointed slightly in/out vs centerline; **camber** — lean in/out at top.

Bad angles → rapid uneven wear, pull, nervous steering.

**Crude toe check:**
— Measure **front-to-front** and **rear-to-rear** of inner tire edges (or consistent tread grooves) at axle height.
— Often slight **toe-in ~0–2 mm** front narrower than rear — **model-specific**.
— Adjust **tie-rod sleeves** — equal both sides to keep steering wheel centered.

Gets you to a shop — not a substitute for laser alignment.


MAINTENANCE

— Grease **zerks** on ball joints/tie rods if fitted — **~15–20k km**.
— Inspect **CV boots** every service — crack = plan replacement.
— Bushings — visual crack/tear.
— **Torque suspension fasteners** annually or after off-road.


CRISIS SPARES (SUSPENSION / STEERING)

— Outer tie-rod ends (pair).
— Sway bar links (pair).
— Ball joints if you can press them.
— Wheel bearings (pair).
— CV boot kits + grease.
— PS fluid **~0.5 L** (correct type).
