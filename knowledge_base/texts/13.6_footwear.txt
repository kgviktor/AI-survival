CHAPTER 13. GEAR AND EQUIPMENT
13.6 Footwear: types, fit, care, field repair

WHY FEET FIRST

Blisters, soaked feet, and sprains **stop movement** — then infection and morale collapse.

**Protect the feet** — basic survival rule.


BOOT TYPES

**High hiking boots** — ankle support; debris and scree protection; many use waterproof-breathable liners; **Vibram-class** outsoles preferred.

**Low hikers** — lighter; less roll protection.

**Military-style boots** — durable; often heavier.

**Rubber boots** — total waterproof for flood/muck; poor breathability; add warm socks; not for long-distance ergonomics.

**Insulated winter boots** — Thinsulate or wool felt ratings by climate.

**Gaiters** — keep snow/mud out of cuffs.


FIT

— Try **late day** when feet swell; with **trail socks** you will actually wear.
— ~1–1.5 cm toe space downhill.
— Heel locked when laced — no slip.

**Break in** new boots **weeks** before a hard trip.

Quality markers: real membrane (not marketing-only “waterproof”), solid sole rubber, resole-able construction if possible.


BLISTER PREVENTION

Causes: friction, moisture, wrong size, new leather.

Fixes: proper lacing; **change wet socks**; thin liner + outer sock; lubricant or tape on hot spots early.

Small blister — **don’t pop**; pad with hydrocolloid.

Large tense blister — sterile puncture at edge, drain, **keep roof skin**, antiseptic, dress.

**Infection** — red streaks, pus — escalate care.


DAILY CARE

Brush mud; wipe; **dry in shade** — sun and fire crack leather and glue.

Newspaper or boot trees hold shape drying.

Condition leather ~2–4 weeks when in use; refresh DWR on fabric boots.

Check laces and delamination before trips.


FIELD REPAIR

**Sole peel:** shoe contact cement — both sides, open ~10–15 min, press; optional awl + waxed thread backup.

**No glue:** wrap duct tape — hours to days only.

**Laces:** paracord, cloth strip.

**Upper tear:** awl + heavy thread; leather patch + glue.

**Worn slick sole:** rubber sheet from tube or belt; glue.

**Seam weep:** seam sealer (Seam Grip-type); beeswax rub traditional.

**Waterproofing:** wax on leather; silicone spray on synthetics — test discoloration first.
