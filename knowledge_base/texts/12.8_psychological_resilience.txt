CHAPTER 12. GROUP AND SOCIAL SURVIVAL
12.8 Long-term psychological resilience

WHY MINDSET = SURVIVAL

Skills fail without will. Panic, despair, and rage drive fatal mistakes. People who quit often die of problems survivors solve.

**Three rough phases:**

**Phase 1 — Acute (first ~72 h):** shock, panic, or numbness — avoid irreversible moves.

**Phase 2 — Adaptation (~1–4 weeks):** new rhythm — risk of **false** “we’re fine” complacency.

**Phase 3 — Chronic (months+):** fatigue, hope loss, friction — build **systems** of support, not heroics alone.


NORMAL VS ALARMING STRESS REACTIONS

Normal short-term:
— Crying, shaking after danger; sleep glitch; irritability when hungry/tired; intrusive memories first days; appetite dip.

**Seek help / intervention:**
— Emotional flatline >~1 week; cannot work or self-care; rising unprovoked aggression; suicidal words or planning; weeks without sleep or food; frozen on tiny decisions.


TOOLS FOR RESILIENCE

1. **Daily rhythm** — fixed wake, meals, work, rest; small anchors (morning brief, evening check-in).

2. **Physical work** — burns cortisol; walk, squats, chores — not gym vanity, nervous-system hygiene.

3. **Purpose** — everyone should know today’s useful task; celebrate small wins aloud.

4. **Social glue** — “How are you?” for real; humor when appropriate; shared meals; safe touch among consenting kin.

5. **Micro-pleasures** — tea, sugar, book, music — morale is logistics.

6. **Agency pocket** — personal space, personal gear respect, some choices you control.


GRIEF AND LOSS

Grief often moves through denial, anger, bargaining, depression, and acceptance — not linear, not on a schedule.

Support:
— **Be there.** Do not preach “stay strong” or “they’re better off.”
— Say the dead person’s name.
— Practical help: food, water, cover their shift briefly.
— After a few days, **gentle** return to duty — structure heals some pain.

Group loss:
— Short farewell; minute silence; keep a token or story — builds continuity.


ANXIETY QUICK TOOLS

— **4-7-8 breath** or box breathing.
— **5-4-3-2-1 grounding:** see 5 things, hear 4, touch 3, smell 2, taste 1.
— Ten squats or brisk walk — body reset.

Worry sort:
— List fears; mark “can I act today?” — act or consciously park the rest.

Planning:
— Even a vague week plan reduces helplessness.


SUICIDE RISK — FIELD RESPONSE

Signs:
— Direct statements; giving belongings away; sudden calm after deep depression; isolation.

Steps:
1. **Stay with them.** Do not leave alone.
2. Ask directly if they plan self-harm — clarity reduces risk.
3. Listen without judgment.
4. Remove means (weapons, meds stockpiles, ropes) if safe to do so.
5. Continuous watch until professional help or sustained improvement.
6. Offer **meaningful task** they alone can do for the group — bridge back.


GROUP MORALE DECAY

Signs:
— Output drops; petty fights spike; “nothing matters” talk.

Countermeasures:
— Recall concrete progress timelines.
— Scheduled rest and small celebrations.
— Rotate brutal jobs.
— Affirm each person’s value with **examples**.
— Talk about **a** future — even uncertain — “when this eases, we will…”

**Professional mental health care** when society restores — first aid above is not therapy.
