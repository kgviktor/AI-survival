CHAPTER 14. VEHICLE: SERVICE AND REPAIR
14.3 Fuel system: clogs, air, filter replacement

GASOLINE INJECTED

Tank → in-tank pump → filter → rail → injectors. Pressure often ~2.5–4 bar.

**Carburetor** (old): mechanical pump → float bowl — simpler field fixes.

**Diesel:** lift pump → **water separator** → filter → high-pressure pump (common rail **1000+ bar**) — DIY HP pump repair unrealistic.


SYMPTOMS

No start: empty tank (check), dead pump, clogged filter, air in diesel lines.

Rough / stumble: dirty injectors, weak pump, bad pressure regulator.

Fuel smell: **leak** — fire risk — no sparks.


EXTERNAL FILTER REPLACEMENT (GAS)

1. **Relieve pressure** — fuse for pump out, crank until stalls, or schrader bleed if equipped (rag catch).
2. Catch drips; note **arrow direction** on new filter.
3. Replace; start; leak-check.

**Ventilated area. No flames.**


INJECTOR CLEANING

Minor: quality fuel; tank additives periodically.

Severe: professional ultrasonic; field — remove and solvent flush **only if trained** — fire/toxic risk.


DIESEL ISSUES

**Air in lines** after filter service — long crank, uneven running, stall.

**Bleed** per manual — hand primer on filter head or electric lift pump cycle.

**Gelled / frozen fuel:** summer diesel waxes near 0 °C; use **winter-grade** fuel; warm filter/tank area; commercial anti-gel.

**Drain water** from separator when lamp on or seasonally — water destroys injectors.


PUMP CHECK

Key ON (not start): many cars **prime ~2–3 s** — faint hum from tank. Silence — check fuse/relay/pump.

Field: spare pump module if you rely on remote travel.


LEAK REPAIRS

**Gasoline = extreme fire risk.**

Find wet fittings; short hose + clamps as shim; epoxy only dry depressurized joints.

Tank hole: drain low; clean; epoxy rated for fuel; or solder tab patch metal tank — then verify **no seep** with engine running, **no ignition sources nearby**.
