CHAPTER 10. FOOD PRODUCTION
10.3 Electricity-free hydroponics

WHAT HYDROPONICS IS

Growing in nutrient solution without soil. Roots get water, oxygen, and minerals directly from liquid.

Vs soil:
— Often ~3–5× faster growth for many leafy crops.
— Less total water in recirculating setups.
— Fewer soil weeds/diseases.
— Vertical stacks possible.
— Lighter than mineral soil.
— Yield more predictable **if** solution is managed.

Powered systems need pumps/airstones. **Without electricity**, use passive methods.


PASSIVE DEEP WATER CULTURE (DWC-LITE)

Principle:
— Net cup in lid; roots hang into reservoir.
— **Air gap** between liquid surface and bottom of cup — roots breathe in humid air above the water.

Why it can work without a pump:
— As solution level drops from use, the air space grows.
— Keep initial level ~2–4 cm **below** the net cup bottom (adjust as roots elongate).

Build:
1. Lidded bucket or tote ~5–20 L.
2. Holes in lid for net cups.
3. Medium in cup: clay pebbles, gravel, coco, perlite.
4. Seedling or cutting in medium.
5. Fill with nutrient solution (recipes below).

Maintenance:
— Top up as level falls.
— Full change every ~1–2 weeks (salt buildup).
— pH target ~5.5–6.5 for most crops.

Good passive DWC crops:
— Lettuce, spinach, basil, mint — excellent.
— Green onion, parsley, dill.
— Strawberry, cucumber (needs trellis).
— Cherry tomato possible but needs **large** volume and careful management.


WICK SYSTEM

Principle:
— Reservoir below or beside pot; cotton/nylon wick lifts solution by capillarity.

Build:
— Lower tank: nutrient solution.
— Upper pot: coco or perlite (not dense garden soil).
— 1–3 wicks ~1–2 cm wide through pot bottom into liquid.

Limits:
— Small, low-demand plants (herbs, lettuce).
— Not enough flow for heavy feeders (large tomato, cucumber).


NUTRIENT SOLUTION

Plants need **N-P-K** and micronutrients. Pure water starves them.

OPTION A — Commercial hydro fertilizer (best if you can stock):
— Any complete hydro blend per label (e.g. two-part A/B, or “Masterblend-style” tomato formula).
— Typical tomato/pepper powder recipe often cited online: ~4-18-38 base + calcium nitrate + Epsom salt in measured ratios per 10 L — **follow a tested chart** for your brand.

OPTION B — Pharmacy / hardware improvisation (incomplete but usable weeks for greens):
Per ~10 L water (rough starting point — verify with small test batch):
— Calcium nitrate (if sold as fertilizer): ~10 g.
— Epsom salt (magnesium sulfate): ~2.5 g.
— Monopotassium phosphate: ~2.5 g (garden/chemical supplier).
— Iron chelate or liquid iron micro-dose per label (~few drops to 10 L).

OPTION C — Emergency organic weak tea:
— ~1 Tbsp wood ash per 10 L, steep ~24 h, strain (K, P).
— Compost tea: handful compost in water, ~24 h, strain (N + traces).
— Mix ~1 part teas to ~20 parts water as feed.
— Yellowing lower leaves = hunger — upgrade feed if possible.

Water and pH:
— Target ~5.5–6.5.
— Rainwater often close; high-pH tap: few drops lemon juice or vinegar, retest.
— Too acidic: pinch baking soda, mix, retest.
— Cheap **aquarium pH test strips** work in the field.

Reservoir hygiene:
— Full refresh ~weekly–biweekly; wipe white salt crust.
— Spent solution can water soil beds.


VERTICAL TOWERS

— PVC or stacked PET bottles with side planting pockets.
— Pour nutrient from top daily; collects in sump; manual return or re-pour.
— High plant density per m² floor — great for greens/strawberry on balcony/roof.


LIGHTING

— Window/balcony: prefer natural sun; south-facing best.
— If supplementing: LED ~5000–6500 K, ~15–30 cm above canopy, ~14–18 h for lettuce.
— Power budget example: 16 h × 30 W ≈ 480 Wh/day per small shelf — prioritize sun first.
