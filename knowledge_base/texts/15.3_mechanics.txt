CHAPTER 15. FIREARMS: HANDLING BASICS
15.3 Mechanism and function: bolt, chamber, magazine

THE SHOT CYCLE (ALL GUNS)

Regardless of type, the sequence is the same in concept:

1. Feed a cartridge into the **chamber** (from magazine, tube, cylinder, or by hand).
2. **Lock** the breech with the bolt/slide/breechblock.
3. **Firing pin/striker** hits the **primer** → powder ignites.
4. Pressure drives the **bullet/slug/shot** down the bore.
5. Unlock; **extract** case; **eject**; **load** next round (semi-auto/full-auto) or wait for manual cycle.

Understanding this helps you **clear malfunctions**, **unload safely**, and see why each part matters.


MAIN PARTS

**BARREL**
— Guides the projectile.
— **Rifled** — spiral grooves for spin → accuracy.
— **Smooth** — for shot/slugs (rifling would damage shot patterns).
— **Chamber** — rear enlargement where the cartridge seats.

**BOLT / BREECHBLOCK**
— Locks the chamber for firing.
— Holds **firing pin** or transfers striker energy.
— **Extractor** — pulls case from chamber.
— **Ejector** — kicks case clear of the action.

**BOLT CARRIER GROUP (self-loaders)**
— Moves rearward on recoil or gas impulse; carries the bolt (AK, AR, etc.).

**FIRE CONTROL**
— **Trigger** releases hammer or striker.
— **Hammer** or **striker spring** supplies strike energy.
— **Safety** blocks trigger, sear, and/or hammer — layout varies.

**SAFETY TYPES**
— **Lever** (AK, many older designs).
— **Trigger-tab / blade** (some striker pistols).
— **Decocker** (lowers hammer without firing) on some pistols.
— Not all systems have an external manual safety.

**MAGAZINE**
— **Detachable box** — pistols, rifles.
— **Tubular** — under barrel on many shotguns.
— **Cylinder** — revolver.
— **Fixed internal** — some shotguns, old military rifles — loaded through the action.

**CHARGING HANDLE / SLIDE**
— Retracts bolt/slide for loading or stoppage reduction.
— AK: right-side handle on carrier; pistols: serrations on slide rear.


AK-PATTERN — OVERVIEW

Simple, robust gas-operated system.

**Field-strip concept (verify empty first!):**
1. Remove top cover (rear button/lever per model).
2. Recoil spring assembly out.
3. Bolt carrier back and out; bolt rotates out of carrier.
4. Barrel/receiver stay; handguards/stock separate on full strip.

**Gas operation:**
— Port bleeds gas → drives piston/carrier rear → unlock, extract, eject.
— Return spring drives carrier forward → strips round from magazine → chambers → locks.

**Selector:** often combines **safe** with **bolt handle lock** on many AKs — **on safe, charging handle may not move** — clear magazine first, then move selector as needed to open action (**always follow 15.4**).


SEMI-AUTO PISTOL — OVERVIEW

**Frame** — fire control, grip.
**Slide** — moves; contains breech face, extractor, may house striker.
**Barrel** — locked to slide briefly (Browning tilt) or fixed (blowback .22, etc.).
**Recoil spring** — around barrel or under.

**Cycle:** trigger releases striker/hammer → shot → slide recoils → extract/eject → spring returns slide → loads next round.

**Slide stop:** locks slide open on **empty magazine** — signal to reload; after new mag, release slide (lever or sling-shot).


PUMP SHOTGUN

**Barrel, tubular mag, forend, bolt** tied to forend.

**Cycle:** pull forend **back** — unlock, eject; forward — chamber next shell from tube.

**Loading:** shells into tube through port (details vary); first round may need action closed per manual.

**Break-action:** open at hinge; insert in each chamber; two triggers or selector; close.


STRIPPING FOR CLEANING

**Always verify unloaded** (mag out, chamber empty) **first.**

**AK:** sequence above — detail differs by variant (milled vs stamped, etc.).

**Pistol:** mag out, lock slide back, verify empty, then takedown per model (lever, pin, trigger guard tilt — **use the manual for your gun**).

**Never force** unfamiliar takedown; wrong moves can launch springs or damage parts.
