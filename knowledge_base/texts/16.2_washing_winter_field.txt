CHAPTER 16. PERSONAL HYGIENE IN THE FIELD
16.2 Washing in winter and field conditions: snow, river, sauna

WINTER WASHING

Cold, frozen water, and **hypothermia risk** complicate hygiene — but winter hygiene still matters. Sweat and dirt **wet insulation** from the inside; damp clothing in frost = **cold injury risk.**

**Rule:** wash **fast**, in **warmth** or by **fire**, and **dress immediately.** **Never** stand wet in the cold. Lay out **dry** clothes **before** you start.


SNOW BATH

**When:** roughly **≥−10 °C**, **sheltered from wind**, you are **well-fed**, **not** already hypothermic.

**Method:**
1. Undress near **heat** (fire or enclosed shelter).
2. Take handfuls of **clean fresh snow.**
3. Rub skin **briskly** — mechanical clean + blood flow.
4. Whole body **~2–3 minutes.**
5. Dress at once.

**Do not use** if hypothermic, frostbitten, ill, weak, or below **~−15 °C** without solid shelter.

**Partial:** face, neck, armpits — open jacket, quick rub, close — works without full undress.


RIVERS AND LAKES

**Warm season:** natural full bath possible. **Carry** water to heat by fire for soaping when you can. **Wading/swimming** only if **current, depth, and bottom** are safe. Wash **away** from drinking-water intake points.

**Winter / open water:** find a **polynya** or melt snow in shelter. **Slip risk** at banks. Heat water; wash **by the fire**, not standing in ice water.

**Cold water:** **&lt;~15 °C** OK for short wipes; **&lt;~10 °C** prolonged contact risks chill. For comfort and cleaning, **~35–40 °C** when you can heat water.


IMPROVISED SAUNA / STEAM

**Field benefits:** real wash + warming + stress relief.

**Pit steam (basic idea):**
1. Dig pit ~**1 × 1 × 0.7 m**.  
2. Heat **large stones** in fire **~1–1.5 h**.  
3. Move stones into pit.  
4. Cover with tarp, branches, or blanket **“dome.”**  
5. Enter; **splash** small amounts of water on stones → steam.  
6. Wash inside **~15–30 min** — do not overdo when exhausted or dehydrated.

**Tent steam:** small teepee of plastic/tarp; hot stones in basin; splash water; curtain door.

**Dugout sauna:** longer project — heated rock pile — reusable.

**Safety:**
— **Ventilation** — CO risk if fire is poorly vented (**Ch. 8**).  
— **Overheating** — dizziness, collapse — exit, cool, hydrate.  
— **After steam** — cool air hits fast — **dry and dress quickly.**  
— **Do not** sauna **starving** or **severely dehydrated.**


HOT TOWEL / STEAM WIPE

Boil water; hold **steamy** wet cloth over skin — loosens grime; wipe.  

**Foot steam:** hot water basin; feet above steam under a cloth — softens skin; comfort after marching (**16.4**).


SEASONS

**Spring thaw:** more water — good for catch-up hygiene; water still cold — heat when possible.

**Summer:** easiest; open water — **check contamination** (**Ch. 2–3**).

**Autumn:** wash while mild; **store** covered water for winter.

**Winter:** full wash **~every 5–7 days** at fire or sauna; daily **critical zones** (armpits, groin); snow as supplement.
