CHAPTER 16. PERSONAL HYGIENE IN THE FIELD
16.5 Hair and scalp in the field

PRACTICAL PRIORITIES

Long hair tangles, holds dirt, and supports **lice** (**16.6**). **Short hair** is simpler. If you cannot cut — keep hair **under a hat** or tied back.

**Hair still helps:** insulates head in winter; shades scalp in summer; but **matted dirty hair** smells and stands out visually and to scent (**16.9**).


WASHING HAIR WITHOUT SHAMPOO

**How often:** **~every 7–10 days** may suffice; more if heavy sweat or contaminated rain.

**Soap:** works; may feel harsh — **vinegar rinse** (~1 tbsp / L water) cuts alkaline “squeak.”

**Wood ash lye (weak):** strain ash water; wash; rinse clean — historical method.

**Egg yolk:** beat with little water; work into hair; rinse **cool** — hot cooks protein.

**Clay (fatty type):** paste on damp hair; rinse — absorbs oil.

**Water only + thorough brushing** — partial clean — better than nothing.


DRY “SHAMPOO”

**Starch or flour:** dust roots, rub, **brush out** **~5–10 min** — absorbs oil; white residue if not brushed well.

**Baking soda:** stronger oil removal; can **dry scalp** if overused.

**Powdered charcoal:** odor/oil — shows on light hair.

**Fine dry soil** (some traditions): rub in, brush — abrasive; use **clean** dust.


SCALP CARE

**Dandruff / oiliness** worsens with stress and poor diet. **Daily brushing** stimulates skin.

**Dilute vinegar (~1:3)** on scalp **~5–10 min** then rinse — shifts pH; may reduce fungal component.

**Itch:** often oil or dirt — wash/brush; **chamomile** or **nettle** rinse soothes (folk use).

**Stress shedding** — common short-term — often improves when nutrition and stress improve.


COMBING AND BRAIDING

Comb or brush **daily** — detangles, spreads oils, **early lice detection** (**16.6**). Fingers work slowly.

**Braid or ponytail** — less tangle, less environmental contact, easier lice checks.


FIELD HAIRCUTS

**Why short:** easier hygiene, less lice habitat, less heat retention in summer.

**Without scissors:** careful **knife** trim (risk); **not** recommended: burning ends.

**With scissors/knife:** “bowl” or even trim; damp hair cuts more evenly.

**Shaved head:** maximum simplicity for lice control — **sharp blade + lather** — practice and care to avoid cuts.


BEARD AND MUSTACHE

Long beard traps crumbs, odor, parasites. **~3–5 mm** stubble is a compromise.

**Shave without foam:** warm water + soap lather + sharp blade; **with grain** for less irritation; **oil** can substitute lather. Same **lice** checks as scalp if dense.
