CHAPTER 12. GROUP AND SOCIAL SURVIVAL
12.11 Education and knowledge without the internet

WHY IT MATTERS

Knowledge is **non-rival** — sharing multiplies it.

Long crisis risks:
— Experts die or leave — expertise dies with them.
— Children miss civilization basics.
— Within a generation, “obvious” skills vanish.

Goals:
— Preserve what you know.
— Teach the young.
— On-ramp new adults fast to needed skills.


PRIORITY KNOWLEDGE

Life-support practicals:
— First aid and field hygiene.
— Water find + treat.
— Food grow, preserve, store.
— Shelter build and repair.
— Fire and safe heating.
— Land nav.

Literacy and numeracy:
— Reading unlocks manuals; math runs inventory, rations, and trade.

Crafts:
— Sewing, smithing basics, pottery, carpentry — things that **make** goods.

Social memory:
— Community rules and origin story — “who we are, how we live.”


KNOWLEDGE STORAGE

**Books** — gold standard offline:
— No power; stable text; shared copies.

Cache priorities:
— Medical: field surgery, nursing, herbal references with caution.
— Agronomy, animal husbandry, vet basics.
— Electrical and mechanical primers.
— Chemistry (household and industrial safety).
— Cooking, preserving, fermentation.
— Construction and forestry/plant ID.

Where to find:
— Public libraries; schools; used bookstores; estate sales; your own shelf now.

Care:
— Dry, cool, rodent-safe; zip or wax paper wrap against mold.

**Living manuals:**
— Specialists write **what is not in books** — tribal knowledge sheets.
— Duplicate copies in **two+** locations.

**Oral tradition:**
— Rhyme and song carry checklists better than prose — e.g. wound-care steps as a chant.


TEACHING WITHOUT SCREENS

**Mentorship:** expert + 1–2 learners; I do / you do / I fix.

**Class block:** theory to group — then **hands-on same day**.

**Demonstration:** silent run-through, then explain why.

**Verification:** week later — “show me” or “teach it back.” Mastery = they can train a third person.


MINIMUM “SCHOOL” FOR KIDS

Why:
— Future adults; occupied kids stress less; structure calms adults too.

Setup:
— Literate adult; ~1–2 h/day; table, paper, maybe a board.

Sample block:
— After breakfast: reading, writing, math, age-fit practical (garden, kitchen, first aid story).

Ages:
— ~5–7: letters, numbers, play-based.
— ~8–12: literacy, math, supervised chores with explanations.
— ~13+: real work tracks with teaching toward specialty.


COMMUNITY ARCHIVE

Record:
— Daily log: events, decisions.
— Roster + skills map.
— Area sketch: resources, hazards.
— Recipes, SOPs, rules updates.
— Short bios — who people were.

Storage:
— **Multiple** copies, separate buildings.
— Named curator.
— Evacuation load order: people, critical survival, then archive.

Value later:
— History for kids born in crisis; evidence if courts return; template for rebuilding order.
