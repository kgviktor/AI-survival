CHAPTER 9. COMMUNICATIONS AND INFORMATION
9.3 Signals and alerts: sound, light, smoke, visual signs

TWO MODES

Distress / rescue signals (be seen):
— Goal: strangers find and help you.
— Max visibility OK when hostile presence is unlikely.

Group / tactical signals (stay hidden):
— Goal: talk only to your people.
— Minimal signature; pre-briefed codes.

Using “loud” signals in a hostile area can be fatal — choose deliberately.


INTERNATIONAL DISTRESS PATTERNS

**Three of anything** (widely recognized group distress):
— Three gunshots; three whistle blasts; three fires in a row or triangle; three flashes; three blows on metal.

~1 minute pause between **groups** of three.

SOS (Morse): · · · — — — · · ·  
Sound/light: three short, three long, three short.

Ground signal “SOS”:
— Large letters from brush, rocks, snow — aim **~10 m+** for air search.

“Require assistance” (ICAO-style ground symbol):
— Large **V** visible from air.
— Aircraft acknowledgment: wing rock (context-dependent).


LIGHT SIGNALS

Signaling mirror:
— Sun flash visible for many km with practice.
— Aim through sight hole or past fingers at target.
— Improvise: foil, can lid, phone screen, glasses lens.
— **Do not flash toward road traffic or aircraft cockpits** at close range.

Flashlight:
— Morse SOS or three short flashes toward search aircraft / expected route.

Chem light (glow stick):
— Visible hundreds of meters; spin on cord for air ID.
— Runtime often ~8–12 h.

Laser pointer:
— Green beams trace far at night — **never aim at eyes or aircraft.**

Pyrotechnics:
— Marine/red parachute flares, pen flares — long sight range; **fire/burn hazard**; check expiry (~3–5 years typical).


SMOKE SIGNALS

Smoke works **by day**; flame/light at night.

Color tricks:
— **White smoke:** green boughs, leaves, moss, wet grass on hot coals.
— **Dark smoke:** rubber, plastics, oil — use sparingly (toxic, enviro).

Contrast: white on dark forest; dark plume on snow/sky.

Three fires in a triangle:
— ~25–30 m spacing — classic air distress layout.

On-demand puff:
— Keep strong base fire; throw wet fuel on for a sharp column when you hear aircraft.


SOUND SIGNALS

Whistle:
— Carries farther than voice; less fatigue.
— Metal or plastic ~120 dB+; **three blasts = distress.**
— Pealess designs (e.g. Fox 40 class) work wet.

Voice:
— “Help!” / local distress words; cup hands megaphone-style; shout on **exhale** bursts.

Metal percussion:
— Pots, pipes — carries in forest.

Vehicle horn:
— Three long — three short — three long (SOS rhythm) if context fits.


GROUP TACTICAL (PRE-BRIEFED)

Hand signs (examples):
— Hand up: stop.
— Flat hand forward: go / advance.
— Fist: danger / hold.
— Two fingers spread: split / flank.
— Hand at throat: high threat / chokepoint.
— Palm down: get down.
— Finger circle overhead: rally here.

Flashlight codes (dark):
— One flash: “OK / here.”
— Two: “Come to me.”
— Three: “Alert / danger.”
— Red filter: extreme caution.

Quiet audio:
— One click: stop.
— Two: go.
— Three: danger.
— Codeword for immediate exfil: e.g. “**word**” = leave now.

Notes and trail marks:
— Cache notes; tree blaze/chalk marks; stick/cairn arrows.


MARKING FOR YOUR OWN TEAM

Natural arrows:
— Rocks, sticks, cones pointing direction.
— Crossed sticks: “not this way / blocked.”
— Three stones: “we were here / note.”

Tree blazes (use only where legal and ethical):
— **V** notch: direction of travel.
— **X**: danger / do not proceed.
— Single vertical: “route / we passed.”

Flagging tape:
— Tie at turns; remove if stealth matters.

Dead-drop notes:
— Pre-agree hiding spots: “third rock at gate,” “behind loose board.”
