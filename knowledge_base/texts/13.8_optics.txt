CHAPTER 13. GEAR AND EQUIPMENT
13.8 Optics: binoculars, monoculars, sights

WHY OPTICS

**See before being seen** — route recon, perimeter check, threat ID, hunting, landmark spotting.

**Binoculars usually beat a rifle scope** for general survival tasks.


BINOCULARS

**8×42, 10×42** etc. — first number = magnification; second = objective lens mm.

**Power:** 6–8× steady hand, wide field; 10× common; 12×+ needs support.

**Objective:** 32 mm compact day; **42 mm** all-round twilight; 50 mm heavy but bright.

**Exit pupil** = objective ÷ power (e.g. 42÷8 = 5.25 mm) — match young eye ~7 mm night / ~3–5 mm day use.

**Porro** — wide, cheap good optics possible, bulky.

**Roof** — compact; quality costs; **FMC** (fully multi-coated) + phase-corrected prisms on good roofs.

**Nitrogen/argon fill** — internal fog resistance; **waterproof** for field.

**Minimum useful survival glass:** 8×42 waterproof FMC-filled.

**Brands orientation:** premium Euro glass; mid Vortex / Nikon Monarch class / Bushnell Legend; budget lines for “better than nothing.”


MONOCULAR

Half weight; one eye; tires faster; great **EDC pocket** backup.


SIGHTS (WEAPONS CONTEXT)

**Red dot** — fast close; needs battery on many units.

**Magnified scope** — distance precision.

**LPVO** 1–6× — hybrid.

**Passive prism / irons** — no battery — reliability plus.

**Care:** blow dust off before wipe; microfiber; lens caps on storage.

**Legal:** weapon-mounted optics follow local arms law.


NIGHT OBSERVATION

Gen 1 NV cheap but moon-dependent; Gen 2/3 costly.

**Thermal** — detects heat — powerful for perimeter **if** budget allows.

**Budget approach:** good handheld throw + **red** light discipline; some phone + IR illuminator setups (limitations, legal caution).

**Do not** shine visible lasers at aircraft.
