CHAPTER 2. WATER
2.3 Water treatment and disinfection: boiling, chlorine, iodine, filters

HAZARDS IN WATER

Water can carry three hazard types:

Biological (removed by boiling and chemicals):
— Bacteria: cholera, typhoid, dysentery, leptospirosis
— Viruses: hepatitis A, polio, norovirus
— Protozoa: Giardia, Cryptosporidium, amoebae

Chemical (not removed by boiling):
— Heavy metals: lead, mercury, arsenic
— Nitrates, pesticides, petroleum
— Industrial pollutants
Carbon filtration or distillation helps some chemicals.

Mechanical (particles, turbidity):
— Removed by settling and filtration
— Reduces chemical disinfectant effectiveness
— Clarify first, then disinfect


FULL TREATMENT PIPELINE

Complete treatment — three steps:
1. Settling (30–60 minutes)
2. Filtration
3. Disinfection (boiling or chemicals)

Skip steps only when the source is known to be clean.


STEP 1: SETTLING

Pour water into a container; do not disturb for 30–60 minutes. Large particles sink. Carefully decant the upper ~2/3 into another vessel without disturbing sediment. A pinch of alum can speed coagulation — particles clump and settle faster.


STEP 2: FILTRATION

DIY layered filter:
Cut the bottom off a plastic bottle; invert with neck down. Layers bottom to top (flow direction toward the neck):
— Layer 1 (at neck): cloth or cotton — catches large debris
— Layer 2: fine clean sand (5–10 cm) — mechanical filtration
— Layer 3: coarse gravel (5–10 cm) — pre-filter
— Layer 4 (top): activated carbon (10–15 cm) — reduces some chemicals, odors, color

Activated carbon:
Crush pharmacy charcoal tablets. Or powder from non-resinous wood charcoal. A 10–15 cm carbon bed helps noticeably. Carbon removes: some organics, part of heavy metals, odors, color. Carbon does NOT kill bacteria or viruses and will not fix high chemical loads.

Slow sand filter (biological):
A slow sand filter grows a biofilm of beneficial microbes that reduce pathogens. Takes ~2–3 weeks to “mature.” Flow ~0.1–0.3 L/h per dm² surface. Can be very effective against bacteria (up to ~99.9%).

Cloth stacking:
Several layers of dense cloth remove particles and some cysts. Not reliable against bacteria and viruses.


STEP 3: DISINFECTION

BOILING

Most reliable method. Kills bacteria, viruses, and most protozoa.

Boil time:
— Sea level: 1 minute of rolling boil is enough
— ~2000 m elevation: 3 minutes (lower boiling point)
— ~5000 m: 5 minutes
— When unsure: 3–5 minutes for margin

Giardia and Cryptosporidium:
Killed at ~70 °C; boiling guarantees it. Chlorine kills Giardia but is weak against Cryptosporidium at normal doses.

Note:
Boiling does not remove chemical contaminants. Some toxins (e.g., algal toxins) are heat-stable. Taste improves if you cover the pot while cooling.


CHLORINE (BLEACH)

Active ingredient: sodium hypochlorite.

Household bleach (~5–6% sodium hypochlorite):
Per 1 L clear water: 2 drops
Per 1 L cloudy water: 4 drops
Wait 30 minutes before drinking
Per 10 L: ~20 drops (~1 mL)

Important:
Use plain unscented bleach. If concentration is ~10%, halve the dose. Bleach loses strength with age — old bottles are weaker.

Chlorine tablets (e.g., Aquatabs):
Follow package directions. Often 1 tablet per liter, 30–60 minutes. More stable than liquid for storage.

Chlorine limits:
— Weak against Cryptosporidium
— Efficiency drops sharply in turbid water (particles consume chlorine)
— Odor can be reduced with activated carbon or by letting water stand open


IODINE

Tincture of iodine 5%:
Per 1 L clear water: 5 drops
Per 1 L cloudy water: 10 drops
Wait 30 minutes

Iodine tablets:
Follow directions; often 1–2 tablets per liter.

Iodine limits:
— Contraindicated for some thyroid conditions
— Weak against Cryptosporidium
— Unpleasant taste/smell — vitamin C after contact time can neutralize iodine and taste
— Not for continuous use >~3 weeks


ULTRAVIOLET

SODIS (solar disinfection):
Fill clear PET bottles (1.5–2 L). Place on a reflective metal surface. Sun exposure:
— Clear sky: 6 hours
— Cloudy: 2 days
Water must be fairly clear — filter turbid water first.

Effectiveness:
Kills many bacteria and viruses. Weaker against Cryptosporidium. Free, no chemicals.

UV lamps/sterilizers:
Fastest if you have power.


HYDROGEN PEROXIDE

3% solution (pharmacy):
~2–3 mL per liter (~40–60 drops)
Wait 30–60 minutes

Effective against many bacteria and viruses. Breaks down to water and oxygen — little aftertaste. Less stable in storage than tablets.


POTASSIUM PERMANGANATE

Weak solution (pale pink, not dark red):
Better for surface disinfection than as a primary drinking-water method. Overdose (dark pink/purple) burns mucosa — do not exceed.


COMPARISON TABLE

Method        | Bacteria | Viruses | Giardia | Crypto | Chemistry | Speed
Boiling       |   +++    |  +++    |   +++   |  +++   |   no      | ~5 min
Chlorine      |   +++    |  +++    |   ++    |   -    |   no      | 30 min
Iodine        |   +++    |  +++    |   ++    |   -    |   no      | 30 min
SODIS (sun)   |   +++    |  +++    |   ++    |   +    |   no      | 6 h
Carbon alone  |    -     |   -     |    -    |   -    |   +++     | ~1 h
Filter+carbon |   ++     |   +     |   ++    |   +    |   +++     | ~2 h
Peroxide      |   +++    |  ++     |    +    |   -    |   no      | 60 min

Recommendation: combine methods. Filtration + boiling = strong protection against most threats.
