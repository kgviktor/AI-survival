CHAPTER 14. VEHICLE: SERVICE AND REPAIR
14.7 Transmission and clutch: failure signs, temporary fixes

TRANSMISSION TYPES

**Manual (MT):**
— Driver selects gears.
— Simpler, often more field-repairable, less parasitic loss.
— **Preferred** for many crisis/off-road roles.

**Automatic (AT):**
— Torque converter or multi-clutch auto — uses **ATF**.
— Complex internal repair; **overheats fast** when slipping in mud/sand.

**CVT:**
— Poor tolerance for sustained slip and heavy towing — **weakest** for hard crisis use.

**4×4 / AWD:**
— **Full-time** — always driving multiple axles (e.g. some Subaru, Audi quattro).
— **Part-time** — driver engages 4H/4L (common on trucks, simple 4×4s).
— Use **4H/4L only on low-traction** — prolonged dry-pavement 4×4 can **wind up** driveline.


CLUTCH (MT)

Layout: friction disc → pressure plate → flywheel. Pedal out = engine coupled; pedal in = disconnected.

**Worn disc:**
— **Slip** — revs rise, speed doesn’t under load.
— Burnt smell on hills.
— “Grabs high” on pedal travel.

**Throw-out bearing:**
— Noise **only when pedal pressed** — often squeal/grind.

**Fork / cable:**
— Stiff pedal; no disengagement.

**Free play (cable/mechanical):**
— Often **~20–30 mm** at pedal — spec in manual. Too little = slip; too much = hard shifts.

**Hydraulic clutch:**
— Shared or separate reservoir; **spongy pedal** = air — bleed like brakes.

**Last-resort “creep start”** if clutch won’t disengage:
— Engine off, **1st** engaged, starter cranks vehicle forward — needs strong starter/battery — **hard on geartrain** — emergency only.


MANUAL GEARBOX — SYMPTOMS

**Hard to engage:**
— Air in hydraulic clutch.
— Worn synchronizers (grind **2nd/3rd** common).
— Shift linkage bent or loose.

**Pops out of gear:**
— Worn dogs/syncros; loose linkage bolts.

**Noise:**
— Neutral growl — input shaft / bearings.
— One gear whines — that gear set.
— Howl moving — **low oil**.


**MT FLUID:**
— Side plug level check; change **~60–100k km** or manual.
— Spec often **GL-4 75W-90** (many MT) — **not** GL-5 in some synchronized boxes — **verify**.
— Top up to hole edge.


AUTOMATIC — SYMPTOMS

— Harsh shifts — dirty/old fluid, worn clutches.
— Missing gear — solenoid / clutch pack.
— **Slip** — revs rise, no acceleration — friction damage.
— **Dark burnt-smelling fluid** — overheated — service urgently.
— Noise — bearings or converter.

**ATF check** (if dipstick exists): warm, idling in **P**, correct mark.

Fresh fluid often clear red; **black/brown burnt** = trouble.

Change interval **~60k km** or manual; some “sealed” units still need service at shops.

**AT overheat:**
— Mud/sand wheelspin heats fluid in minutes.
— Lamp, smell, slip — **stop 20–30 min**; check cooler lines not blocked.

**Field reality:** internal AT failure → usually **tow** — not roadside rebuild.


PART-TIME 4×4 AND TRANSFER CASE

**4H** — slippery roads, dirt, snow — speed limits per manual (e.g. up to **~80 km/h** on slip).

**4L** — crawl, steep pulls — **low speed** — engage **stopped** or per manual.

**2H** — dry road.

Don’t cruise long on **dry asphalt in locked 4H** — binding/heat.

**Transfer case oil** — seasonal level check; change **~60–100k km**.


CV JOINTS (AXLES)

Transmit torque while steering (FWD / many 4×4 fronts).

**Click** on full-lock acceleration — worn CV.

**Torn boot** → grease out, dirt in → joint dies fast — **boot kit early**.


PROPSHAFT (CARDAN)

RWD / 4×4 rear shaft with universal joints.

**Speed-related vibration** — imbalance or **worn U-joint**.

**Clunk on takeoff** — worn splines / U-joint.

Lift vehicle; **no play** in U-joints.

**Field:** U-joint replacement possible with basic tools; if shaft fails on **FWD-based** vehicle, sometimes remove shaft and **front-drive limp** (not all vehicles — **check manual**).
