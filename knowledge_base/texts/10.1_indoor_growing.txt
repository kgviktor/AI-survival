CHAPTER 10. FOOD PRODUCTION
10.1 Growing indoors: windowsill and balcony

WHY GROW INSIDE

In a long crisis (weeks to months) stored food runs out. Apartment growing:
— Fresh greens, vitamins, some calories.
— No yard required.
— Relatively discreet.
— Works with minimal inputs.

Realistic expectations:
— You cannot fully feed a family from one windowsill alone.
— Sprouts, microgreens, small roots — very doable.
— Psychological boost: living food improves morale.


LIGHT IS THE MAIN LIMIT

Rough daily needs:
— Leaf herbs (parsley, dill, basil, spinach): ~4–6 h direct sun **or** ~12–16 h good artificial.
— Tomatoes, peppers: ~6–8 h direct sun — poor on a sill without supplemental light.
— Sprouts: almost no light needed.
— Microgreens: ~2–4 h.

Best windows:
— South / southwest: most sun.
— High latitudes in winter: often too dark without grow lights.
— Supplement: “full spectrum” LED or neutral white ~5000–6500 K.
  — ~15–25 cm above plants.
  — ~10–30 W for a small shelf.
  — If power is scarce: run lights ~4–6 h from battery/solar.

Mirrors and foil:
— Reflective surfaces around the window add useful photons.
— Foil on side walls can add up to ~30% effective light.


WHAT YOU CAN ACTUALLY GROW

Sprouts (fastest — ~3–7 days, little or no soil):
— Wheat, lentils, mung, chickpeas, peas, soy, radish, sunflower.
— Rich in vitamins (C, B-group, E) and enzymes.
— Soak seeds ~8–12 h → rinse ~2×/day in a jar with mesh lid.
— By day ~3 often ready to eat.
— Yield ~3× seed volume by mass.

Microgreens (~7–14 days):
— Sunflower, mustard, radish, broccoli, pea, wheat, sesame.
— Sow densely in shallow trays (~2–3 cm medium).
— Snip at ~5–8 cm.
— Low calories but nutrient density can be many times mature leaves.

Leaf crops (~2–4 weeks to first pick):
— Leaf lettuce: fastest, easy. Pick outer leaves → cut-and-come-again.
— Spinach: cool-tolerant; iron and vitamins.
— Dill, parsley: slower (~4–6 weeks) but valuable.
— Green onion: bulbs in a glass of water → shoots in ~7–10 days — easiest trick.
— Basil, mint, lemon balm: pots; harvest gradually.

Root crops (~2–3+ months):
— Radish: fastest root (~20–30 days). Pot depth ~15 cm.
— Carrot: ~70–90 days; deep pot ~30+ cm.
— Potato: bag or ~20 L bucket; ~70–90 days.
— Beet: ~60–70 days.

Fruiting (~3+ months, pollination needed):
— Cherry tomatoes: compact types; ~60–80 days to fruit.
— Peppers: productive over a long season.
— Cucumbers: quick (~40–50 days); trellis; hand-pollinate indoors.


CONTAINERS AND MEDIUM

Containers:
— Pots, totes, buckets, crates, milk cartons, Tetra Paks — anything that holds soil.
— **Drainage holes mandatory** — else root rot.
— Leaf crops: ~0.5–1 L soil per plant.
— Tomatoes/peppers: minimum ~5–10 L.
— Potato: ~15–20 L.

Growing medium:
— Bagged potting mix: ideal.
— Garden soil: usable but may import pests/disease.
— DIY: compost (~50%) + perlite or sand (~30%) + garden soil (~20%).
— Coco coir: light, holds water, neutral, durable.
— Soilless / hydro: see `10.3_hydroponics.txt`.

Drainage:
— ~2–3 cm layer of gravel, clay pebbles, broken pots at bottom.
— No drainage → standing water → rot.


WATERING AND FEEDING

Water:
— Check top ~2–3 cm — if dry, water.
— Greens: often every 1–2 days.
— Roots, tomatoes: ~every 2–3 days.
— Overwatering hurts more than slight underwatering for many plants.
— Room-temperature water; if chlorinated, let stand ~24 h.

Feeding after ~3–4 weeks (nutrients deplete):
— Compost tea: compost + water, steep ~24 h, dilute, water in.
— Wood ash leachate: potassium and phosphorus; alkaline — use sparingly.
— Crushed eggshell: calcium.
— Banana peel soak: potassium.
— Yeast feed: ~1 g yeast + ~1 tsp sugar per liter — mild growth boost.


BALCONY

Extra benefits:
— More light (outdoor or open railing).
— More area.
— Air movement — fewer fungal issues.

What to add vs windowsill only:
— Tomatoes, cucumbers, zucchini, beans.
— Perimeter boxes for greens and radish.
— Vertical pockets: burlap, PET bottles on walls.

Winter glazed balcony (~5–15 °C):
— Green onion, spinach, lettuce — slow but alive.
— Garlic cloves for greens → shoots in ~2 weeks.
— Frost threat: move tubs inside.

Vertical PET towers:
— Cut ~1.5–2 L bottles; side holes for plants; fill with medium; hang or wall-mount.
— Good for greens, strawberry, radish.


SEED BANK

Strategic stock:
— Buy varieties you will actually grow.
— Store in airtight bag + desiccant; cool, dark.
— Viability: many seeds ~2–5 years (carrot, onion shorter; tomato, cucumber, pepper longer).

Good staples:
— Onion (fast greens).
— Radish.
— Leaf lettuce, spinach.
— Dill, parsley.
— Cherry tomato.
— Peas (food + sprouts).
— Lentils, mung (sprouts).
— Sunflower (sprouts + oil crop).
— Beans (dry storage + fast vine).

Saving your own:
— Let fruit fully ripen on plant.
— Clean seeds; dry ~1–2 weeks in shade; paper envelope + label.
— Tomatoes: ferment seeds in water ~3–4 days to remove gel → rinse → dry.
