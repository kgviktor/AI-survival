CHAPTER 10. FOOD PRODUCTION
10.2 Rooftop and courtyard gardening

URBAN SPACE POTENTIAL

Cities hide huge idle area:
— Flat roofs on blocks (100–1000+ m²).
— Courtyards and internal yards.
— Vacant lots.
— Lawns and green strips.
— Balconies and loggias.

Rough productivity (intensive beds):
— ~10 m² can yield ~50–100 kg vegetables per season (climate and skill dependent).
— ~100 m² can materially help feed a group of ~5–10 people in a long crisis (3+ months).


ROOFTOP — LOAD FIRST

**Soil is heavy:** ~1 m² bed 20 cm deep ≈ 200–300 kg wet mass.

— Reinforced concrete flat roofs on older panel buildings often rate ~150–500 kg/m² design load — **verify with an engineer if unsure**.
— Do not concentrate load on edges/corners without knowledge.
— Light metal or unknown wood decks: **do not use** without professional check.

Light substrates:
— Coco coir ~0.15 kg/L vs mineral soil ~1 kg/L.
— Perlite ~0.1 kg/L.
— 50/50 coco + perlite: excellent light bed.
— Hydroponics: lightest wet system.

Rooftop bed build:
— Containers: totes, buckets, builder bags.
— Or framed boxes from pallets/lumber.
— **Waterproof membrane** under soil — protect the roof membrane.
— Drain: holes → pipe to drain or storage, not ponding on waterproofing.

Water on roof:
— Without mains: barrels from downspouts.
— Gutter → cistern.
— Gravity drip from tank.
— Buckets by hand.

Wind:
— Above ~5 floors: constant wind dries soil and breaks stems.
— Wind mesh (shade cloth) cuts wind but passes light.
— Compact/dwarf cultivars.
— Mulch surface to cut evaporation.

Access and safety:
— Confirm legal access to roof; **unprotected edges = fatal fall risk**.
— Rail or barrier around work zone.


COURTYARD GARDEN

Site selection:
— ≥6 h sun if possible.
— Wind break (fence, building).
— Water access.
— Not under dense trees (shade + root competition).

Urban soil contamination:
— Heavy metals, fuels common near roads, old industrial sites, parking.
— Fix: **raised beds** with imported clean soil, or containers.
— Geotextile under clean fill separates from suspect native soil.
— Minimum clean depth ~20–25 cm most crops; ~30–40 cm for roots.

Raised bed frames:
— Boards, slate, brick, blocks — whatever is safe and available.
— Width ~80–120 cm (reach both sides without stepping in).
— Height ~20–40 cm.
— Untreated wood: char or used motor oil (traditional) vs rot — or use plastic/composite.

Mulch:
— ~5–10 cm organic layer: chips, grass, leaves, straw, cardboard.
— Cuts water loss (~2–3× fewer waterings).
— Suppresses weeds.
— Feeds soil as it breaks down.


CROP PLANNING

Rotation (if permanent plot):
— Do not repeat same family in same spot every year.
— Example loop: greens → roots → legumes → nightshades → greens.
— Beans and peas fix nitrogen — feed the next crop.

Companion planting:
— “Three Sisters”: corn + climbing beans + squash — corn trellis, beans fix N, squash mulches soil.
— Basil near tomato: some pest confusion; aroma.
— Marigolds on edges: nematode/suppress some pests.

Sowing calendar:
— **Adjust to your frost dates and latitude.** Temperate Northern example:
— Early spring: cold-hardy (carrot, radish, onion, pea, spinach).
— After last frost: warm crops (tomato, cucumber, squash, beans).
— Mid-summer: second sowing of greens and radish.
— Early fall: last harvests; prep for winter.

Season extension:
— Floating row cover (fleece): a few degrees frost protection.
— Hoop + plastic tunnel: +1–2 months shoulder season.
— Under cover in cool season: scallions, spinach, chard, mache/corn salad.


WATER

Rain barrels:
— ~200–1000 L under downspouts.
— First flush from roof is dirty — divert it.
— ~200 L ≈ ~2–3 days irrigation for a small intensive bed (climate dependent).

Saving water:
— Drip from barrel along row.
— Water morning or evening, not midday heat.
— Mulch.
— Buried bottle with pinhole — slow olla-style trickle.


PEST CONTROL (LOW-TECH)

Physical:
— Bird netting (especially roofs).
— Fine mesh against carrot fly etc.
— Hand-pick caterpillars.

Organic sprays / dusts:
— Wood-ash spray: aphids, whitefly (test on one leaf first).
— Garlic/onion soak: aphids, mites.
— Dilute soap water: soft-bodied insects.
— Ash + tobacco dust: slugs (toxicity caution near food).

Pollinators:
— Flowering herbs (marigold, dill, basil, parsley in bloom) attract bees.
— On isolated roofs, pollinators may be scarce — **hand-pollinate** with cotton swab or small brush.
