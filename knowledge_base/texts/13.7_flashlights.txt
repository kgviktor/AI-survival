CHAPTER 13. GEAR AND EQUIPMENT
13.7 Flashlights and headlamps: lumens, IPX, power

WHY LIGHT STACKS MATTER

Darkness breaks security, travel, cooking, and medical work. **Multiple independent lights** are mandatory.


FORM FACTORS

**Headlamp** — hands free; repair, medicine, chores.

**Handheld tactical** — more throw than many headlamps; signaling; one hand occupied.

**Lantern** — 360° area light for room or tent.

**Keychain light** — always-on backup — not primary.


LUMENS (ROUGH GUIDE)

— ~20–50 lm — close tasks, map.
— ~100–200 lm — walking dark trail.
— ~300–500 lm — confident outdoor use.
— ~500–1000+ lm — search, long throw — **heat and drain** on turbo; check **sustained** ANSI FL1 figures, not ad peak forever.

**Throw** — headlamp ~50–100 m enough for camp; handheld can reach hundreds of meters with right reflector.


RUNTIME

Turbo often **minutes**; mid modes ~10–30+ h per battery set. Plan on **medium** for real work.


IPX RATING

— IPX4 — splashes.
— IPX6 — strong jets.
— IPX7 — 1 m / 30 min typical claim.
— **Survival minimum:** IPX6+ preferred; IPX7 if budget allows.

**ANSI FL1** — standardized lumen, runtime, drop, water claims — compare honestly.


BATTERIES

**AA / AAA** — everywhere; alkaline or **lithium primary** for cold and shelf life.

**CR123A** — compact 3 V; cold tolerant; less common.

**18650 / 21700 Li-ion** — high performance; need charger and **safe handling**; great with solar USB.

**Strategy:** one AA light + spare alkalines/lithium **and** one 18650 USB-charge light if you have solar/car path.

**Dual-fuel** radios/lights reduce single-point failure.


MODES

Low / med / high; **red** preserves night vision for watch; **SOS** blink if you need signaling; strobe — discipline use (friendly blinding).


SURVIVAL SET (PER PERSON MINIMUM)

— Headlamp, AA or dual-fuel, weather resistant.
— Handheld with decent throw.
— Keychain backup.
— **Spare cells** cached dry.

**Group add:** area lantern; chem light sticks (no battery, marking, low signature option).
